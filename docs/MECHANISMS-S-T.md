# Mechanisms S–T

## Serotonin

Mood stability, contentment, and social behavior. Not simply "the happiness chemical" — regulates aggression, appetite, and gut motility. 90%+ produced in the gut. Precursor: dietary tryptophan.

## Testosterone

Primary male anabolic hormone. Present in both sexes. Promotes muscle protein synthesis, libido, energy, assertiveness, and competitive drive. Regulated by sleep, exercise intensity, and body fat percentage.

## Thyroid Hormones (T3, T4)

Regulate metabolic rate. Affect body temperature, energy, cognition, and weight. Thyroid dysfunction (hypo/hyper) has broad whole-body effects. Iodine deficiency is the most common global cause of hypothyroidism.
