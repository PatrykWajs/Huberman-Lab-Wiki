# Episode 323: Essentials - Science of Mindsets for Health & Performance - Dr. Alia Crum

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

This Essentials episode features highlights from Andrew Huberman's conversation with Dr. Alia Crum, professor of psychology at Stanford and director of the Stanford Mind & Body Lab. Dr. Crum presents research demonstrating that mindsets — core beliefs and assumptions about domains like stress, food, and exercise — don't just shape motivation and behavior; they directly alter physiology. Through landmark studies including the milkshake experiment and the hotel housekeeper study, she shows that what we believe about what we're doing changes how our bodies actually respond, and presents a practical three-step framework for adopting a stress-is-enhancing mindset.

## Key Takeaways

- **Mindsets are core beliefs that shape physiology, not just motivation:** Dr. Crum defines mindsets as core beliefs about a domain that orient expectations, explanations, and goals. They act as a default operating system — programming the brain's subconscious response before conscious thought engages. Examples span stress, food, exercise, illness, and side effects. [Mindset definition](transcript.md#mindset-core-beliefs-examples-of-mindset)

- **The milkshake study: believing you're eating indulgently changes your ghrelin response:** Participants who believed they drank a 620-calorie indulgent shake showed a 3x greater drop in ghrelin (the hunger hormone) than when they believed the same shake was a sensible diet drink. The shake was identical in both conditions (~300 calories). Their bodies responded as if they had consumed more food. [Milkshake study](transcript.md#mindset-shapes-bodys-response-placebo-effects-tool-mindset-for-weight-loss)

- **Thinking "indulgent" is metabolically superior to thinking "sensible":** Crum expected a "healthy" mindset to be advantageous, but the opposite held — a deprivation mindset left participants physiologically hungry with a blunted ghrelin drop, suggesting slower satiety signaling and potentially slower metabolism. Framing meals as satisfying and sufficient produces a more adaptive physiological response. [Milkshake study](transcript.md#mindset-shapes-bodys-response-placebo-effects-tool-mindset-for-weight-loss)

- **Diet tribe benefits may be partly mindset-driven:** Whether plant-based, carnivore, or keto, health outcomes are a combined product of what you actually eat and what you believe about that diet. Skepticism about a diet, or guilt from failing to follow one, may blunt or negate its benefits through the associated stress and anxiety. [Different diets](transcript.md#different-diets-mindset)

- **Nocebo effect: negative beliefs produce real negative physiological outcomes:** The nocebo effect — the placebo's "ugly stepsister" — is when negative expectations cause genuine harm. Patients told about side effects experience them at significantly higher rates. Research on psychogenic fever shows that merely thinking you are sick can raise body temperature 1–3°F. [Nocebo effect](transcript.md#nocebo-effect)

- **The hotel housekeeper study: mindset about exercise produces measurable physical benefits without behavior change:** Hotel housekeepers already exceeded Surgeon General exercise guidelines but believed they were sedentary. When told their work constituted good exercise, they lost weight and reduced systolic blood pressure by ~10 points in four weeks — with no change in behavior. Public health framing that emphasizes deficiency creates a harmful mindset. [Hotel housekeeper study](transcript.md#exercise-mindsets-weight-loss-health-benefits)

- **Stress is a paradox — not simply debilitating:** The scientific literature shows stress can narrow focus, boost attention, speed information processing, activate anabolic hormones through physiological toughening, and lead to post-traumatic growth. The public narrative of "stress is always bad" is an oversimplification that itself worsens outcomes. [Stress is enhancing](transcript.md#stress-tool-stress-is-enhancing-mindset-mental-physical-benefits)

- **A stress-is-enhancing mindset reduces symptoms and improves performance:** In a UBS study during the 2008 financial crisis, employees who watched 9 minutes of "stress-is-enhancing" videos reported fewer physical symptoms (backaches, insomnia, muscle tension, racing heart) and better work performance versus those who watched "stress-is-debilitating" videos. The debilitating videos did not make things worse — that message was already the cultural baseline. [UBS study](transcript.md#stress-tool-stress-is-enhancing-mindset-mental-physical-benefits)

- **Stress-is-enhancing mindset changes the hormonal stress response:** People induced to adopt a more enhancing stress mindset show more moderate cortisol responses and higher DHEA levels under stress. This aligns with Duncan French's finding that extreme adrenaline spikes can raise testosterone — epinephrine is biochemically derived from dopamine, and these anabolic hormone pathways interact in the pituitary and hypothalamus. [Hormones and mindset](transcript.md#mindset-stress-motivation-physiology-hormones)

- **Mindsets are a portal between conscious and subconscious processes:** Mindsets operate as background default programs below conscious awareness. "Stress = bad" runs automatically and triggers protective rather than growth-promoting physiological cascades. Surfacing the mindset — making it conscious — is the first step to reprogramming it. [Mindsets as portals](transcript.md#mindsets-as-portals-to-unconscious-tool-stress-mindset-awareness)

- **Stress only arises when something matters to you:** Stress is defined as experiencing or anticipating adversity in goal-related efforts — we don't stress about things we don't care about. Recognizing this shifts the question from "how do I get rid of stress?" to "how do I leverage it toward what I care about?" [Leverage stress](transcript.md#leverage-stress-tool-adopt-a-stress-is-enhancing-mindset)

- **Athletic experience confirms mindset determines performance independent of physical readiness:** Crum, a former gymnast, notes that athletes know from direct experience that mental state determines performance moment to moment. Visualization is prerequisite: if you can't see something in your mind, you cannot execute it on the beam. [Athletics](transcript.md#mindsets-matter-athletics)

## Tools & Protocols

### Mindset for Weight Loss / Eating

- **What:** Frame meals as satisfying and sufficient rather than restrictive or "sensible."
- **How:** Think of food as indulgent and enough — even when it is not high-calorie. Avoid guilt or anxiety around eating choices, as that stress may negate dietary benefits.
- **When:** At every meal, especially when dieting or monitoring intake.
- **Ref:** [Milkshake study](transcript.md#mindset-shapes-bodys-response-placebo-effects-tool-mindset-for-weight-loss)

### Stress Mindset Awareness

- **What:** Surface your current implicit belief about stress and examine it consciously.
- **How:** Ask yourself: "What do I actually believe about the nature of stress? Do I believe it is debilitating, or can it enhance me?" Most people hold an implicit "stress is bad" belief installed by culture and media — naming it is step one to reprogramming it.
- **When:** During any stressful period, or as a one-time reflective exercise.
- **Ref:** [Mindsets as portals](transcript.md#mindsets-as-portals-to-unconscious-tool-stress-mindset-awareness)

### Adopt a Stress-Is-Enhancing Mindset (Three-Step Method)

- **What:** Dr. Crum's three-step framework for leveraging stress rather than fighting it.
- **How:** (1) **Acknowledge** — Recognize and name that you are stressed. Do not suppress or deny it. (2) **Welcome** — Accept it, because stress signals something you care about; it is goal-connected energy. (3) **Utilize** — Direct the energy of the stress response toward the goal you care about. Ask "How can I use this?" rather than "How do I get rid of this?"
- **When:** In real time when experiencing stress; a full toolkit is also available at Stanford SPARK.
- **Ref:** [Leverage stress](transcript.md#leverage-stress-tool-adopt-a-stress-is-enhancing-mindset)

### Reframe Physical Activity as Exercise

- **What:** Actively classify physical labor and active work as exercise to capture its full physiological benefit.
- **How:** Remind yourself that any physical activity — walking, cleaning, physical job duties — counts as exercise and produces measurable health benefits. The mindset change is not merely motivational; it produces physiological change.
- **When:** During physically demanding work or daily activity not habitually labeled as "exercise."
- **Ref:** [Hotel housekeeper study](transcript.md#exercise-mindsets-weight-loss-health-benefits)
