# Episode 311: Essentials - Psychedelics for Treating Mental Disorders - Dr. Matthew Johnson

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

In this Huberman Lab Essentials episode, Andrew Huberman speaks with Dr. Matthew Johnson, a senior researcher at the Center of Excellence for Psilocybin Research and Treatment at Sheppard Pratt. They explore the science and therapeutic potential of psilocybin, MDMA, and LSD — covering how psychedelics profoundly alter perception and self-identity, why "letting go" is the core mechanism of therapeutic benefit, and the evidence (and lack thereof) for microdosing. Johnson also discusses emerging research into whether psilocybin can repair neurological damage from repetitive head injuries.

## Key Takeaways

- **Psychedelics span multiple pharmacological classes** — classic 5-HT2A agonists (psilocybin, LSD, DMT, mescaline — with two structural subtypes: tryptamines and phenethylamines), NMDA antagonists (ketamine, PCP, dextromethorphan), and entactogens/empathogens (MDMA, meaning "touching within" and "affording empathy") — united only by their ability to profoundly alter one's sense of reality and self. [Different Classes of Psychedelics](transcript.md#000144-different-classes-of-psychedelics)
- **Classic psychedelics act on the serotonin 2A receptor** as agonists or partial agonists; exactly why this produces radical alterations in consciousness remains an active research question — serotonin itself is not psychedelic, so post-receptor signaling pathways beyond simple binding must account for the effect. [LSD, Psilocybin & Serotonin](transcript.md#000618-lsd-psilocybin--serotonin)
- **Psychedelics violate predictive models of reality** — Johnson describes a volunteer who appeared to try to dive through a painting, genuinely believing she could pass through it; this dis-habituation of deeply held perceptual models is a core mechanism, not a side effect. [Psychedelics & Altering Models](transcript.md#000433-psychedelics--altering-models)
- **Clinical trials involve layered screening and preparation** — multi-day structured psychiatric interviews (full DSM coverage), cardiovascular evaluation, and multiple sessions of guide rapport-building and didactic preparation before any psychedelic is administered; the explicit goal is no surprises. [Psychedelic Clinical Trials](transcript.md#000844-psychedelic-clinical-trials)
- **The session environment is deliberately unstructured** — pure synthetic psilocybin (20–30 mg), a couch, eye mask, curated music, and full emotional permission; cognitive tasks and fMRI are avoided because they produce less meaningful experiences. Any emotional response — including uncontrolled crying — is explicitly welcome. [Therapy, Trust, Psychedelics](transcript.md#001228-therapy-trust-psychedelics)
- **The core mechanism of lasting benefit is persisting changes in self-representation** — a fundamental shift in how a person defines their identity and agency, collapsing rigid self-models ("I'm a smoker," "I'm a depressed person who fails") into moments of profound felt agency that Johnson calls "duh experiences." A cancer patient example: "I'm causing most of my own suffering — I can choose to go outside, play with my grandkids." [Letting Go & Psychedelic Experience, Self-Representation, Lasting Changes](transcript.md#001536-letting-go--psychedelic-experience-self-representation-lasting-changes)
- **Ego dissolution is the gateway to both transcendence and terror** — complete loss of the sense of being a separate self can be handled two ways: surrender leads toward mystical unity (data-linked to positive long-term outcomes); resistance leads toward a bad trip. MDMA's lower risk of this reality-shattering threshold may make it better suited to trauma treatment for a wider population. [MDMA, Dopamine & Serotonin; Bad Trips & Transcendental Experience](transcript.md#002119-mdma-dopamine--serotonin-bad-trips--transcendental-experience)
- **Two major safety contraindications:** (1) psychotic disorders (schizophrenia) and the manic phase of bipolar disorder — not ordinary depression or anxiety — and (2) bad trips, which occur in roughly one-third of people at 30 mg psilocybin even in optimal clinical settings. [Dangers of Psychedelics](transcript.md#002443-dangers-of-psychedelics)
- **Microdosing has no peer-reviewed support for its primary claims** — no studies demonstrate improved cognition, creativity, or sustained mood enhancement; the best-documented finding is minor impairment of time estimation. The specific cycling protocols advocated by aficionados (e.g., Stamets' one-day-on / two-to-three-days-off formula, with benefits emerging on the off days after several weeks) have never been formally tested. Johnson's best bet for any real effect is antidepressant action analogous to SSRIs, grounded in the decades-long precedent of serotonin augmentation (tricyclics, MAOIs) reducing depressive symptoms. [Microdosing Psychedelics, Antidepressant](transcript.md#002704-microdosing-psychedelics-antidepressant)
- **Heroic-dose psilocybin (20–30 mg, 1–3 sessions) produces the most compelling results** — lasting improvements in depression and addiction persisting months to over a year from just one to three administrations; Johnson explicitly calls these "big effects" and says all the most interesting things about psychedelics are in the heroic-dose range. [Microdosing Psychedelics, Antidepressant](transcript.md#002704-microdosing-psychedelics-antidepressant)
- **Preliminary data suggests psychedelics may repair neurological damage** — rodent studies (Olson and Roth labs) demonstrate psychedelic-induced neuroplasticity; human anecdotes from athletes with repetitive head impacts report improved cognition and memory. Johnson is planning trials with retired MMA athletes, measuring depression, cognitive function, and gray matter changes via MRI. [Head Injuries, Psychedelics, Depression](transcript.md#003120-head-injuries-psychedelics-depression)

## Tools & Protocols

### Psilocybin-Assisted Therapy (Clinical Model)
- **What:** Structured therapeutic use of pure synthetic psilocybin in a supervised clinical setting.
- **How:** Multi-day psychiatric (full DSM structured interviews) and cardiovascular screening; multiple sessions of guide rapport-building and didactic preparation covering the full range of possible experiences (including the most beautiful and most terrifying); single session at 20–30 mg psilocybin on a couch with eye mask and curated music; no cognitive tasks or fMRI; full emotional permission — crying, fear, and silence all equally welcome; post-session integration.
- **When:** For treatment-resistant depression, addiction (especially tobacco cessation), cancer-related anxiety/depression, and PTSD (MDMA variant).
- **Ref:** [Therapy, Trust, Psychedelics](transcript.md#001228-therapy-trust-psychedelics)

### "Letting Go" as Core Therapeutic Mechanism
- **What:** Actively surrendering to the psychedelic experience rather than attempting to control or resist it.
- **How:** Guides instruct participants that all emotional responses are valid and welcome; when challenging content arises, lean into it rather than push it away; the therapeutic target is dis-habituation of deeply held self-models; at the onset of ego-dissolution states in particular, surrendering rather than resisting is the critical behavioral choice.
- **When:** During high-dose psilocybin or classic psychedelic sessions; especially important when reality-shattering or self-dissolution begins.
- **Ref:** [Letting Go & Psychedelic Experience, Self-Representation, Lasting Changes](transcript.md#001536-letting-go--psychedelic-experience-self-representation-lasting-changes)

### Microdosing (Current Evidence)
- **What:** Sub-perceptual doses of psilocybin claimed to enhance focus, mood, or creativity without a full psychedelic experience.
- **How:** Protocols like Paul Stamets' stack (dose one day, off two–three days, repeat for several weeks — with claimed benefits emerging on off days) have not been formally studied in any clinical trial matching that regimen. Published studies to date have used simpler, non-matched designs.
- **When:** Not currently supported by peer-reviewed evidence for cognitive or mood enhancement; minor time-perception impairment has been observed; Johnson considers antidepressant effects the most biologically plausible real benefit if any exists, given the decades-long precedent of serotonin augmentation treating depression.
- **Ref:** [Microdosing Psychedelics, Antidepressant](transcript.md#002704-microdosing-psychedelics-antidepressant)

### Cardiovascular Safety Protocol (Clinical Session)
- **What:** Real-time blood pressure and pulse monitoring during psilocybin sessions with a physician on standby.
- **How:** If blood pressure exceeds a set threshold, the on-site physician administers sublingual nitroglycerin to lower it without disrupting the psychedelic experience.
- **When:** During any high-dose psilocybin session in a clinical trial context.
- **Ref:** [Therapy, Trust, Psychedelics](transcript.md#001228-therapy-trust-psychedelics)
