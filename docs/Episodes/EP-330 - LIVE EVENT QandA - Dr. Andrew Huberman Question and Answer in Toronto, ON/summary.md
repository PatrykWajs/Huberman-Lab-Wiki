# Episode 330: LIVE EVENT Q&A — Dr. Andrew Huberman Question & Answer in Toronto, ON

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

This episode is the recorded Q&A session from the Huberman Lab live event at Meridian Hall in Toronto, Ontario, part of the lecture series called "The Brain Body Contract." Huberman answers audience questions spanning mental health and the unconscious mind, emotional resilience under stress, how the brain generates inspiration, seasonal depression biology, neuroplasticity mechanisms, movement protocols for desk workers, and his personal morning perceptual exercise he calls "space time bridging." Sponsors of the live event were AG1 and Eight Sleep.

## Key Takeaways

- **Why the Dr. Paul Conti series was made:** Conti is a Stanford- and Harvard-trained psychiatrist with deep insight into the unconscious mind. The series was motivated by the absence of a "mental fitness" equivalent to the Andy Galpin physical fitness series. All tools and worksheets are zero-cost and require no therapist. [00:02:41](transcript.md#what-motivated-you-to-do-the-guest-series-with-dr-paul-conti)

- **The unconscious mind as the supercomputer:** Conti argues the unconscious — not the forebrain — is the dominant driver of behavior. Accessing it happens best in liminal states between waking and sleep, through dreams, and through mirror work. Symbols in dreams are often gender- and species-flipped versions of real-life conflicts. [00:02:41](transcript.md#what-motivated-you-to-do-the-guest-series-with-dr-paul-conti)

- **The three drives that predict relationships:** According to Conti, the aggressive drive, pleasure drive, and generative drive predict relational compatibility far better than personality type or shared interests. [00:02:41](transcript.md#what-motivated-you-to-do-the-guest-series-with-dr-paul-conti)

- **Emotional resilience is built outside triggering moments:** There is little one can do in real time during a trigger except perhaps a physiological sigh. Resilience comes from consistent self-care, good sleep, and morning routines that keep the nervous system stable. Getting comfortable with adrenaline circulating in your system is key. [00:08:07](transcript.md#enhancing-emotional-resilience-in-triggering-situations-protocols-and-best-practices)

- **No input, no output — Strummer's Law for inspiration:** Joe Strummer's principle applies to creativity. The best ideas come when you are not actively seeking them. Huberman walks or hikes at least once a week without earphones to achieve wordlessness and allow ideas to geyser up from the unconscious. [00:12:46](transcript.md#understanding-and-fostering-sudden-inspiration-in-the-brain)

- **Awe vs. delight as the signature of inspiration:** Awe overwhelms attention but requires no action. Delight links an external experience to your internal narrative and creates an impulse to do something. Delight is what inspiration actually feels like. [00:12:46](transcript.md#understanding-and-fostering-sudden-inspiration-in-the-brain)

- **Seasonal depression is about melatonin signal rate of change, not absolute duration:** The brain compares melatonin duration today against the previous day. Depression sets in as the signal lengthens day over day through fall. The fix is to extend bright morning light exposure slightly as days shorten — a 900 lux drawing tablet works as well as expensive SAD lamps. [00:16:36](transcript.md#how-can-canadians-fight-the-season-depression)

- **Neuroplasticity works through neuromodulators:** Dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine, and epinephrine all open windows for plasticity. Psilocybin and MDMA both drive plasticity mainly via massive serotonin increases. Children playing musical instruments with others produce resting-state brain connectivity that mirrors what psychedelics produce in adults. [00:22:45](transcript.md#how-do-you-increase-neuroplasticity-after-30)

- **The soleus push-up for desk workers:** A University of Texas Houston study found that repeatedly lifting the heel while seated produced significant reductions in blood glucose and insulin levels, mimicking effects of walking. The soleus is 1% of total musculature but has a unique continuous fuel-utilization pathway. [00:28:46](transcript.md#what-type-of-movement-protocol-do-you-recommend-for-someone-working-from-home)

- **Zone 2 cardio and resistance training remain the foundation:** 150–200 minutes per week of zone 2 cardio (barely able to hold a conversation) plus three days of resistance training is the baseline protocol, per Peter Attia's framework. [00:28:46](transcript.md#what-type-of-movement-protocol-do-you-recommend-for-someone-working-from-home)

- **Space time bridging is a one-minute perceptual exercise, not a meditation:** By stepping through time domains — eyes closed focusing inward, then opening to nearby objects, then the horizon, then imagining the whole globe — you train deliberate shifts in temporal perception. High stress fine-slices time; panoramic vision broadens it. The exercise improves task switching. [00:33:02](transcript.md#what-does-your-morning-meditation-consist-of)

- **Macro-dose psychedelics show clinical promise; microdosing does not yet:** Clinical trials show macro-dose psilocybin is promising for major depression, eating disorders, and alcohol use disorder. Evidence for microdosing efficacy is weak. Pharmacologic approaches should not be the primary entry point to neuroplasticity. [00:22:45](transcript.md#how-do-you-increase-neuroplasticity-after-30)
