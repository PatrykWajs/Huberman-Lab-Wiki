# Episode 335: How Women Can Improve Their Fertility & Hormone Health - Dr. Natalie Crawford

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Natalie Crawford, double board-certified OB-GYN and reproductive endocrinologist and founder of Fora Fertility in Austin, TX, joins Andrew Huberman for a comprehensive deep-dive into female hormone health, fertility markers, and reproductive optimization at every life stage. The conversation covers fertility as a systemic health indicator (not merely a pregnancy metric), egg quality vs. quantity, ovulation tracking, AMH testing, egg freezing myths, IVF science, secondary infertility, perimenopause and hormone replacement therapy, and how lifestyle factors — sleep, skeletal muscle, diet, toxin avoidance — compound into measurable fertility outcomes. Dr. Crawford details how substances including cannabis, nicotine, and NSAIDs directly impair egg and sperm health, reviews supplements with the strongest human evidence (CoQ10, omega-3s, vitamin D), explains how endocrine disruptors, biotin supplementation, and BPA/phthalate exposure alter hormone lab results and reproductive outcomes, and shares her personal journey through four pregnancy losses and a late celiac disease diagnosis that fundamentally shaped her clinical philosophy.

## Key Takeaways

- **Fertility is a systemic health marker, not just a reproductive metric.** Infertility is associated with higher rates of metabolic syndrome, cancer, heart attack, stroke, and earlier mortality — not because infertility causes these, but because it is often the first warning sign of underlying chronic inflammation or insulin resistance. Ovulation and menstrual regularity are windows into hormonal and metabolic health at any age. [00:02:26](transcript.md#fertility-as-a-health-marker-infertility)

- **AMH test is essential — and costs only ~$79 out of pocket.** AMH (anti-Müllerian hormone) measures ovarian reserve (egg quantity, not quality). The American College of OB-GYN recommends it only after a formal infertility diagnosis, which Dr. Crawford calls counterproductive. Any woman who may want children should get it; it is available at LabCorp direct or via Function Health for approximately $79 without insurance. Causes of a low AMH — autoimmune disease, insulin resistance, endometriosis, smoking — can often be investigated and partially addressed. [00:48:17](transcript.md#tool-amh-test-fertility-education--patient-choices)

- **Ovulation tracking is a far more sensitive health marker than period tracking.** Knowing when you ovulate reveals the health of both the follicular and luteal phases. The earliest sign of an ovulation disorder is a shortened luteal phase (under 11 days) — invisible if you track only bleed dates. Ovulation disorders progress predictably: luteal phase defect → prolonged follicular phase → cycle irregularity → true amenorrhea. [00:53:13](transcript.md#tool-tracking-ovulation-ovulation-disorders)

- **NSAIDs block ovulation; cold plunges carry similar risk.** Ibuprofen and naproxen suppress the acute prostaglandin-driven inflammatory surge required for the follicle to rupture and release the egg. Hormonal changes proceed normally but the egg is never released. NSAIDs are safe only during menstruation itself; avoid for all other cycle days when trying to conceive. Cold plunges also dampen the acute inflammatory response needed for follicle rupture and are not recommended when actively trying to conceive. [01:29:28](transcript.md#support-egg-quality-tools-ovulation--avoiding-nsaids-5-lifestyle-non-negotiables)

- **Egg freezing does not deplete ovarian reserve.** A pervasive myth. IVF and egg freezing stimulate only the cohort of eggs already outside the ovarian vault — the same eggs that would die naturally that month regardless. The procedure cannot tap into the vault. Women are constantly losing eggs whether pregnant, breastfeeding, on birth control, or doing nothing; IVF simply allows more of that month's cohort to survive instead of dying unused. 17 million babies have been born via IVF. [01:01:13](transcript.md#does-egg-freezing-cause-early-menopause-in-vitro-fertilization-ivf)

- **Sleep is the most powerful and modifiable fertility lever.** FSH and LH are released during early-morning sleep. Subjectively reported poor sleep doubles infertility rates, lowers egg yield at retrieval, and reduces monthly pregnancy probability. Melatonin 1–3 mg taken 30 minutes before bed can improve egg quality — particularly for women with elevated inflammation, endometriosis, or undergoing IVF. Avoid the common 10 mg commercial doses; a pediatric 1 mg dose provides sufficient augmentation without replacing endogenous melatonin. [01:34:03](transcript.md#sleep-melatonin-cold-plunge)

- **Cannabis is the most clinically concerning fertility disruptor in current practice.** Cannabis use in the prior year reduces eggs retrieved at retrieval by ~25% and fertilization rates by ~28%. Male cannabis use causes DNA fragmentation in sperm, leading to significantly higher miscarriage rates in female partners and poor embryo development at the day-3 IVF stage. THC crosses the placenta directly; edibles tend to carry the highest THC levels. Approximately 15% of US women report using cannabis while pregnant. [01:53:12](transcript.md#cannabis--detriments-to-egg--sperm-health)

- **Nicotine and smoking directly reduce ovarian reserve and sperm counts.** Cigarette smoking is one of the few exposures that penetrates the ovarian vault and decreases egg count, causing earlier menopause. Oral nicotine pouches clearly tank sperm counts. Both disrupt ovulation via hormonal interference at the hypothalamic level. [01:58:57](transcript.md#nicotine-smoking-egg-health--sperm-count-healthy-lifestyle-practices)

- **CoQ10 has the strongest human evidence of any fertility supplement.** It supports mitochondrial function in eggs — the sole energy source controlling chromosome segregation and embryo development for the first 3 days post-fertilization (the male genome does not activate until day 3). Recommended universally during "trimester zero" alongside a prenatal vitamin with folate, omega-3 fatty acids, and vitamin D. Discontinue CoQ10 upon confirmed pregnancy due to lack of safety data. For sperm health: add L-carnitine, zinc, and selenium. NAD/NR/NMN shows promising animal data and may benefit unexplained infertility specifically; not a universal recommendation. [01:38:41](transcript.md#curcumin-nadnr-coq10-supplements-for-prenatal-care--sperm-health)

- **GLP-1 agonists at low doses show clinical promise for endometriosis and unexplained infertility.** Beyond their well-accepted use in PCOS and insulin resistance, Dr. Crawford is using low-dose GLP-1s as an anti-inflammatory tool in patients with probable or confirmed endometriosis, observing improved embryo outcomes in subsequent IVF cycles. This is emerging clinical experience without a definitive trial yet. Important caveat: very low body fat impairs hypothalamic signaling and ovulation, so GLP-1 use requires careful patient selection and experienced oversight. [02:02:21](transcript.md#glp-1s-pcos-endometriosis-human-growth-hormone)

- **Endocrine disruptors are a documented, dose-dependent fertility threat.** The EARTH cohort study showed higher EDC exposure led to fewer eggs retrieved, fewer embryos, and lower live birth rates even in IVF patients. Phthalates in scented products and BPA/BPS in thermal paper receipts are primary exposure vectors. "Fragrance-free" (nothing added) is meaningfully different from "unscented" (scent masked with another agent). Lavender, tea tree, and evening primrose oil have documented endocrine-disrupting properties and should be avoided as topical products. [02:17:27](transcript.md#endocrine-disruptors-fragrances-receipts-tool-fragrance-free)

- **Celiac disease and gluten sensitivity are underdiagnosed causes of recurrent pregnancy loss.** Dr. Crawford had four pregnancy losses and was dismissed despite advocating for testing. She conceived naturally after eliminating gluten and received a formal celiac diagnosis a decade later. Any unexplained recurrent pregnancy loss workup should include celiac/gluten sensitivity screening. After two pregnancy losses, a full evaluation (blood tests, semen analysis, sperm DNA fragmentation, uterine and tubal assessment) is warranted; the old standard of waiting for three losses is unacceptable. [02:22:48](transcript.md#patient-education--empowerment-inflammation-celiac-disease)

- **An anti-inflammatory diet anchored in fiber and healthy fats supports reproductive health.** Dietary cholesterol is the backbone for all steroid hormone synthesis including progesterone — insufficient saturated and monounsaturated fat reduces progesterone production and endometrial receptivity. High fiber (fruits, vegetables, whole grains) supports gut microbiome, insulin sensitivity, and hormonal balance. Ultra-processed foods and excess red meat do not belong in a fertility-optimized diet; the highest red meat consumption quartile correlates with poorer IVF embryo outcomes and increased endometriosis staging. [02:25:40](transcript.md#anti-inflammatory-diet-protein-fiber-red-meat)

- **Building skeletal muscle is the single most powerful metabolic lever for hormonal health.** It is the primary mechanism for reversing insulin resistance — the root driver of PCOS, anovulation, and poor reproductive outcomes. Chronic stress compounds insulin resistance; sleep, stress management, and resistance training work synergistically. [01:29:28](transcript.md#support-egg-quality-tools-ovulation--avoiding-nsaids-5-lifestyle-non-negotiables)

- **Secondary infertility is real and frequently underacknowledged.** Prior successful pregnancy offers statistical reassurance (pregnancy rates remain ~18–20% per month through age 37, vs. dropping sooner for first-timers), but does not guarantee future conception. Age-related egg quality decline, worsening sperm quality, and tincture-of-time diseases like endometriosis and adenomyosis all progress regardless of prior birth history. Seek evaluation after 6 months of trying without success (with the same partner, after a prior live birth). [00:22:02](transcript.md#does-prior-pregnancy-make-conception-easier-secondary-infertility)

- **Biotin supplements above 300 mcg produce false hormone lab results.** Biotin binds to the steroid assay and produces inaccurate readings for estradiol, progesterone, hCG, TSH, and testosterone. Many commercial hair/skin/nail supplements contain 10–30x the 300 mcg threshold. Discontinue all biotin-containing supplements for at least 7 days before any hormone panel. [02:10:58](transcript.md#platelet-rich-plasma-paternal-age--sperm-quality-biotin)

## Tools & Protocols

### AMH Test (Ovarian Reserve Screening)
- **What:** Blood test for anti-Müllerian hormone; reflects egg quantity (not quality); categorized as normal, above average, below average, or critically low; also informs prognosis for egg freezing and IVF yield
- **How:** Order via OB-GYN, fertility clinic, LabCorp direct, or Function Health; ~$79 out of pocket; if low, investigate underlying causes (autoimmune disease, insulin resistance, endometriosis, smoking); repeat annually if levels are meaningful or low
- **When:** Any woman who may want children, regardless of current plans; do not wait for a formal infertility diagnosis
- **Ref:** [00:48:17](transcript.md#tool-amh-test-fertility-education--patient-choices)

### Ovulation Tracking
- **What:** Monitor when you ovulate (not just when you bleed); track luteal phase length (under 11 days = luteal phase defect requiring investigation for prolactin, thyroid, AMH, PCOS)
- **How:** LH surge ovulation predictor kits (OPKs) or basal body temperature charting; stop hormonal birth control 3–6 months early to re-establish baseline cycle and detect any underlying disorder before actively trying
- **When:** Begin 3–6 months before attempting conception; ongoing when trying to conceive
- **Ref:** [00:53:13](transcript.md#tool-tracking-ovulation-ovulation-disorders)

### Avoid NSAIDs and Cold Plunges When Trying to Conceive
- **What:** Ibuprofen (Advil), naproxen (Aleve), and likely cold plunges can block the acute inflammatory response required for follicle rupture and egg release
- **How:** NSAIDs permitted only during menstrual bleeding; avoid for all other cycle days when trying to conceive; avoid cold plunges when actively trying to conceive
- **When:** Applies whenever actively trying to conceive
- **Ref:** [01:29:28](transcript.md#support-egg-quality-tools-ovulation--avoiding-nsaids-5-lifestyle-non-negotiables)

### Sleep Optimization for Fertility
- **What:** 7–9 hours nightly (women often need ~7.5 hrs, more in the luteal phase when progesterone synthesis is metabolically costly); consistent circadian timing supports FSH/LH pulsatility during early morning hours
- **How:** Dark room, cool temperature (~65–68°F), consistent sleep/wake schedule, partner alignment on bedtime; melatonin 1–3 mg 30 min before bed for those with elevated inflammation, endometriosis, or undergoing IVF (pediatric 1 mg dose is often sufficient augmentation)
- **When:** Ongoing; most critical during trimester zero (60–90 days pre-conception)
- **Ref:** [01:34:03](transcript.md#sleep-melatonin-cold-plunge)

### Trimester Zero Supplement Stack
- **What:** Evidence-based supplementation in the pre-conception window; supports mitochondrial health, chromosome integrity, and nutrient sufficiency for both egg and sperm
- **How:** CoQ10 + prenatal vitamin with folate + omega-3 fatty acids + vitamin D; stop CoQ10 once pregnancy confirmed (lack of safety data in pregnancy). For male partners: add L-carnitine, zinc, and selenium. Stop all biotin supplements ≥300 mcg at least 7 days before any hormone testing
- **When:** Begin 60–90 days before attempting conception ("trimester zero"); prenatal vitamin continues through pregnancy
- **Ref:** [01:38:41](transcript.md#curcumin-nadnr-coq10-supplements-for-prenatal-care--sperm-health)

### Fragrance-Free Environmental Swap
- **What:** Reduce phthalate and BPA/BPS exposure from everyday products; daily home exposures constitute the highest cumulative risk
- **How:** Replace all scented personal care and household products with "fragrance-free" labeled alternatives (not "unscented," which contains a masking agent); decline thermal paper receipts or wear gloves if occupationally exposed; avoid lavender, tea tree, and evening primrose oil as topical products
- **When:** Ongoing; implement before and during conception attempts
- **Ref:** [02:17:27](transcript.md#endocrine-disruptors-fragrances-receipts-tool-fragrance-free)

### Semen Analysis (Male Partner Baseline)
- **What:** Full sperm parameters — count, motility, morphology, and DNA fragmentation; at-home plus/minus strips are insufficient for clinical decision-making
- **How:** Clinic semen analysis or CLIA-certified mail-in kit; do not assume sperm are present because ejaculate is present; cannabis, nicotine, and heat exposure should be eliminated 90 days before testing
- **When:** Order before or simultaneously with female-side workup; do not wait 12 months of failed attempts; order immediately as part of any pregnancy loss evaluation
- **Ref:** [00:29:02](transcript.md#testing-sperm-pregnancy-loss--conceiving-again-fertility-testing)

### Birth Control Transition Planning
- **What:** Plan the exit from hormonal contraception to restore ovulatory tracking and endometrial readiness before attempting conception
- **How:** Stop the combined pill 3–6 months before planned conception to learn cycle patterns and detect any underlying disorder; remove progesterone IUD at least 6 months before conception to allow endometrial lining to rebuild; avoid Depo-Provera if planning pregnancy within 2 years (a single injection can suppress ovulation for up to 18 months)
- **When:** 3–6 months before starting to try; earlier for IUD or Depo-Provera users
- **Ref:** [01:21:22](transcript.md#concieving-after-hormonal-birth-control-iud-or-depo-provera)

### Five Lifestyle Non-Negotiables (Dr. Crawford's Framework)
- **What:** Sleep, stress management, skeletal muscle building (the primary insulin-resistance reversal tool and most powerful metabolic lever for hormonal health), anti-inflammatory diet, toxin avoidance
- **How:** No smoking, no cannabis, limit alcohol, maintain healthy BMI, prioritize 7–9 hours sleep, build strength through resistance training, eat high-fiber whole foods with adequate healthy fats (for progesterone synthesis), eliminate ultra-processed foods and behavioral toxins
- **When:** Ongoing; compounds over years but most impactful in trimester zero
- **Ref:** [01:29:28](transcript.md#support-egg-quality-tools-ovulation--avoiding-nsaids-5-lifestyle-non-negotiables)

### Biotin Washout Before Hormone Labs
- **What:** Biotin ≥300 mcg (present in many hair/skin/nail supplements at 10–30x this threshold) binds to the steroid assay and produces false readings for multiple hormones
- **How:** Stop all biotin-containing supplements for at least 7 days before any hormone panel; check supplement labels carefully for biotin content
- **When:** Before testing estradiol, progesterone, hCG, TSH, or testosterone
- **Ref:** [02:10:58](transcript.md#platelet-rich-plasma-paternal-age--sperm-quality-biotin)

### Recurrent Pregnancy Loss Evaluation
- **What:** After two pregnancy losses (or one with complications), pursue a full diagnostic workup rather than waiting for a third loss
- **How:** Blood tests (autoimmune, thrombophilia, thyroid, celiac antibodies), semen analysis with DNA fragmentation, uterine and tubal evaluation (saline sonogram); lighter periods after any intrauterine procedure warrant evaluation for scar tissue (Asherman's syndrome)
- **When:** After second pregnancy loss; after first if accompanied by heavy bleeding, infection, DNC, or change in cycle pattern
- **Ref:** [00:29:02](transcript.md#testing-sperm-pregnancy-loss--conceiving-again-fertility-testing)
