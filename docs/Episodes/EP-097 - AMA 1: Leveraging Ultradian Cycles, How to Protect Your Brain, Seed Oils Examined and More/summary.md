# Episode 97: AMA #1: Leveraging Ultradian Cycles, How to Protect Your Brain, Seed Oils Examined and More

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

This is the first Ask Me Anything (AMA) episode, a format exclusive to Huberman Lab Premium subscribers. The publicly available preview (~22 minutes) answers a single high-upvoted question from subscriber Jackson Lipfert on how to practically leverage ultradian 90-minute cycles for focused learning and cognitive work. Huberman explains the biological mechanism — ultradian glucocorticoid fluctuations that gate neuroplasticity molecules like BDNF — and gives a step-by-step protocol for identifying and scheduling daily focus blocks. He clarifies the common myth that all 90 minutes of an ultradian block are equally focused, explaining that only ~60 minutes represent true deep work. The full premium AMA covers additional topics including zone 2 cardio, seed oils, morning routines, brain protection, and aging protocols. The premium channel also supports human-subjects research at universities like Stanford via a dollar-for-dollar match from the Tiny Foundation.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Ultradian rhythms are 90-minute cycles** that persist from birth to death, during both waking and sleep, driven by fluctuations in the glucocorticoid (cortisol) system. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

2. **You cannot simply set a stopwatch at wake-up.** Ultradian cycles carry over from sleep — if you wake mid-cycle, that cycle continues. The practical fix is to identify when you feel peak alertness in the morning and start there. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

3. **Morning sunlight amplifies the cortisol peak** that launches the first ultradian work cycle of the day, making early light exposure a lever for shifting when focused learning is optimal. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

4. **Only ~60 minutes of the 90-minute block is true deep focus.** The first 10–15 minutes are warm-up; the last portion tapers off. Plan for one hour of peak learning inside each 90-minute block. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

5. **Most people can sustainably capture 1–2 ultradian work blocks per day;** three is exceptional, four is extraordinary and requires extreme sleep and schedule management — essentially a writing or learning retreat scenario. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

6. **A third block can be unlocked** by waking earlier, getting bright light immediately, and doing 10–15 minutes of high-intensity exercise (skipping rope, jumping jacks, brief jog), which shifts the cortisol pulse earlier and opens an additional morning work window (e.g. 6:00–7:30 AM). [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

7. **Neuroplasticity molecules — especially BDNF — fluctuate with ultradian glucocorticoid changes,** making these windows the biologically optimal times to encode learning (cognitive or motor skill). Synaptic receptors and modulators involved in plasticity are also regulated by these ultradian glucocorticoid shifts. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

8. **Actual neural rewiring happens during sleep and NSDR, not during the work block itself.** Deep sleep the following night and Non-Sleep Deep Rest the same day consolidate what was triggered during the focus session — this is why NSDR can accelerate learning. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

9. **The focus circuits themselves are subject to neuroplasticity** — the more consistently you engage in focused work blocks, the easier focusing becomes over time. Huberman does these every day including weekends (weekend sessions may be lighter, e.g. reading). [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

10. **Ultradian blocks apply equally to motor and physical skill learning** as to cognitive learning — any skill requiring focused attention and coordination of muscular movement with cognitive demands benefits from being timed within these windows. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

11. **The governing scientific concept is "iterative metaplasticity"** — a large literature largely unknown to the general public that Huberman plans to cover in depth on the standard podcast. [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

## Tools & Protocols

### Identify Your Peak Alertness Windows

- **What:** Observe when peak mental and physical energy naturally arrives — once before noon, once between noon and evening. These mark the onset of optimal ultradian learning windows.
- **How:** For 1–2 days, note the times when focus and energy peak without forcing them. No special equipment needed — just track your alertness trajectory through the day. Caffeine intake, exercise timing, and other daily habits will shift these peaks, so calibrate to your actual routine.
- **When:** One-time calibration, then ongoing awareness. Repeat if schedule, sleep timing, or caffeine habits change significantly.
- **Ref:** [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

### 90-Minute Focus Block

- **What:** A structured work session aligned to an ultradian cycle onset, set for 90 minutes, with the expectation that ~60 minutes will be truly deep focus.
- **How:** Start the block at your identified peak-alertness time. Eliminate distractions — phone off or in another room, Wi-Fi off if possible. Accept the first 10–15 minutes as mental warm-up. Work on cognitively demanding material — learning, writing, math, languages, creative work, or physical skill practice. Refocus when distracted; that is normal and expected. Let focus taper naturally toward the end.
- **When:** 1–2 times per day (morning and afternoon windows). Aim for daily consistency — even weekends (lighter cognitive load such as reading is acceptable).
- **Ref:** [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

### Morning Sunlight to Anchor Cortisol Peak

- **What:** Get sunlight (or bright artificial light) in your eyes as soon as possible after waking to amplify the morning cortisol pulse, which sets ultradian cycle timing for the day and enhances alertness, immune function, and focus capacity.
- **How:** Step outside within minutes of waking. On cloudy days or pre-sunrise, use bright indoor lighting. No sunglasses.
- **When:** Every morning, immediately upon waking.
- **Ref:** [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

### Early Wake + Brief High-Intensity Exercise to Unlock a Third Block

- **What:** Deliberately shift the morning cortisol pulse earlier by waking earlier and doing a short bout of high-intensity exercise, creating a third ultradian focus window before the standard mid-morning block.
- **How:** Set alarm ~1.5–2 hours earlier than usual. Immediately get bright light (artificial or natural). Do 10–15 minutes of high-intensity movement — skipping rope, jumping jacks, or a brief jog. This shifts the cortisol peak earlier so a work block between ~6:00–7:30 AM becomes available before the usual 9:30–10:00 AM block.
- **When:** Only when three blocks are genuinely needed. Requires adjusting bedtime earlier to protect sleep duration — both quality and duration of sleep matter for learning consolidation.
- **Ref:** [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)

### Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR) for Learning Consolidation

- **What:** A deliberate rest protocol (yoga nidra, deliberate relaxation) used between or after focus blocks to accelerate consolidation of what was learned, since actual neural rewiring occurs during rest — not during the work block.
- **How:** 10–30 minutes of deliberate non-sleep rest after a focus block or after lunch. Not required to fall asleep. Napping also counts if that is your preference.
- **When:** After a focus block or mid-afternoon. Optional but accelerates neuroplasticity consolidation.
- **Ref:** [00:01:09](transcript.md#000109-how-do-you-use-ultradian-cycles-in-your-daily-work)
