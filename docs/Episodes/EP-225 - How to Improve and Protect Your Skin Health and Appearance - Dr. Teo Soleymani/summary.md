# Episode 225: How to Improve & Protect Your Skin Health & Appearance | Dr. Teo Soleymani

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Teo Soleymani, a double-board-certified dermatologist and dermatologic surgeon, joins Andrew Huberman to discuss science-backed protocols for improving skin health, appearance, and longevity. They cover sun exposure, sunscreen selection, and nutrition through to retinoids, laser resurfacing, and red light therapy, plus the causes and treatments for acne, rosacea, eczema, psoriasis, vitiligo, and skin cancer surveillance. Most protocols discussed are zero-to-low cost and immediately actionable.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Skin turns over every 28 days** — the epidermis fully renews itself monthly via stem cells in its lowest layer, making the skin uniquely regenerative among organs and highly responsive to behavioral and topical interventions. [00:05:45](transcript.md#000545-skin-turnover-skin-appearance--stress)

2. **Chronic stress accelerates skin aging** — acute stress causes release of inflammatory chemical messengers (autocrine, paracrine, holocrine signaling) and vasoconstriction; chronic stress drives cortisol-mediated breakdown of collagen and elastin, visible as premature wrinkling, hair loss, and skin fragility. [00:05:45](transcript.md#000545-skin-turnover-skin-appearance--stress)

3. **Nicotine causes measurable vasoconstriction in skin** — smoking and vaping produce similar chronic vasoconstriction that impairs wound healing and accelerates aging; transdermal patches and gum deliver far lower nicotine concentrations to the skin surface and are less damaging. Surgeons transition smokers to patches/gum pre-surgery to protect wound healing. [00:16:31](transcript.md#001631-nicotine-vaping--skin-appearance)

4. **Alcohol has both direct and indirect negative skin effects** — short-term diuresis causes puffiness, hollowing, and dull appearance; chronic alcohol use disrupts the gut microbiome (high-percentage alcohols can act as gut paralytics), impairs sleep, elevates cortisol, and promotes inflammatory skin conditions. Acetaldehyde buildup (not alcohol itself) causes the vasodilation flush seen in individuals who cannot metabolize it efficiently. [00:18:37](transcript.md#001837-alcohol-skin-health)

5. **Drinking water does not directly improve skin hydration** — transepidermal water loss is genetically defined; moisturizer choice matched to skin type is the more effective lever. Morning eye puffiness is caused by lymphatic fluid redistribution during sleep (supine position), worsened by high-salt meals and seasonal allergies. [00:24:33](transcript.md#002433-hydration-fluid-intake--genetics)

6. **Mineral-based (zinc oxide/titanium dioxide) sunscreens are preferred over chemical sunscreens** — a 2020 study found chemical sunscreen ingredients absorb into blood plasma at 100–500x the FDA safety threshold; these lipophilic phenolic compounds (oxybenzone, octocrylene, octinoxate, homosalate) resemble BPA structurally and may cause endocrine disruption and nervous system effects. The FDA's 2021 proposed order reclassified 16 chemical filters from GRASE to "not GRASE." [01:15:56](transcript.md#011556-choose-mineral-or-chemical-sunscreen)

7. **Sun exposure has a paradoxical relationship with the most deadly skin cancers** — melanoma and squamous cell carcinoma frequently arise in non-sun-exposed areas; no randomized trial has shown topical sunscreen reduces basal cell carcinoma incidence (the most common skin cancer, 1 in 4 Americans) or disease-specific mortality. Sunscreen consistently protects against premature photoaging. Non-burning sun exposure has broad health benefits including vitamin D synthesis (~15 min on forearms) and circadian regulation. [01:02:45](transcript.md#010245-physical-barriers-sunscreens-oral-supplements--skin-cancer)

8. **Retinoids are the gold standard topical for skin rejuvenation** — prescription tretinoin/adapalene/tazarotene accelerate skin turnover from 28 days to 7–9 days, grow new collagen (histologically verified), reduce pre-cancerous actinic keratoses, and visibly improve skin. OTC retinol is an inactive prodrug requiring two-step conversion and is sold at non-therapeutic concentrations; most OTC retinols destabilize within one year. [01:42:58](transcript.md#014258-retinoids-vs-retinol-skin-appearance)

9. **Laser resurfacing reduces skin cancer risk by ~20% and activates youthful gene programs** — microarray gene studies confirm resurfacing lasers activate quiescent youthful cell gene expression; ablative lasers (~every 5 years, 2-week downtime) give deeper results; non-ablative fractional lasers (e.g., Fraxel) offer meaningful improvement with minimal recovery and are recommended annually or biannually. Microdermabrasion only removes dead stratum corneum — far shallower than laser. [01:49:45](transcript.md#014945-laser-resurfacing-exfoliation-microdermabrasion)

10. **Red light therapy improves vascular flow and accelerates skin recovery** — evidence supports benefit for hair restoration, post-procedure healing, and UV damage repair; in animal studies, pre-treating skin with red light before UV exposure significantly reduces subsequent UV-induced damage. Wall panels are substantially more effective than battery-powered face masks due to higher energy density. [01:56:52](transcript.md#015652-red-light-therapy--phototherapy-face-masks-light-panels)

11. **High-protein, anti-inflammatory diet is the most impactful dietary change for skin** — complete animal-source proteins, tolerable fruits and vegetables, and low-glycemic eating reduce inflammatory skin conditions measurably; high-glycemic foods and skim/nonfat dairy (which contains emulsifiers with a glycemic response) worsen acne; collagen peptides (~10 g/day) and omega-3 fatty acids have supporting evidence. Collagen's cosmetic benefit may partly stem from increased plasma osmolality drawing water into vessels rather than direct collagen synthesis. [01:34:28](transcript.md#013428-tool-nutrition-for-skin-health-protein-anti-inflammatory-collagen-omega-3)

12. **Polypodium leucotomos (oral fern extract) provides internal UV and visible-light protection** — derived from an Amazonian fern used by indigenous populations before multi-day fishing expeditions, it increases the skin's minimal erythema dose; it also blocks visible-light effects relevant to melasma, which chemical sunscreens cannot do. Doses studied: 50–480 mg; commonly 240–480 mg daily. [01:20:24](transcript.md#012024-polypodium-supplement-sun-exposure-skin-redness)

13. **Squamous cell carcinoma now kills more Americans than melanoma** — as of ~2018 (when Nobel Prize-winning immunotherapy transformed melanoma outcomes), SCC causes one death every 37 minutes vs. one every 62 minutes for melanoma. HPV strains 16, 18, 31, 33 are an increasing cause of SCC in sun-protected genital and oral areas, especially in younger adults. Annual full-body dermatologist exam — every square inch including genitals — is the standard recommendation; the Gardasil vaccine (recommended through late 40s for both sexes) provides protection. [02:30:15](transcript.md#023015-tattoos-tool-monitoring-for-skin-cancer-moles-annual-exams)

## Tools & Protocols

### Moisturizer Selection
- **What:** Choose a moisturizer matched to skin type — oily, normal, or dry.
- **How:** Look for "non-comedogenic" on the label. Ointments (petrolatum/Vaseline/Aquaphor) = best barrier, ideal for eczema-prone or dry skin. Creams (oil-water emulsions) = normal skin. Lotions (powders resuspended in water) = oily or acne-prone skin. Avoid fragrances and unnecessary additives. Jar packaging provides more occlusion but can cause breakouts in acne-prone individuals; pump packaging is lighter. Price does not predict performance — drugstore options are equally effective.
- **When:** After cleansing; more frequently in dry climates or winter.
- **Ref:** [00:26:19](transcript.md#002619-tool-selecting-a-moisturizer)

### Skin Cleansing
**What:** Use a gentle, fragrance-free cleanser matched to skin type — expensive cleansers show no evidence of outperforming inexpensive ones.
**How:** Unscented Dove bar soap or Cetaphil are dermatologist-recommended for sensitive and normal skin. For oily/acne-prone skin: mild salicylic acid or benzoyl peroxide wash. Shower immediately after exercise. Do not over-wash — once daily is typically sufficient; twice if very oily or post-exercise. Avoid harsh scrubbing.
**When:** Daily; immediately after exercise.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 00:32:14](transcript.md)

### Dandruff / Seborrheic Dermatitis Treatment
**What:** Targeted antifungal or zinc-pyrithione shampoos for flaky/oily scalp caused by Malassezia yeast overgrowth.
**How:** For medical-grade dandruff (seborrheic dermatitis): ketoconazole 1% shampoo (OTC) or 2% (prescription). For mild dry/flaky scalp: zinc pyrithione shampoos (e.g., Head & Shoulders). For oily scalp without true dandruff: salicylic acid shampoos. Use 1–2x per week; avoid daily use of medicated shampoos unless directed.
**When:** As needed; frequency adjusted to severity.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 00:41:57](transcript.md)

### Sun Exposure Protocol
**What:** Get regular, non-burning sun exposure for mood, vitamin D, and circadian health while protecting against premature photoaging.
**How:** Early morning sunlight (low-angle sun) for circadian benefit — no sunscreen needed at low-angle, low-intensity times. Moderate mid-day sun in small doses for vitamin D production. Use physical barriers (hat, long sleeves, shade) as primary protection; mineral sunscreen as secondary. The critical threshold is to avoid burning — this is the primary harm mechanism.
**When:** Daily morning sun exposure; mid-day sun in moderate, non-burning doses.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 00:50:20](transcript.md)

### Mineral-Based Sunscreen Use
**What:** Apply broad-spectrum zinc oxide and/or titanium dioxide sunscreen to exposed skin.
**How:** Select any mineral sunscreen labeled "broad spectrum" (covers UVA + UVB) with SPF 30–50. Apply to face, ears, neck, and any exposed skin 15–20 min before going out. Reapply every 2 hours in prolonged sun. Avoid chemical UV filters (oxybenzone, octinoxate, avobenzone, homosalate, etc.), especially for children. Choose a texture/feel you actually like — compliance is paramount.
**When:** Any time skin will be exposed to intense or mid-day sun.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 01:26:02](transcript.md)

### Polypodium Leucotomos (Internal Sun Protection)
**What:** Oral fern extract supplement that raises the skin's minimal erythema dose, reducing UV-induced redness and damage.
**How:** Take orally as directed (available standalone or in combination products like Sun Powder, which also contains nicotinamide). Works as an adjunct — does not replace topical sunscreens but complements them. Particularly useful when reapplication is inconvenient (outdoor work, sports).
**When:** Daily oral supplement; take before anticipated prolonged sun exposure.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 01:20:24](transcript.md)

### Nutrition for Skin Health
**What:** High-protein, anti-inflammatory diet to reduce inflammatory skin conditions and support collagen production.
**How:** Prioritize complete animal-source proteins (eggs, meat, fish, chicken) for full amino acid profiles. Eat fruits and vegetables tolerable to your gut microbiome. Eliminate high-glycemic foods (refined carbs, sugar) — especially important for acne. Trial dairy elimination for 4–6 weeks if acne-prone. Consider hydrolyzed collagen peptides (~10 g/day) and omega-3 fatty acids (fish oil) as supplements with supporting evidence for skin structure and inflammation reduction.
**When:** Daily dietary practice.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 01:34:28](transcript.md)

### Retinoid / Retinol Protocol
**What:** Topical vitamin A derivative to accelerate skin turnover, reduce pre-cancers, and improve skin appearance.
**How:** Start with OTC retinol at low concentration (0.025–0.05%) if new; apply pea-sized amount to face at night after cleansing. Prescription tretinoin (0.025–0.1%) is more potent — requires dermatologist. Allow 6–12 weeks before visible improvement. Use SPF daily as skin becomes more photosensitive. Reduce frequency or concentration if excessive dryness or irritation occurs. Build up from 2–3 nights/week to nightly.
**When:** Nightly application; build up frequency over weeks.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 01:42:58](transcript.md)

### Red Light Therapy
**What:** Red (630–700 nm) and near-infrared (800–1000 nm) light exposure to improve vascular flow, skin healing, and appearance.
**How:** Use a clinically-validated device (face mask or full panel); follow manufacturer protocol — typically 10–20 minutes per session. Use post-procedure to accelerate healing, for hair restoration, or general skin health maintenance. Pre-treating skin with red light before anticipated UV exposure may reduce subsequent UV-induced damage.
**When:** 3–5 sessions per week; any time of day.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 01:56:52](transcript.md)

### Annual Skin Cancer Monitoring
**What:** Full-body skin examination by a board-certified dermatologist plus monthly self-checks.
**How:** Schedule annual full-body exam with a dermatologist. Perform monthly self-checks using a hand mirror for hard-to-see areas (back, scalp). Use the ABCDE framework: Asymmetry, Border irregularity, Color variation, Diameter >6 mm, Evolution (any change over time). Alert dermatologist if you have tattoos that could mask lesions. High-risk individuals (fair skin, family history, immunosuppression) may need exams every 6 months. Consider the Gardasil HPV vaccine — it covers strains 16, 18, 31, 33+ that cause squamous cell carcinoma; recommended through late 40s for both sexes.
**When:** Annually minimum; self-check monthly.
**Ref:** [transcript.md — 02:30:15](transcript.md)
