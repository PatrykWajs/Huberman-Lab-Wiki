# Episode 67: How to Optimize Your Hormones for Health & Vitality | Dr. Kyle Gillett

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Kyle Gillett, a dual board-certified physician in family medicine and obesity medicine, joins Andrew Huberman for a comprehensive deep dive into hormone optimization for both men and women across the lifespan. The conversation covers the six pillars of hormone health — diet, exercise, stress, sleep, sunlight, and spiritual health — and systematically examines testosterone, estrogen, DHT, prolactin, growth hormone, and peptides through the lens of behavioral tools, nutrition, supplementation, and clinical therapies. The episode is unusually dense with actionable protocols and mechanistic explanations, making it a reference-grade resource on endocrinology for a general audience.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The Six Pillars of Hormone Health** — Diet, exercise, stress optimization, sleep, sunlight/outdoor exposure, and spiritual health are the foundational levers. Diet and exercise are the two most powerful; resistance training is specifically highlighted for hormone optimization. [00:14:17](transcript.md#001417-the-six-pillars-of-hormone-health-optimization)

2. **Caloric restriction vs. testosterone** — Obesity suppresses testosterone via aromatase converting testosterone to estrogen in adipose tissue. Moderate caloric restriction improves testosterone in overweight individuals, but the same restriction in young, healthy lean individuals lowers it (supported by a 2021 Mayo Clinic Proceedings systematic review). [00:21:06](transcript.md#002106-caloric-restriction-obesity--testosterone)

3. **Intermittent fasting and growth hormone** — Fasting increases GH pulsatility overnight. A 6–8 hour eating window can meaningfully raise GH. IGF-1 has a half-life of nearly a week, providing sustained anabolic signaling. Eating 2–3 hours before sleep still permits good GH output, though the longer the fast, the larger the pulse. [00:23:55](transcript.md#002355-intermittent-fasting-growth-hormone-gh-igf-1)

4. **DHT, hair loss, and creatine** — DHT is a potent androgen converted from testosterone via 5-alpha reductase. Creatine monohydrate raises DHT (not testosterone directly) and may accelerate genetic male pattern baldness in susceptible individuals. Finasteride inhibits isoforms 2 and 3; saw palmetto inhibits 1 and 2; dutasteride inhibits all three — each with different tissue-specific effects and side effect profiles. Hair lost from creatine can recover within months if follicles are still intact. [00:43:46](transcript.md#004346-dht-in-men-and-women-turmericicurcumin-creatine)

5. **PCOS and inositol** — PCOS affects up to 10–20% of women (often underdiagnosed) and is characterized by androgen excess, insulin resistance, and irregular cycles. Myo-inositol (insulin sensitizer) combined with D-chiro-inositol (weak anti-androgen) in a 40:1 ratio can improve insulin sensitivity, lower androgens, and restore menstrual regularity. DIM (diindolylmethane) is a mild anti-androgen/anti-estrogen useful adjunct. [00:58:12](transcript.md#005812-polycystic-ovary-syndrome-pcos-inositol-dim)

6. **Oral contraceptives and hormone effects** — Oral contraceptives contain synthetic estrogen (e.g., ethinyl estradiol, 100x more potent than endogenous estradiol at the liver) that dramatically raises SHBG, crashing free testosterone and DHT. This flattens the mid-cycle androgen peak and associated libido surge. Different synthetic progestins carry different blood clot risks (related to SHBG/platelet elevation) and anti-androgenic effects. Fertility recovery after stopping OCP is generally complete by 6–12 months off. [01:04:00](transcript.md#010400-oral-contraception-perceived-attractiveness-fertility)

7. **Testosterone supplementation does not cause prostate cancer** — TRT does not cause prostate cancer, but will accelerate growth of existing prostate cancer. Prostate cancer is nearly universal with aging (50% prevalence by autopsy at 80, 90%+ at 90). PSA, hsCRP, prostate MRI, and dietary fiber are the key monitoring and prevention tools. Interestingly, having female offspring correlates with slightly higher prostate cancer risk — possibly linking estrogen, not testosterone, to prostate cancer. [01:15:34](transcript.md#011534-testosterone-supplementation--prostate-cancer)

8. **Tongkat Ali (Long Jack)** — 400 mg/day of Indonesian tongkat ali modestly reduces SHBG, weakly inhibits aromatase, and may act as a non-selective estrogen receptor modifier — all increasing free testosterone. Cycling protocol: 11 months on, 1 month off alone; 3 weeks on, 1 week off when combined with Fadogia. Also useful for women with hyperestrogenism (endometriosis) and as post-OCP PCT to normalize SHBG. [01:47:04](transcript.md#014704-tongkat-ali-long-jack-estrogenotestosterone-levels)

9. **Fadogia Agrestis and LH** — Fadogia appears to raise LH and increase LH receptor sensitivity in the testes, stimulating endogenous testosterone production. Recommended cycle: 3 weeks on, 1 week off at ~600 mg/day. Best candidates have subclinical secondary hypogonadism (low LH, normal prolactin and estrogen). Not suitable if LH is already elevated (suggesting primary gonadal issue). [01:52:25](transcript.md#015225-fadogia-agrestis-luteinizing-hormone-lh-frequency)

10. **Prolactin and dopamine balance** — Prolactin and estrogen are closely linked (estrogen upregulates the PRL gene). High prolactin suppresses testosterone and libido. Pituitary microadenomas are far more common than most realize. First-line: eliminate casein and gluten (mu-opioid receptor agonists that raise prolactin). Then consider P5P (50 mg 1–2x/day) and mixed tocopherol vitamin E. GABA-based sleep supplements (including alcohol) should not be taken daily — 3–4 nights/week max; exceptions are magnesium and L-theanine. [02:04:18](transcript.md#020418-prolactin--dopamine-pituitary-damage)

11. **Cold and heat exposure effects on fertility** — Testes require 5–10°F cooler than core body temperature. Varicoceles (present in ~20–25% of men, usually left-sided) raise scrotal temperature and impair sperm. Hot tubs and jacuzzis are "enemies number one and two of sperm." Saunas should be avoided entirely when trying to conceive (sperm recovery takes 6–8 weeks). Cold exposure does not appear to change estrogen output in women but does increase beta-adrenergic CNS activity. [02:29:05](transcript.md#022905-cold--hot-exposure-hormones--fertility)

12. **BPC-157 for injury repair** — BPC-157 is bioidentical to a gastric-derived peptide. It accelerates healing of tendons, ligaments, and muscles by upregulating VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor), increasing blood flow to poorly-vascularized tissues. Typical dosing: 250–500 mcg subcutaneously or intramuscularly near the injury site for up to 6 weeks. Contraindicated in active cancer or high cancer risk (VEGF promotion is the opposite of anti-cancer drugs like Avastin). Sourcing is critical — unregulated sources often contain LPS that causes inflammation. [02:39:38](transcript.md#023938-bpc-157--injury-dosing-frequency)

## Tools & Protocols

### Diet for Hormone Optimization
- **What:** Individualized whole-food diet, minimizing ultra-processed foods.
- **How:** Prioritize adequate dietary fat (cholesterol is the precursor to steroid hormones); include soluble and insoluble fiber to support gut estrogen clearance and prostate health. Vegans should supplement with algae-based omega-3s. Grass-fed ruminant products contain naturally occurring healthy trans fats (conjugated omega-3/omega-6). High alcohol and high-fat meals both upregulate aromatase.
- **When:** Daily habit.
- **Ref:** [00:17:14](transcript.md#001714-diet-for-hormone-health-blood-testing)

### Caloric Restriction
- **What:** Modest caloric deficit to reduce adiposity and lower aromatase activity.
- **How:** Sustainable deficit for overweight/obese individuals. Avoid extreme restriction in young, lean individuals — it lowers testosterone.
- **When:** Indicated for those with metabolic syndrome or obesity; unnecessary for lean individuals.
- **Ref:** [00:21:06](transcript.md#002106-caloric-restriction-obesity--testosterone)

### Intermittent Fasting / Time-Restricted Eating
- **What:** Compress eating into a 6–8 hour window to increase GH pulsatility.
- **How:** Fast in the morning, eat from roughly noon to 8 pm. Stopping food 2–3 hours before sleep still supports good GH output.
- **When:** Daily or 5 days/week. Ensure caloric maintenance if lean.
- **Ref:** [00:23:55](transcript.md#002355-intermittent-fasting-growth-hormone-gh-igf-1)

### Resistance Training
- **What:** Weight training is the single best exercise for testosterone and GH optimization.
- **How:** Compound lifts, progressive overload; combine with Zone 2 cardio (150–180 min/week).
- **When:** 3–5x/week.
- **Ref:** [00:20:21](transcript.md#002021-exercise-for-hormone-health)

### Sleep Optimization
- **What:** 7–9 hours of quality sleep is the most important pillar after diet and exercise. The majority of GH is secreted early in sleep.
- **How:** Consistent sleep/wake schedule, dark/cool room, limit blue light before bed. Avoid food 2–3 hours before sleep to maximize GH pulse.
- **When:** Nightly.
- **Ref:** [00:29:08](transcript.md#002908-sleep-quality--hormones)

### Sleep Supplement Frequency
- **What:** Many sleep supplements should not be taken nightly — daily GABA agonism is immunosuppressive and blunts response over time.
- **How:** GABA, Trazodone, and melatonin (situation-dependent) — use 3–4 nights/week max, not daily. Magnesium (threonate or glycinate) and L-theanine are safe to take nightly. Alcohol follows the same rule: 2 drinks twice/week is preferable to 1 drink/night.
- **When:** As needed; rotate non-daily supplements.
- **Ref:** [01:14:27](transcript.md#011427-sleep-supplement-frequency)

### Blood Testing Frequency
- **What:** Regular hormone panels (testosterone, estrogen, SHBG, LH, FSH, prolactin, thyroid, hsCRP, TMAO if on L-carnitine).
- **How:** Get both fasting and non-fasting samples ("high tide and low tide"). Start in your 20s to build a baseline.
- **When:** Every 3–6 months for preventative monitoring; adjust around interventions. FSAs/HSAs and declining costs make testing increasingly accessible despite unlikely insurance coverage.
- **Ref:** [02:18:19](transcript.md#021819-blood-test-frequency)

### Inositol (for PCOS)
- **What:** Myo-inositol + D-chiro-inositol to improve insulin sensitivity and lower androgens.
- **How:** 2–4 g myo-inositol combined with D-chiro-inositol in a 40:1 ratio daily. Men and those not seeking anti-androgenic effects should use myo-inositol only.
- **When:** Ongoing for women with PCOS or metabolic syndrome.
- **Ref:** [00:58:12](transcript.md#005812-polycystic-ovary-syndrome-pcos-inositol-dim)

### DIM (Diindolylmethane)
- **What:** Mild anti-androgen and anti-estrogen from cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, kale); promotes healthier estrogen metabolism.
- **How:** 100–200 mg/day with food.
- **When:** Useful for PCOS or estrogen dominance. Note: also lowers androgens — if on TRT and using DIM, the TRT dose may simply be too high.
- **Ref:** [00:58:12](transcript.md#005812-polycystic-ovary-syndrome-pcos-inositol-dim)

### Calcium D-Glucarate
- **What:** Inhibits beta-glucuronidase, preventing reabsorption of excreted estrogens in the gut; also modulates estrogen receptor sensitivity.
- **How:** 500–1,000 mg/day.
- **When:** When estrogen is elevated; pairs well with DIM. Not as potent as an aromatase inhibitor — unlikely to crash estrogen at these doses.
- **Ref:** [01:35:17](transcript.md#013517-estrogen--aromatase-inhibitors-calcium-d-glucarate-dim)

### Saw Palmetto
- **What:** Mild 5-alpha reductase inhibitor (isoforms 1 and 2); supports prostate health.
- **How:** 300–500 mg/day of standardized extract. Takes ~3 months for full effect.
- **When:** Men concerned about DHT-related hair loss or prostate enlargement; lower side effect risk than finasteride.
- **Ref:** [00:50:10](transcript.md#005010-5-alpha-reductase-finasteride-saw-palmetto)

### Finasteride / Dutasteride
- **What:** Prescription 5-alpha reductase inhibitors. Finasteride inhibits isoforms 2 and 3; dutasteride inhibits all three (including the brain isoform, isoform 3).
- **How:** Finasteride: 1 mg/day for hair loss; 5 mg/day for BPH. Dutasteride: as prescribed. Both require physician supervision.
- **When:** Under physician supervision. Post-finasteride syndrome is real but very unlikely if medication is stopped promptly upon side effects. Also blocks conversion of progesterone to sleep/GABA-active 5-alpha metabolites.
- **Ref:** [00:50:10](transcript.md#005010-5-alpha-reductase-finasteride-saw-palmetto)

### Dutasteride Mesotherapy (Hair Loss)
- **What:** Localized scalp injections of dutasteride to reduce DHT conversion only in the scalp — avoids systemic side effects.
- **How:** Repeated injections in areas prone to androgenetic alopecia.
- **When:** For men or women with androgenetic alopecia (including PCOS-related) who want to avoid systemic 5-AR inhibitor side effects.
- **Ref:** [00:55:07](transcript.md#005507-hair-regrowth-male-pattern-baldness)

### Ecdysterone / Turkesterone
- **What:** Ecdysteroids that act as beta-estradiol receptor agonists; may support lean body mass. Work strongly for some, not at all for others.
- **How:** Dosages vary by product. Best candidates have naturally low estrogen.
- **When:** May cause water retention; do not close growth plates (alpha-estradiol receptor); unlikely to reduce libido.
- **Ref:** [01:45:34](transcript.md#014534-aromatase-supplements-ecdysterone-turkesterone)

### Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia / Long Jack)
- **What:** Adaptogenic herb that modestly increases free testosterone by reducing SHBG, weakly inhibiting aromatase, and non-selectively modifying estrogen receptors.
- **How:** 400 mg/day of standardized Indonesian extract, taken early in the day (mild stimulant effect). Do not exceed 400 mg.
- **When:** Cycle 11 months on, 1 month off (solo); 3 weeks on, 1 week off (when combined with Fadogia Agrestis). Useful for both men and women with hyperestrogenism, and as post-OCP PCT to normalize SHBG.
- **Ref:** [01:47:04](transcript.md#014704-tongkat-ali-long-jack-estrogenotestosterone-levels)

### Fadogia Agrestis
- **What:** Nigerian shrub extract that appears to raise LH and increase LH receptor sensitivity in the testes, stimulating endogenous testosterone production.
- **How:** ~600 mg/day.
- **When:** Cycle 3 weeks on, 1 week off. Best for those with subclinical secondary hypogonadism (low LH, normal prolactin and estrogen). Not suitable if LH is already elevated.
- **Ref:** [01:52:25](transcript.md#015225-fadogia-agrestis-luteinizing-hormone-lh-frequency)

### Boron
- **What:** Trace mineral that lowers SHBG, increasing free testosterone and estradiol. Effect is primarily acute.
- **How:** 3–6 mg once to twice daily. Found naturally in dates and raisins grown in mineral-rich Mediterranean soils.
- **When:** Ongoing; particularly useful if SHBG is high. May benefit from cycling given primarily acute effect.
- **Ref:** [01:56:44](transcript.md#015644-boron-sex-hormone-binding-globulin-shbg)

### hCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin)
- **What:** LH analog used to maintain testicular function, fertility, and genital tissue sensitivity. Also improves thyroid function (similar receptor to TSH). Useful in post-finasteride syndrome recovery.
- **How:** 250–500 IU subcutaneously 2–3x/week for fertility support during TRT (prescription only). High-dose fertility protocols use 10,000 IU. Suppresses endogenous LH in a dose-dependent manner. Some use hCG monotherapy as a less lipid-impacting alternative to TRT.
- **When:** Men on TRT who want to preserve fertility or testicular size; post-finasteride syndrome; selected individuals with secondary hypogonadism.
- **Ref:** [01:58:13](transcript.md#015813-human-chorionic-gonadotropin-hcg-fertility)

### Selenium / Brazil Nuts (Thyroid Health)
- **What:** Selenium is essential for thyroid function and synergistic with hCG for thyroid support. Helps prevent hypothyroxinemia of pregnancy.
- **How:** 3–5 Brazil nuts per day provides adequate selenium.
- **When:** Daily; especially relevant for anyone with hypothyroidism or considering hCG.
- **Ref:** [01:58:13](transcript.md#015813-human-chorionic-gonadotropin-hcg-fertility)

### Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)
- **What:** Exogenous testosterone for clinically low testosterone (hypogonadism) or off-label to push into higher-normal range.
- **How:** Typical replacement dose: 100–120 mg/week total, divided into 2–3 subcutaneous or intramuscular injections for steady-state levels. Ester choice (cypionate, enanthate, undecanoate) depends on SHBG. Oral form: JATENZO (testosterone undecanoate, FDA-approved, taken twice daily with food). TRT increases sleep apnea risk dose-dependently even in eugonadal men.
- **When:** Under physician supervision with documented symptoms. Monitor PSA, hematocrit, estrogen, prolactin.
- **Ref:** [01:30:54](transcript.md#013054-testosterone-replacement-therapy-trt)

### Tadalafil (Cialis)
- **What:** PDE5 inhibitor for erectile dysfunction, prostate health (reduces congestion), and pelvic floor support. Also a weak androgen receptor sensitizer (similar to L-carnitine).
- **How:** 2.5–5 mg/day (low dose daily) for prostate/pelvic floor; 10–20 mg as needed for ED (prescription). Half-life ~36 hours. Avoid close to heavy exercise — may prevent compensatory hypertensive response and increase syncope risk.
- **When:** Men with BPH, pelvic floor dysfunction, or ED. Note: Viagra (sildenafil) affects color discrimination at long distances — avoid if a pilot; also may improve scalp blood flow and eyebrow growth.
- **Ref:** [01:24:05](transcript.md#012405-prostate-health--pelvic-floor-viagra-tadalafil)

### Casein and Gluten Avoidance (Prolactin Control)
- **What:** Casein (milk protein) and gluten are mu-opioid receptor agonists in the gut that can raise prolactin — the same mechanism as opiates suppressing LH/FSH.
- **How:** Reduce or eliminate casein-heavy dairy and gluten-containing foods.
- **When:** First-line intervention before supplements if prolactin is mildly elevated.
- **Ref:** [02:08:34](transcript.md#020834-augmenting-dopamine-levels-casein-gluten-vitamin-e-vitamin-b6-p5p)

### Vitamin B6 as P5P (Pyridoxal-5-Phosphate)
- **What:** Pre-activated form of B6 that supports dopamine synthesis and keeps prolactin in range. Unlike regular B6 (pyridoxine), P5P does not accumulate and does not cause peripheral neuropathy.
- **How:** 50 mg once to twice daily.
- **When:** If prolactin is mildly elevated (under ~40 ng/mL) or dopamine-related symptoms are present. Can shorten post-orgasm refractory period by blunting the prolactin spike.
- **Ref:** [02:08:34](transcript.md#020834-augmenting-dopamine-levels-casein-gluten-vitamin-e-vitamin-b6-p5p)

### Vitamin E (Mixed Tocopherols)
- **What:** Fat-soluble antioxidant; modestly supports dopamine and reduces prolactin. Many people are sufficient in alpha-tocopherol but low in the gamma form.
- **How:** 400 IU/day of mixed tocopherols (ensure gamma form is included).
- **When:** As part of a hormone-supportive supplement stack alongside P5P.
- **Ref:** [02:08:34](transcript.md#020834-augmenting-dopamine-levels-casein-gluten-vitamin-e-vitamin-b6-p5p)

### L-Carnitine
- **What:** Increases androgen receptor density; supports sperm motility, egg quality, and mitochondrial fatty acid utilization (carnitine palmitoyl CoA shuttle).
- **How:** Oral L-carnitine/acetyl-L-carnitine is only ~10% bioavailable — 10 g oral ≈ 1 g absorbed. Injectable L-carnitine is 100% bioavailable; inject IM (not subcutaneous — aqueous solution burns). Minimum effective injectable dose for sperm motility/androgen receptor upregulation: ~200 mg.
- **When:** Men and women seeking to improve fertility or testosterone sensitivity. Pair with allicin (garlic) to mitigate TMAO. Check TMAO on bloodwork.
- **Ref:** [02:12:30](transcript.md#021230-l-carnitine--fertility-tmao--allicin-garlic)

### Garlic / Allicin
- **What:** Allicin in garlic inhibits gut bacteria that convert L-carnitine (and choline precursors like alpha-GPC, phosphatidylserine) to TMAO — a cardiovascular risk factor. Also supports cardiovascular tone and blood flow.
- **How:** 1 clove fresh garlic daily, or 600 mg garlic capsule (standardized for allicin). TMAO can be measured directly on a blood panel.
- **When:** Concurrent with L-carnitine supplementation. Some people's microbiomes do not produce much TMAO regardless.
- **Ref:** [02:12:30](transcript.md#021230-l-carnitine--fertility-tmao--allicin-garlic)

### Cold Exposure
- **What:** Cold water immersion or cold showers to increase catecholamines and testosterone, particularly helpful in men with varicoceles or mild primary hypogonadism.
- **How:** 1–5 minutes of cold immersion. Do not apply cold directly to testes if trying to conceive. Specialized cooling undergarments are an emerging fertility tool.
- **When:** Best in the morning. No evidence of estrogen-altering effects in women, but increases beta-adrenergic CNS activity.
- **Ref:** [02:29:05](transcript.md#022905-cold--hot-exposure-hormones--fertility)

### Sauna / Heat Exposure
- **What:** Heat stress supports GH release and cardiovascular health.
- **How:** 15–20 min sauna sessions at 170–200°F.
- **When:** Avoid entirely if trying to conceive — sperm quality recovery takes 6–8 weeks. Hot tubs and jacuzzis are worse than dry saunas (warmed water heats testes faster).
- **Ref:** [02:29:05](transcript.md#022905-cold--hot-exposure-hormones--fertility)

### Tesamorelin (GHRH Analog)
- **What:** Prescription growth hormone-releasing hormone analog; FDA-approved for HIV-associated lipodystrophy; reduces visceral fat.
- **How:** 1–2 mg subcutaneously daily (prescription).
- **When:** Under physician supervision. Most people without lipodystrophy or GH deficiency do not need GHRPs — cosmetic/body composition benefits can be achieved through lifestyle. Cancer risk consideration applies (similar to any GH-elevating intervention).
- **Ref:** [02:32:34](transcript.md#023234-peptide-hormones-insulin-tesamorelin-ghrelin)

### BPC-157
- **What:** Body protective compound peptide bioidentical to a gastric-derived protein. Accelerates healing of tendons, ligaments, and muscles by upregulating VEGF to increase blood flow to poorly-vascularized tissues.
- **How:** 250–500 mcg subcutaneously or intramuscularly near injury site for up to 6 weeks, or orally for gut healing. Must be sourced from a reputable compounding pharmacy — unregulated sources often contain LPS contamination (tingling/mild fever after injection is likely LPS, not BPC-157 action).
- **When:** Acute injury or chronic tendinopathy; stop once healed. Contraindicated in active cancer or high cancer risk (VEGF promotion is the opposite mechanism of anti-cancer drugs like Avastin). Can be synergistic with GHK-copper peptide and thymosin beta-4 (TB-500) when all three are clinically indicated.
- **Ref:** [02:39:38](transcript.md#023938-bpc-157--injury-dosing-frequency)

### Bremelanotide / PT-141 (Melanotan)
- **What:** FDA-approved melanocyte-stimulating hormone analog for hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD). Acts centrally via CNS — distinct from peripheral hormone action. Also FDA-approved for lipodystrophy.
- **How:** Micro-doses via nasal spray (fastest CNS delivery), subcutaneous injection, or troche. Approved for women; used off-label in men.
- **When:** For confirmed HSDD with other hormones in normal range (typically premenopausal women). Relative contraindication: personal or family history of melanoma. Avoid long-term administration without regular skin checks (dermoscopy).
- **Ref:** [02:45:23](transcript.md#024523-uses-for-melanotan)

### Spiritual Health / Mindfulness
- **What:** Attending to purpose, community, and meaning as a pillar of hormone health — framed as a Venn diagram of body, mind, and soul.
- **How:** Prayer, meditation, community involvement, or any practice aligned with individual beliefs. Dr. Gillett does not prescribe a specific religion; atheists and agnostics are equally served by finding what they truly believe in.
- **When:** Daily. Research by Alia Crum on belief effects and Francis Collins on spiritual health supports measurable physiologic benefits: cortisol reduction, immune support, improved patient outcomes.
- **Ref:** [02:48:21](transcript.md#024821-spiritual-health-impact-on-mental--physical-health)

### Caffeine
- **What:** Adenosine antagonist — affects hormones only insofar as it disrupts sleep quality. Direct effects on testosterone/estrogen are negligible.
- **How:** Moderate intake (1–3 mg/kg body weight); delay first dose 90–120 min after waking to avoid adenosine rebound.
- **When:** Limit after 2 pm to protect sleep quality. The harm is entirely mediated through sleep disruption.
- **Ref:** [02:54:18](transcript.md#025418-caffeine--hormones)

### Relationships and Hormone Optimization
- **What:** Time apart from a partner replenishes dopamine, creating novelty-driven attraction upon reunion. Managing the hormonal shifts of new parenthood proactively reduces relationship strain.
- **How:** Intentionally build in separations — trips, independent activities. Plan ahead for the known high-prolactin/low-dopamine/low-testosterone phase around childbirth and breastfeeding (both partners experience prolactin shifts).
- **When:** Ongoing. Awareness of the hormonal arc (dopamine-driven early relationship → prolactin-dominated new-parent phase) significantly reduces severity of the transition.
- **Ref:** [02:19:41](transcript.md#021941-long-term-relationships--effects-on-hormones)
