## Episode Overview

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

Dr. Mark Hyman, physician and world leader in functional medicine, joins Andrew Huberman to discuss a systems-biology framework for diagnosing and treating root causes of disease rather than managing symptoms. Dr. Hyman recounts how mercury poisoning acquired while living in Beijing in his mid-30s caused chronic fatigue syndrome and cognitive collapse — and how conventional medicine's failure to help drove him to develop functional medicine as his life's work. The conversation covers foundational functional medicine principles, the exposome, nutrition and supplementation priorities, food policy and industry lobbying, toxin exposure and detoxification pathways, cutting-edge interventions including peptides, NAD/NMN, and exosomes, and strategies for cancer screening and Alzheimer's prevention. Both speakers consistently emphasize "test, don't guess" and the value of personalized, data-driven health management over population-level averages. Hyman argues that removing impediments — toxins, allergens, ultra-processed food, chronic stress — and adding back ingredients — whole food, micronutrients, clean air and water, movement, rest, connection, and purpose — is sufficient for the body to heal itself in the majority of cases.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Functional medicine treats root causes, not symptoms.** The same root cause can create completely different diseases in different people, and the same disease label can have entirely different root causes in different individuals. A patient with psoriatic arthritis, migraines, pre-diabetes, depression, and IBS had all five conditions resolve in six weeks by addressing gluten sensitivity, vitamin D deficiency, and gut dysbiosis — eliminating a $50,000/year biologic drug. [00:15:06](transcript.md#001506-wholistic-view-of-body-root-causes)

2. **The exposome determines health outcomes more than genetics.** Genes are not destiny — environmental inputs (diet, toxins, stress, sleep, microbiome, infections) turn genes on and off via epigenetics. Health is created by removing impediments (toxins, allergens, ultra-processed food, chronic stress) and adding back ingredients (whole food, micronutrients, clean air and water, movement, rest, connection, purpose). [00:19:48](transcript.md#001948-medicine-research-exposome-impediments-ingredients-for-health-whole-foods)

3. **Starch and sugar — not fat — are the primary drivers of the metabolic crisis.** Excess refined carbohydrates drive insulin resistance, which underlies obesity, type 2 diabetes, heart disease, Alzheimer's (type 3 diabetes of the brain), and many cancers. The most harmful dietary combination is fat plus starch — the signature pattern of ultra-processed food. Seed oils are a secondary concern that becomes more problematic when heated due to oxidized lipids. [00:26:30](transcript.md#002630-seed-oils-starch-sugar-ultra-processed-foods-obesity-rise)

4. **Ultra-processed food now constitutes ~60% of American calories and must be minimized.** The NOVA classification defines ultra-processed foods as industrial formulations made from substances derived from foods, with added preservatives, colorings, emulsifiers, and additives designed to make them hyperpalatable. Their consumption is associated with higher rates of obesity, diabetes, heart disease, depression, dementia, and cancer — partly because they destroy the gut microbiome. [00:26:30](transcript.md#002630-seed-oils-starch-sugar-ultra-processed-foods-obesity-rise)

5. **Core supplements: omega-3s, vitamin D3, multivitamin, magnesium, iodine, methylated B12.** These address endemic deficiencies in the modern world that whole-food diets alone cannot fully correct. Omega-3 dose: 1–2 g EPA/DHA daily. Vitamin D target: 60–80 ng/mL (typically 2,000–4,000 IU/day with K2). Magnesium glycinate for sleep and anxiety; threonate for brain health. Iodized salt, seaweed, or 150–300 mcg iodine supplement for thyroid function. Methylated B12 (methylcobalamin) essential for those with MTHFR variants. [00:46:25](transcript.md#004625-essential-supplements-omega-3s-vitamin-d3-multivitamin-iodine-methylated-b12)

6. **Budget-constrained priorities: real food first, then vitamin D3 and omega-3s.** The cheapest powerful nutrition interventions are eggs, canned sardines/mackerel, frozen vegetables, and dried legumes. A Harvard study found the cost difference between a healthy and unhealthy diet is ~$1.50/day — and ultra-processed food is far more expensive when long-term health costs are factored in. [00:56:54](transcript.md#005654-supplements-traditional-medicine-limited-budget-nutrition)

7. **ApoB and particle-size testing are superior to standard LDL for cardiovascular risk.** Insulin resistance raises ApoB and LDL particle number while shrinking particle size without necessarily raising LDL concentration. Lean mass hyper-responders — fit, athletic people — may see dramatically elevated LDL on ketogenic diets without proportional cardiovascular risk. Track ApoB response to dietary changes over 4–6 weeks; individualize based on metabolic type. [01:54:03](transcript.md#015403-blood-markers-apob-cholesterol-tool-test-dont-guess-individualization)

8. **GLP-1 agonists should be paired with mandatory nutrition counseling and resistance training.** Over 65% of people regain weight when stopping GLP-1 agonists, often in a worse metabolic position due to muscle loss — muscle burns 7× more calories than fat. After 4 years of use, bowel obstruction risk rises ~450% and pancreatitis risk ~900%. Sub-pharmacologic compounded doses (~$20/month) may be adequate with physician guidance and carry fewer side effects. [01:44:21](transcript.md#014421-glp-1-agonists-doses-risks-food-as-medicine-ketogenic-diet)

9. **Cancer screening is far behind available technology.** The Galleri blood test (cell-free DNA from GRAIL) screens 50+ cancers with ~0.5% false-positive rate and ~75% sensitivity — catching cancers 1–3 years before imaging would detect them. 1 in 188 Function Health members tested positive for previously unknown cancer. Whole-body MRI (e.g., Prenuvo) catches aneurysms, tumors, and structural abnormalities early. Costs will drop significantly as technology scales. [02:22:03](transcript.md#022203-cancer-screening-data-personalized-health-alzheimers-disease)

10. **Alzheimer's is multi-causal and may be reversible in early stages.** Root causes include insulin resistance (type 3 diabetes of the brain), heavy metal toxicity, mold exposure, Lyme disease, gut dysbiosis, and nutritional deficiencies — B12/folate deficiency can mimic early dementia and reverse with supplementation. Blood tests for P-tau 217 and amyloid 42-40 ratios enable early detection years before symptoms. Personalized multimodal intervention (Richard Isaacson, Dale Bredesen's ReCODE protocol) addressing all root causes simultaneously can halt or reverse early progression. [02:22:03](transcript.md#022203-cancer-screening-data-personalized-health-alzheimers-disease)

11. **Detoxification is real and can be actively supported.** Support liver phase 1 and 2 detox pathways with: cruciferous vegetables and sulforaphane (upregulates glutathione), NAC 600 mg/day, garlic, cilantro juice (two bunches/day for heavy metal chelation support), high-fiber diet, lipoic acid, methylated B vitamins (B12, folate, B6), and adequate protein. Silica and alginates from seaweed act as binders. For confirmed heavy metal elevation: DMSA (prescription only) is FDA-approved. Reduce exposure first by switching big fish (tuna) to SMASH fish (salmon, mackerel, anchovies, sardines, herring). [02:02:54](transcript.md#020254-mercury-tool-detoxification-sulforaphane-n-acetylcysteine-nac)

12. **NAD declines with age; boosting it supports mitochondria, DNA repair, and metabolic health.** NAD activates sirtuins — longevity-switch enzymes involved in DNA repair (the genome receives ~100,000 hits per day during replication and use). NAD also stimulates mitochondrial biogenesis, improves insulin sensitivity, and induces autophagy. Hyman takes 1,000 mg NMN daily; Huberman uses sublingual NMN. IV NAD is more potent but uncomfortable — must be administered slowly. [02:30:45](transcript.md#023045-longevity-switches-nad-nmn-exosomes-stem-cells)

13. **Exosomes are therapeutic signaling packets released by stem cells.** Delivered IV or locally into joints, exosomes release growth factors, anti-inflammatory signals, and tissue repair molecules. Hyman used IV exosomes after COVID to reverse post-COVID cognitive and mood collapse within hours; also uses them for knee and back injuries. Stem cells are more powerful but require leaving the US for most applications due to FDA regulations. Exosomes can be administered in the US for certain applications. [02:30:45](transcript.md#023045-longevity-switches-nad-nmn-exosomes-stem-cells)

14. **The GRAS loophole and industry lobbying have corrupted the US food supply.** Over 10,000 chemicals are in the US food supply; the majority have never been independently tested for safety. Food companies can self-certify ingredients as "generally recognized as safe" without any FDA review. SNAP spends ~$7 billion/year on sodas; every state waiver attempt to restrict this has been denied due to beverage industry lobbying. The EU requires chemicals to be proven safe before use — the opposite of the US approach. [01:26:03](transcript.md#012603-toxins-food-additives-generally-recognized-as-safe-gras)

15. **Endocrine-disrupting chemicals and diet are collapsing male testosterone and fertility.** Pesticides, plasticizers, and heavy metals documented by Shanna Swan are driving sperm count and testosterone declines. Excess dietary starch and sugar compound this: in men, belly fat from carbohydrate excess converts testosterone to estrogen. Young men should exhaust lifestyle interventions (sleep, diet, exercise, weight management) before considering TRT; TRT without hCG significantly reduces sperm count. [02:04:56](transcript.md#020456-endocrine-disrupting-chemicals-fertility-tool-hormone-panels-heavy-metals)

## Tools & Protocols

### Omega-3 Supplementation
- **What:** EPA + DHA from fish oil or algae-based source
- **How:** 1–2 g EPA+DHA per day; third-party tested for mercury
- **When:** Daily with food
- **Ref:** [00:46:25](transcript.md#004625-essential-supplements-omega-3s-vitamin-d3-multivitamin-iodine-methylated-b12)

### Vitamin D3 with K2
- **What:** Cholecalciferol (D3) co-administered with vitamin K2
- **How:** 2,000–4,000 IU/day; test 25-OH-D and target 60–80 ng/mL; adjust dose accordingly
- **When:** Daily
- **Ref:** [00:46:25](transcript.md#004625-essential-supplements-omega-3s-vitamin-d3-multivitamin-iodine-methylated-b12)

### Magnesium (Form-Specific)
- **What:** Magnesium glycinate for sleep/anxiety; magnesium threonate for brain/cognition; magnesium citrate for bowel function
- **How:** 200–400 mg glycinate in evening; threonate per product label
- **When:** Glycinate — evening; threonate — as directed
- **Ref:** [00:46:25](transcript.md#004625-essential-supplements-omega-3s-vitamin-d3-multivitamin-iodine-methylated-b12)

### Methylated B12 (Methylcobalamin)
- **What:** Methylcobalamin form — NOT cyanocobalamin
- **How:** Include in methylated multivitamin or standalone; especially important for those with MTHFR variants
- **When:** Daily
- **Ref:** [00:46:25](transcript.md#004625-essential-supplements-omega-3s-vitamin-d3-multivitamin-iodine-methylated-b12)

### Iodine Supplementation
- **What:** Iodine for thyroid, breast tissue, immune function
- **How:** Iodized salt or seaweed; or 150–300 mcg supplement if not using iodized salt
- **When:** Daily
- **Ref:** [00:46:25](transcript.md#004625-essential-supplements-omega-3s-vitamin-d3-multivitamin-iodine-methylated-b12)

### Air Quality — HEPA Filtration
- **What:** HEPA + activated carbon air purifier (MERV-13 or higher)
- **How:** HEPA removes particulates; activated carbon removes volatile organic compounds; place in bedroom and main living area
- **When:** Continuous operation
- **Ref:** [01:02:54](transcript.md#010254-air-tool-air-filters-tap-water-filter-tool-health-expense-whole-foods)

### Water Filtration
- **What:** Point-of-use or whole-house filter removing chlorine, fluoride, heavy metals, PFAS
- **How:** Reverse osmosis with remineralization stage; or Berkey whole-house filter
- **When:** Continuous use
- **Ref:** [01:02:54](transcript.md#010254-air-tool-air-filters-tap-water-filter-tool-health-expense-whole-foods)

### Daily Detox Support Stack
- **What:** NAC + sulforaphane (cruciferous vegetables or supplement) + garlic + lipoic acid + methylated B vitamins + adequate protein
- **How:** NAC 600 mg/day; cruciferous vegetables daily; garlic regularly; lipoic acid; B12/folate/B6 from methylated multivitamin; sufficient dietary protein to support phase 2 liver detox (glucuronidation, glutathione conjugation)
- **When:** Daily
- **Ref:** [02:02:54](transcript.md#020254-mercury-tool-detoxification-sulforaphane-n-acetylcysteine-nac)

### Cilantro Juice for Heavy Metal Chelation
- **What:** Fresh cilantro juiced in quantity; adjunct to fiber and binders (silica, alginates from seaweed)
- **How:** Juice 2 bunches of cilantro and drink daily; combine with high-fiber diet
- **When:** Daily during active detox or ongoing when consuming large fish
- **Ref:** [02:11:36](transcript.md#021136-upregulate-detox-pathways-gut-cleanse-tools-cilantro-juice-fiber)

### DMSA Heavy Metal Chelation
- **What:** FDA-approved chelating drug for confirmed heavy metal elevation
- **How:** 250–500 mg; prescription only; physician-supervised; confirm elevation via blood/urine testing before use
- **When:** Only when heavy metals are confirmed elevated; not prophylactic
- **Ref:** [02:11:36](transcript.md#021136-upregulate-detox-pathways-gut-cleanse-tools-cilantro-juice-fiber)

### Comprehensive Hormone Panel — Men
- **What:** Free testosterone, total testosterone, estradiol, DHEA, DHT, SHBG
- **How:** Standard blood draw; via Function Health or physician
- **When:** Annually or when symptomatic; baseline before considering TRT
- **Ref:** [02:04:56](transcript.md#020456-endocrine-disrupting-chemicals-fertility-tool-hormone-panels-heavy-metals)

### Comprehensive Hormone Panel — Women
- **What:** FSH, LH, estradiol, progesterone, testosterone, DHEA sulfate
- **How:** Standard blood draw; timing relative to cycle for premenopausal women
- **When:** Annually
- **Ref:** [02:04:56](transcript.md#020456-endocrine-disrupting-chemicals-fertility-tool-hormone-panels-heavy-metals)

### Heavy Metal Testing
- **What:** Blood or urine testing for mercury, lead, arsenic, cadmium
- **How:** Via Function Health or physician
- **When:** At least once; more frequently with fish-heavy diet
- **Ref:** [02:04:56](transcript.md#020456-endocrine-disrupting-chemicals-fertility-tool-hormone-panels-heavy-metals)

### ApoB Cardiovascular Testing
- **What:** ApoB blood marker (superior to standard LDL for CVD risk); target under 70 mg/dL per current guidelines
- **How:** Via Function Health or physician
- **When:** Baseline; repeat every 4–12 weeks when modifying diet
- **Ref:** [01:54:03](transcript.md#015403-blood-markers-apob-cholesterol-tool-test-dont-guess-individualization)

### Galleri Multi-Cancer Blood Test
- **What:** Cell-free DNA test screening 50+ cancers (GRAIL); ~0.5% false-positive rate, ~75% sensitivity
- **How:** Single blood draw; order via Function Health or physician
- **When:** Annually for adults 40+; earlier with family history or risk factors
- **Ref:** [02:22:03](transcript.md#022203-cancer-screening-data-personalized-health-alzheimers-disease)

### Whole-Body MRI
- **What:** Structural imaging for early detection of tumors, aneurysms, and anatomical abnormalities; no radiation
- **How:** Prenuvo or similar service
- **When:** Every 1–3 years; especially 50+
- **Ref:** [02:22:03](transcript.md#022203-cancer-screening-data-personalized-health-alzheimers-disease)

### NMN / NAD Optimization
- **What:** NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) or NR — NAD precursors; or IV NAD infusion for more potent effect
- **How:** 500–1,000 mg NMN orally or sublingually daily (Hyman takes 1,000 mg; Huberman uses sublingual NMN); IV NAD administered slowly by physician
- **When:** Daily, morning
- **Ref:** [02:30:45](transcript.md#023045-longevity-switches-nad-nmn-exosomes-stem-cells)

### BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)
- **What:** Peptide promoting angiogenesis and tissue regeneration; useful for athletic recovery
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection or oral; physician guidance; cancer screening recommended before use due to angiogenesis concern with existing tumors
- **When:** Cycling — not continuous; especially useful in athletic recovery phases
- **Ref:** [02:17:08](transcript.md#021708-peptides-pt-141-vyleesi-bpc-157-thymosin-alpha-1-risks-cycling)

### Thymosin Alpha-1
- **What:** Immune-supporting peptide; reactivates thymic T-cell maturation function
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection; physician guidance
- **When:** Short courses for frequent illness, post-COVID/long COVID, or immune dysfunction
- **Ref:** [02:17:08](transcript.md#021708-peptides-pt-141-vyleesi-bpc-157-thymosin-alpha-1-risks-cycling)

### PT-141 (Bremelanotide / Vyleesi)
- **What:** Melanocortin receptor agonist; FDA-approved for hypoactive sexual desire disorder in women; used off-label for men
- **How:** Start with low dose to manage nausea side effect; physician guidance
- **When:** As needed; not for daily use
- **Ref:** [02:17:08](transcript.md#021708-peptides-pt-141-vyleesi-bpc-157-thymosin-alpha-1-risks-cycling)
