# Episode 17: How to Control Your Metabolism by Thyroid & Growth Hormone

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Andrew Huberman closes out the podcast's month-long hormone series by focusing on thyroid hormone and growth hormone — the two systems he argues are most significant for setting overall metabolic rate. He explains the shared hypothalamus–pituitary logic governing both hormones, then details the three essential dietary inputs for thyroid synthesis (iodine, L-tyrosine, selenium), the hidden risks of clean-eating diets and ketogenic approaches on thyroid function, and the anti-inflammatory benefits of adequate iodine. For growth hormone, he walks through every lever in order of accessibility: sleep quality and timing, exercise protocol design (including sex-dependent differences in GH and IGF-1 release), arginine and L-citrulline supplementation, deliberate hyperthermia via sauna (up to 16-fold GH increases), and a candid overview of prescription peptide secretagogues such as sermorelin and ipamorelin. Throughout, the episode stresses that metabolism governs not just body composition but brain function, tissue repair, and injury recovery across the entire lifespan — and that the age-related 2–3× decline in GH between ages 30–40 can be largely offset through behavioral tools alone.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Metabolism means growth, repair, and energy use — not just fat burning** — the brain alone accounts for ~75% of resting metabolic needs; thyroid and growth hormone are the two master controllers of this entire system across every tissue. [00:13:30](transcript.md#001330-metabolism-101-your-brain-the-furnace)

2. **The hypothalamus–pituitary axis governs both thyroid and GH** — TRH and GHRH from the hypothalamus signal the pituitary to secrete TSH and GH respectively; the same releasing-then-stimulating cascade applies here as in testosterone and estrogen. [00:17:33](transcript.md#001733-releasing-hormones-from-your-brain-stimulating-hormones-from-your-pituitary)

3. **Three nutrients are the essential building blocks of thyroid hormone** — iodine, L-tyrosine, and selenium must all be present in sufficient quantities for the thyroid to produce T3 (active) and T4; thyroid then increases glucose uptake by muscle, bone, and brain and drives fat liberation from white adipose tissue. [00:22:45](transcript.md#002245-iodine-l-tyrosine--selenium-the-trio-essential-for-thyroid-function)

4. **Selenium is the most commonly underconsumed thyroid nutrient** — target range is ~100–200 mcg/day; six to eight Brazil nuts supply ~550 mcg, far exceeding what mushrooms, spinach, lentils, or dairy deliver per serving; excess selenium is also possible so don't overdo Brazil nuts. [00:28:09](transcript.md#002809-selenium-for-thyroid-brazil-nuts--other-valuable-sources)

5. **Selenium has significant benefits beyond thyroid** — three studies show reduced preeclampsia risk; evidence for reduced prostate cancer risk; likely reduces acne via thyroid–liver pathway. Particularly important during pregnancy (check prenatal formulas). [00:33:05](transcript.md#003305-selenium-for-pregnancy-prostate-cancer-risk-acne)

6. **"Clean" diets can inadvertently suppress thyroid via iodine depletion** — cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cabbage, etc.) contain glucosinolates that interfere with thyroid function and increase iodine requirements; both all-meat and plant-heavy diets are at risk; ocean air provides minor iodine exposure for coastal residents. [00:35:20](transcript.md#003520-clean-eating-downsides-cruciferous-vegetables-leeching-iodine)

7. **Iodine reduces systemic inflammation** — adequate iodine is associated with reduced c-reactive protein (CRP) and reduced circulating IL-6, linking thyroid support to cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory pathways. [00:39:00](transcript.md#003900-other-benefits-of-iodine-reducing-inflammation)

8. **Ketogenic diets can depress T3 and set up rebound weight gain** — ~1/3 of 22 studies show keto significantly reduces T3 and T4; reintroducing carbohydrates rapidly without gradual transition can cause fast fat regain because the down-regulated thyroid cannot keep up with the sudden metabolic load. [00:45:33](transcript.md#004533-ketogenic-diet--its-effects-on-thyroid-rebound-weight-gain)

9. **GH is released during slow-wave (delta wave) sleep — not on a fixed clock** — sleep deprivation research confirmed GH release is gated by delta brainwave activity, not circadian timing alone; this means delta-inducing waking behaviors (meditation, exercise) also raise GH outside sleep. [00:55:43](transcript.md#005543-delta-wave-brain-activity-is-the-trigger-for-growth-hormone-release)

10. **Eating within 2 hours of sleep blunts the primary nightly GH pulse** — elevated blood glucose suppresses GH release; caloric sports drinks during exercise also flatline GH levels; if hunger disrupts sleep, choose low-glycemic food early enough for glucose to clear before bed. [00:54:49](transcript.md#005449-not-eating-within-2hrs-of-sleep-keep-blood-glucose-lowish-at-sleep)

11. **20 minutes of standard sitting meditation may access delta waves and raise GH** — per Goleman & Davidson's *Altered Traits*, sitting meditation (not yoga nidra or hypnosis) produces slow-wave-sleep-like brainwaves in waking; binaural beats lack quality independent peer-review evidence. [01:01:00](transcript.md#010100-book-altered-traits-binaural-beats-delta-waves-access)

12. **GH declines 2–3× between ages 30–40 — but behavioral tools can fully offset this** — the age-related GH decline is steeper and more universal than testosterone decline; exercise-induced 300–500% GH increases can mathematically offset a 2–3× decline; exogenous GH injections shut down endogenous production. [01:23:09](transcript.md#012309-growth-hormone-changes-across-the-lifespan-no-one-escapes)

13. **Exercise protocol determines GH output — warmup, duration, intensity, and cool-down all matter** — warm body first (~10 min), train 60–75 min at moderate-to-high intensity (not to absolute failure), keep blood glucose low during exercise, actively cool down afterward to preserve the secondary overnight GH pulse (300–500% increases both during and the following night). [01:04:35](transcript.md#010435-specific-types--duration-of-exercise-that-stimulate-growth-hormone--warmups)

14. **Sex-dependent differences exist in when GH and IGF-1 peak during exercise** — women reach maximum GH/IGF-1 in the first 30 min; men must continue to ~60 min; IGF-1 from the liver is responsible for much of the memory and cognition improvements from exercise (Pierce et al., *Frontiers in Endocrinology*, 2020). [01:10:36](transcript.md#011036-sex-differences-for-when-during-exercise-growth-hormone-and-igf-1-release-occurs-males-have-to-last-longer)

15. **Arginine + exercise do not stack for GH — choose one or the other** — GH response is clamped at 300–500% whether you take arginine alone, exercise alone, or both together; use arginine before sleep on rest days; L-citrulline converts to arginine more efficiently, has stronger evidence (double-blind studies), fewer GI side effects, and reduces CRP; both lower blood pressure — caution with heart conditions. [01:14:10](transcript.md#011410-supplements-that-increase-growth-hormone-100-400-or-more-arginine-ornithine)

16. **Sauna cycle protocol raises GH up to 16-fold and lowers cortisol** — 20 min sauna → 30 min cool-down → repeat; effect compounds over 3 consecutive days; Podstawski et al. (2021) confirmed GH increase, cortisol decrease, no direct effects on testosterone, DHEA, or prolactin; no cold plunge immediately after sauna; do not sauna close to bedtime. [01:29:20](transcript.md#012920-specific-heat-protocols-for-increasing-growth-hormone-up-to-16-fold)

## Tools & Protocols

### Iodine Intake
- **What:** Essential building block for T3 and T4; also reduces CRP and IL-6 (anti-inflammatory benefit).
- **How:** Iodized table salt, seaweed (kelp, nori), seafood, dairy; kelp tablets if diet is restricted. Coastal ocean air provides minor topical iodine. Most people get enough unless following extreme clean-eating diets.
- **When:** Daily — consistent dietary intake is the goal. Monitor if following an all-meat or heavily plant-based diet high in cruciferous vegetables.
- **Ref:** [00:26:05](transcript.md#002605-how-much-iodine-do-we-need-by-food-supplement-or-ocean-air)

### Selenium for Thyroid
- **What:** Required for iodine and L-tyrosine to interact properly in T3/T4 synthesis. Additional benefits: reduced preeclampsia risk, reduced prostate cancer risk, reduced acne.
- **How:** 6–8 Brazil nuts/day (~550 mcg selenium). Alternatively selenomethionine supplement targeting ~100–200 mcg/day (check country-specific guidelines). Children under 14 need only 30–40 mcg/day. Do not megadose — excess is possible.
- **When:** Daily. Critical during pregnancy; talk to a physician and check prenatal formulas.
- **Ref:** [00:28:09](transcript.md#002809-selenium-for-thyroid-brazil-nuts--other-valuable-sources)

### L-Tyrosine
- **What:** Amino acid precursor to both thyroid hormones (T3, T4) and catecholamines (dopamine, norepinephrine).
- **How:** Meat, eggs, dairy, nuts. Adequate dietary protein generally covers thyroid needs. Supplement (500 mg–2 g) more relevant for acute focus/alertness than for thyroid optimization.
- **When:** Daily via diet.
- **Ref:** [00:22:45](transcript.md#002245-iodine-l-tyrosine--selenium-the-trio-essential-for-thyroid-function)

### Fasting Before Sleep for GH
- **What:** Keeping blood glucose low at sleep onset preserves the primary nightly GH pulse during slow-wave sleep.
- **How:** Stop eating at least 2 hours before bed. Avoid carbohydrate-heavy evening snacks. Water and non-caloric beverages are fine. If hunger disrupts sleep, choose a small, low-glycemic option well before the 2-hour cutoff.
- **When:** Every night as a baseline habit.
- **Ref:** [00:54:49](transcript.md#005449-not-eating-within-2hrs-of-sleep-keep-blood-glucose-lowish-at-sleep)

### Low-Dose Melatonin for GH
- **What:** Very low-dose melatonin shifts early-night sleep toward more delta/slow-wave activity, amplifying the first GH pulse. Standard over-the-counter doses (1–12 mg) are super-physiological and can negatively interact with reproductive hormones.
- **How:** ~0.5 mg (500 mcg) taken 30–60 min before sleep. Huberman personally disfavors routine melatonin use but acknowledges this specific low-dose application has supporting data.
- **When:** Nightly if desired; especially useful for those with shallow early-night sleep.
- **Ref:** [00:58:25](transcript.md#005825-low-doses-of-melatonin-supplementation-for-increasing-gh-release)

### Meditation for Delta Waves and GH
- **What:** 20 minutes of sitting meditation can produce delta-wave-like brainwave states in waking, potentially triggering GH release outside of sleep.
- **How:** Standard sitting meditation focused on breath. Yoga nidra and hypnosis are not confirmed to produce delta waves. Binaural beats: no quality independent peer-reviewed evidence.
- **When:** Daily practice; timing flexible.
- **Ref:** [01:01:00](transcript.md#010100-book-altered-traits-binaural-beats-delta-waves-access)

### Exercise Protocol for GH
- **What:** Structured exercise to maximize 300–500% GH increases both during the session and the following night.
- **How:** (1) Warm up ~10 min to physically elevate body temperature. (2) Train 60–75 min moderate-to-high intensity (resistance or endurance) — do not go to absolute failure. (3) Avoid caloric sports drinks during exercise (flatlines GH). (4) Cool down actively after training (cool shower, ice pack, cool room) to trigger the secondary overnight GH pulse. Women: biggest GH/IGF-1 peak in first 30 min. Men: peak at ~60 min — do not stop early.
- **When:** Any time of day; earlier timing avoids interference with nighttime GH release.
- **Ref:** [01:04:35](transcript.md#010435-specific-types--duration-of-exercise-that-stimulate-growth-hormone--warmups)

### Arginine / L-Citrulline for GH
- **What:** Amino acid pathway that increases GH 100–600% above baseline when blood glucose is low; also causes vasodilation and lowers blood pressure.
- **How:** Oral arginine 3–9 g on an empty stomach — do not exceed 9 g (GH is blunted above this threshold). L-citrulline converts to arginine more efficiently, has double-blind study support (males), fewer GI side effects, reduces CRP, and may be the superior route. Both lower blood pressure — caution required for those with heart conditions. Do NOT combine with intense exercise: GH is clamped at the same ceiling regardless.
- **When:** Before sleep on low-carb evenings, or on rest days. Not as pre-workout on high-intensity training days.
- **Ref:** [01:14:10](transcript.md#011410-supplements-that-increase-growth-hormone-100-400-or-more-arginine-ornithine)

### Sauna / Deliberate Hyperthermia for GH
- **What:** Repeated sauna cycles raise GH up to 16-fold and decrease cortisol; no direct effects on testosterone, DHEA, or prolactin (Podstawski et al., 2021).
- **How:** Sauna at 176–210°F (80–99°C). Protocol: 20 min sauna → 30 min cool-down → 20 min sauna again; effect compounds over 3 consecutive days. No sauna access: wrestler-style plastic suits over sweats, or steam-filled hotel bathroom with hoodie and sweatpants → cool shower → repeat. Do NOT immediately follow with cold plunge (may blunt GH). Do NOT sauna close to bedtime (disrupts sleep).
- **When:** Dedicated days with full cool-down before sleep. Begin cautiously — hyperthermia is dangerous; physician clearance recommended.
- **Ref:** [01:29:20](transcript.md#012920-specific-heat-protocols-for-increasing-growth-hormone-up-to-16-fold)

### Peptide Secretagogues (Overview)
- **What:** Synthetic peptides mimicking GHRH or ghrelin to stimulate endogenous GH release rather than replacing it with exogenous GH. Preserve pulsatile release pattern; lower immediate shutdown risk than exogenous GH, but sustained use can alter gene expression for GH production long-term.
- **How:** Sermorelin, ipamorelin, tesamorelin (injectable, typically pre-sleep on empty stomach); MK-677/ibutamoren (oral). Ghrelin-related gastric peptides also used for wound healing and tissue repair. Dosing requires physician guidance.
- **When:** Prescription only in the US. Used under medical supervision or off-label (longevity, entertainment industry). Prominent in Olympics/performance contexts per Huberman's observation.
- **Ref:** [01:36:00](transcript.md#013600-prescription-growth-hormone--emerging-peptides-therapeutics-secretagogues-etc)
