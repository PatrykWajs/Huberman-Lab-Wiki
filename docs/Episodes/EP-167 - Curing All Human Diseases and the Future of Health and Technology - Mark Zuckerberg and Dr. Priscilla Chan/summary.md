# Episode 167: Curing All Human Diseases & the Future of Health & Technology | Mark Zuckerberg & Dr. Priscilla Chan

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Andrew Huberman hosts Mark Zuckerberg (CEO of Meta) and Dr. Priscilla Chan (M.D., pediatrician and co-CEO of the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative) in a two-part conversation. The first hour covers CZI's mission to cure, prevent, or manage all human diseases by 2100 — through funding basic science, building open-source tools (CELLxGENE, Napari), applying transformer-based AI to single-cell biology, and running cross-institutional Biohub institutes in San Francisco, Chicago, and New York. Priscilla shares her family's origin story as Chinese-Vietnamese boat refugees, explaining how that history of radical optimism underpins the CZI's ambition. The second hour, with Zuckerberg alone, covers Meta's technology ecosystem: social media algorithms and mental health, parental controls on Instagram (supervised accounts, restrict function), the Quest 3 VR/mixed reality headset and its fitness and creativity applications, Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses with AI assistant and privacy light, and the coming wave of AI creator assistants — including AI personas played by figures like Snoop Dogg as a stepping stone toward creator-controlled AI assistants.

## Key Takeaways

1. **CZI's mission is to cure, prevent, or manage all human diseases by 2100:** [00:05:35](transcript.md#000535-chan-zuckerberg-initiative-czi--human-disease-research)
   Launched in 2015, CZI is not targeting individual diseases but investing in underlying infrastructure — funding great scientists, building open-source tools, and running Biohub institutes that tackle grand challenges across disciplinary boundaries. The key insight is that disease-agnostic basic science produces the most durable breakthroughs.

2. **Most large scientific discoveries follow the invention of a new tool:** [00:08:51](transcript.md#000851-innovation--discovery-science--engineering)
   Zuckerberg draws an analogy to software engineering: you cannot debug a codebase without stepping through it line by line. CZI's first decade is therefore dedicated to building observation and measurement tools for biology — microscopes, imaging, single-cell sequencing — before expecting cures. The next 10 years should be primarily about being able to measure and observe more things in human biology.

3. **CZI funds science differently from NIH by incentivizing collaboration, open science, and pre-prints:** [00:12:53](transcript.md#001253-funding-building-tools--imaging)
   CZI grants require grantees to publish pre-prints, collaborate across disciplines, and share tools openly. Funding is a small fraction of NIH's but fills gaps NIH cannot: incentivizing new points of view and building durable, shareable software (e.g., Napari for image annotation, CELLxGENE for single-cell data) that individual labs cannot maintain. CZI also owns an electron microscope at its imaging institute, with researchers developing a mini-phase plate to increase electron alignment and resolution.

4. **Single-cell RNA sequencing is producing near-complete cell atlases for humans, mice, and flies:** [00:17:57](transcript.md#001757-healthy-vs-diseased-cells-human-cell-atlas--ai-virtual-cells)
   Every cell reads DNA differently via mRNA. CZI has funded single-cell methods since 2017, producing nearly complete atlases of how cells behave when healthy versus diseased. The body has an estimated 37 trillion cells, each with large sets of mRNA, making AI essential for extracting insight. Chan uses a cake-with-a-typo analogy: science knows the mutation (typo) and the bad outcome (bad cake), but CZI is filling in everything that happens in between — how the chef interprets the typo, what happens in the oven.

5. **CELLxGENE and the ionocyte discovery show how open data sharing changes disease understanding:** [00:21:59](transcript.md#002159-single-cell-methods--disease-cellxgene-tool)
   CELLxGENE lets scientists input a gene of interest and receive a heat map of which cell types express it, plus the most relevant papers — enabling cross-organ hypothesis generation and surfacing unexpected side-effect risks for drug development. Its application to cystic fibrosis revealed the ionocyte — an entirely new cell type in the lung directly implicated in CF — upending the established disease model taught in medical school. We do not yet know all human cell types; new ones continue to be discovered (e.g., 18+ fat cell subtypes via single-cell sequencing).

6. **CZI is building one of the largest non-profit life-sciences AI clusters (~1,000 GPUs) to train a virtual cell:** [00:29:53](transcript.md#002953-ai--hypothesis-generation-long-term-projects--collaboration)
   By training a transformer-based model on Human Cell Atlas data and other inputs, CZI aims to build a virtual cell — a computational model that can predict cell states, simulate drug interactions, and generate millions of hypotheses in silico. LLM hallucinations are reframed as a feature: they generate possible states that scientists then validate experimentally, compressing the hypothesis-to-experiment cycle. The transformer architecture has not yet shown an asymptote in what it can learn from more data, though whether current architectures are sufficient to reach human-level AI is an open question in the field.

7. **CZI Biohubs in San Francisco, Chicago, and New York each tackle distinct grand challenges:** [00:42:11](transcript.md#004211-czi-biohubs-chicago-new-york)
   All biohubs require at least three collaborating institutions and an interdisciplinary team. SF Biohub (Stanford/UCSF/UC Berkeley) focuses on infectious disease and single-cell mapping. Chicago Biohub (University of Chicago/Northwestern/UIUC) is engineering skin tissue embedded with ultra-thin sensors to read what cells secrete and how they signal during inflammation — inflammation drives roughly 50% of all deaths — and studying the neuromuscular junction relevant to ALS and age-related falls. New York Biohub (Columbia/Rockefeller/NYU/Cornell) is engineering immune cells to navigate the body and report on internal conditions (a "Fantastic Voyage" approach), targeting immune-privileged sites like ovaries, pancreas, and the nervous system for cancer and neurodegeneration.

8. **CZI's Rare As One portfolio funds patient-driven rare disease research and drug development:** [00:50:52](transcript.md#005052-universities--biohubs-therapeutics--rare-diseases)
   Over 7,000 rare diseases collectively affect many people. CZI funds patient advocates to build disease organizations, bioregistries, and natural history datasets, then partners them with researchers and drug developers. Rare diseases are scientifically valuable because mutated genes reveal how normal biology works. CZI does not itself pursue therapeutics — it generates knowledge and tools, and biotech companies take those targets forward; some SF Biohub teams have also spun out startups.

9. **Social media's effect on mental health is content- and context-dependent, not simply a function of time on platform:** [01:13:17](transcript.md#011317-algorithms-clickbait-news-individual-experience)
   Zuckerberg argues passive, low-quality consumption correlates with worse outcomes while active social connection correlates with positive ones. Meta has moved away from clickbait by tracking long-term satisfaction signals — not just clicks — mapping algorithms to ground truth from real people evaluating which stories were most meaningful to them. He cautions against paternalism: giving people latitude to like what they like, within clear limits (no bullying, no incitement to violence).

10. **Meta's teen safety tools on Instagram are comprehensive but largely unknown to parents:** [01:19:17](transcript.md#011917-parental-controls-meta-social-media-tools--tailoring-experience)
    Supervised accounts let parents approve followers, see who teens message (but not message content), set daily time limits, restrict content categories, and enable/disable features. Anyone under 16 defaults to a private experience. Huberman notes he was surprised by the breadth of tools after a Meta team briefing; awareness — not tool availability — is the main gap. The restrict function (hides comments without notifying the poster) is highlighted as more nuanced than the blunt block function. Zuckerberg recommends in-product contextual education, not just onboarding lists, as the primary delivery mechanism.

11. **Quest 3 mixed reality is a genuine step change — physical fitness is a core use case, and creative applications are emerging:** [01:28:55](transcript.md#012855-virtual-reality-vr-mixed-reality-experiences--smart-glasses)
    Quest 3's full-color passthrough cameras overlay virtual content on the physical room; users can see furniture, people, and objects while engaging with virtual experiences. Huberman tried a boxing app that produced genuine cardiovascular exertion without vestibular disruption. A piano learning demo overlays highlighted keys on a real or virtual keyboard to teach songs in real time — with implications for democratizing access to expensive instruments. Zuckerberg sees the headset as "a computer for your whole body." Kickboxing and grappling remain technically harder due to leg tracking and haptic resistance challenges.

12. **Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses are functional AI companions with live streaming, audio AI, and a hardware privacy light:** [01:49:31](transcript.md#014931-ray-ban-meta-smart-glasses-potential-privacy--risks)
    Current $299 glasses include a camera (twice as good as v1), speakers, microphones, and the "Hey Meta" AI assistant. The AI can answer questions about what the camera sees, take calls, play music, and live-stream to a family group. A white pulsing privacy light activates whenever the camera is recording — more disclosure than a phone. Huberman notes glasses could also address the myopia epidemic by measuring near vs. far viewing distance and could capture attentional and autonomic data (pupil size, visual attention) inaccessible to a phone. A first full holographic AR version is in internal testing and planned for public release within a few years.

13. **AI creator assistants are coming — identity protection and representational fidelity are the key design challenges:** [02:06:42](transcript.md#020642-ai-assistants--creators-identity-protection)
    Meta plans to allow creators to build AI assistants of themselves to respond to follower DMs at scale. The prerequisite is high confidence the AI represents the creator accurately — current LLMs are not yet reliably predictable enough. As a stepping stone, Meta launched AI personas (chef, fitness, travel, DIY, dungeon master) played by well-known figures (e.g., Snoop Dogg as dungeon master) that are fictional characters, not impersonations. Impersonation has been against Meta platform policy since the company's founding. Zuckerberg expects creator AI assistant tools to be available in a meaningful form by the following year.

## Tools & Protocols

### Single-Cell RNA Sequencing (scRNA-seq)

- **What:** A laboratory method that reads the mRNA expression profile of individual cells, revealing which genes each cell is actively using at a given moment.
- **How:** Cells are dissociated, barcoded, and their RNA is sequenced at scale. Computational tools then cluster cells by expression profile to identify cell types and states under healthy and diseased conditions.
- **When:** Used to build cell atlases and to identify cell-type-specific disease mechanisms. CZI has funded this work since 2017 and the field has produced nearly complete atlases of humans, mice, and flies.
- **Ref:** [00:17:57](transcript.md#001757-healthy-vs-diseased-cells-human-cell-atlas--ai-virtual-cells)

### CELLxGENE (CZI Open-Source Tool)

- **What:** A browser-based open-source tool for exploring, annotating, and contributing to single-cell gene expression datasets across species and tissues, built and maintained by CZI engineers.
- **How:** Scientists upload single-cell datasets; others query, filter, and analyze them. Input a gene of interest and receive a heat map of expression across all cell types, plus relevant papers. The tool aggregates data into a growing public corpus usable for AI model training.
- **When:** Free to use at cellxgene.cziscience.com. Essential for anyone doing single-cell analysis who wants to compare their data to a global reference or identify unexpected cross-organ expression patterns.
- **Ref:** [00:21:59](transcript.md#002159-single-cell-methods--disease-cellxgene-tool)

### Virtual Cell Modeling (In Silico Experiments)

- **What:** A computational model of a human cell trained on large single-cell datasets that can simulate biological processes and predict how a cell will respond to perturbations, drug interactions, or disease states.
- **How:** Transformer-based LLM architectures are trained on Human Cell Atlas gene expression data. The resulting model can be queried to predict cell behavior, generate hypotheses, and prioritize experiments. CZI is building one of the largest non-profit life-sciences AI clusters (~1,000 GPUs) for this work. LLM hallucinations function as hypothesis generators; scientists then validate outputs in the lab.
- **When:** Still in development. Intended to compress the hypothesis-to-experiment cycle from years to days. In silico experiments must still be validated in vitro and in vivo before clinical translation.
- **Ref:** [00:29:53](transcript.md#002953-ai--hypothesis-generation-long-term-projects--collaboration)

### CZI Biohub Network

- **What:** A network of cross-institutional research institutes — San Francisco, Chicago, New York — each tackling a grand challenge in biology requiring collaboration across multiple universities and disciplines.
- **How:** Each biohub requires at least three collaborating institutions and an interdisciplinary team. Biohub staff (~50 per hub) are independent of the universities; faculty at partner universities also contribute. CZI funds 10-15 year projects with hundreds of millions of dollars. Proposals are solicited via competitive RFP; 57 proposals from 150+ institutions were received for the second round.
- **When:** Ongoing. SF Biohub launched ~2016; Chicago and New York followed.
- **Ref:** [00:42:11](transcript.md#004211-czi-biohubs-chicago-new-york)

### Meta Supervised Accounts (Teen Safety on Instagram)

- **What:** Parental control system on Instagram allowing parents to approve followers, restrict content, monitor messaging contacts, and set daily time limits for teen accounts.
- **How:** Parent links their account to teen's account via Family Center in the Instagram app. Parents can approve or deny all new followers, see who the teen messages (not content), enable/disable content categories, and set a hard daily time cap. Anyone under 16 defaults to a private account.
- **When:** Active now on Instagram. Awareness is the main gap — tools exist but most parents do not know about them.
- **Ref:** [01:19:17](transcript.md#011917-parental-controls-meta-social-media-tools--tailoring-experience)

### Quest 3 Mixed Reality Headset

- **What:** Meta's VR/MR headset featuring full-color passthrough cameras that overlay virtual content on the real physical environment, enabling both fully immersive VR and augmented reality experiences.
- **How:** Worn on the head; cameras show the real room in high fidelity with virtual objects rendered on top. Controllers or hand tracking allow interaction. Hand tracking is advanced enough to support virtual keyboard typing at ~100 words per minute. Body position is estimated by fusing camera data with accelerometer readings.
- **When:** Available now. Key use cases include fitness (boxing, cardio, dancing), virtual workspaces, creative tools (virtual piano learning with key highlighting), and social/gaming experiences.
- **Ref:** [01:28:55](transcript.md#012855-virtual-reality-vr-mixed-reality-experiences--smart-glasses)

### Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses

- **What:** Consumer eyeglasses with built-in speakers, microphones, camera, live-streaming capability, and AI assistant ("Hey Meta") — styled identically to standard Ray-Ban frames. Current price: $299.
- **How:** Worn like normal glasses. Speaker delivers audio privately to the wearer. Voice commands trigger AI assistant for music, calls, questions, and live streaming. Camera can capture photos/video and the AI can analyze what it sees in real time. A white pulsing privacy light activates whenever the camera sensor is recording.
- **When:** Available now. Best used as a lightweight always-on AI companion and audio device. A first version with holographic display is in internal testing; a public AR version planned within a few years at a higher price point.
- **Ref:** [01:49:31](transcript.md#014931-ray-ban-meta-smart-glasses-potential-privacy--risks)
