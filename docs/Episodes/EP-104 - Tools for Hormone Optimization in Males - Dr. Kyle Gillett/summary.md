# Episode 104: Tools for Hormone Optimization in Males | Dr. Kyle Gillett

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Kyle Gillett, a dual board-certified physician in family medicine and obesity medicine, joins Andrew Huberman for a comprehensive, age-stratified guide to male hormone optimization covering every stage of life from the first puberty in infancy through geriatric years. The conversation establishes a clear hierarchy of intervention — from six behavioral lifestyle pillars through supplementation to peptides and prescription hormone therapies — with specific dosages, blood markers to track, and actionable protocols at each tier. Special attention is given to the interplay between testosterone, free testosterone, DHT, estrogen, growth hormone, and thyroid hormone, as well as subjective calibration tools like the ADAM questionnaire and the dopamine wave pool framework for understanding libido and motivation. Practical protocols are presented for managing fertility, scrotal temperature, pelvic floor health, hair loss, and prostate health, alongside a rapid-fire Q&A covering the effects of cannabis, nicotine, alcohol, cycling, and dietary fat on hormones. The episode closes with important guidance on environmental endocrine disruptors — BPA, phthalates, and microplastics — and the research of Dr. Shanna Swan on their impact on male reproductive health.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Two puberties exist** — the first occurs in the first three months of life (driven by DHEA from the adrenals), and the second is the familiar puberty driven by gonadotropins. Childhood obesity accelerates the second puberty via leptin signaling to the hypothalamus, which can shorten stature by closing growth plates early. [00:15:14](transcript.md#001514-first-vs-second-puberty)

2. **Dirty bulking stunts height** — deliberately overeating to gain fat during puberty elevates leptin, triggers early gonadotropin release, and closes growth plates prematurely. Resistance training alone does not stunt growth; excess adiposity does. [00:07:43](transcript.md#000743-puberty-height-resistance-training-childhood-obesity)

3. **Blood work is required to personalize hormone optimization** — essential markers include total testosterone, free testosterone or SHBG, estradiol (sensitive assay), LH, FSH, DHEA-S, prolactin, IGF-1, TSH, free T3, free T4, PSA (men 40+), CBC, CMP, lipids, and vitamin D. Morning draw; ideally first baseline at age 18, every 6 months thereafter. [00:17:17](transcript.md#001717-hormone-optimization--blood-work)

4. **The six lifestyle pillars form the non-negotiable foundation** — diet, exercise, sleep, stress management, sunlight and outdoor movement, and purpose (self-actualization per Maslow's hierarchy). All must be optimized before supplementation or prescription therapy. [00:22:14](transcript.md#002214-diet-exercise-sleep--hormones)

5. **The ADAM questionnaire is a validated subjective screen** for androgen deficiency — covers libido, energy, strength, mood, and morning erections. Clinical motivational interviewing often reveals symptoms the questionnaire misses. Erectile dysfunction is "the canary in the coal mine" for cardiovascular disease. [00:34:08](transcript.md#003408-determining-individual-hormone-levels-adam-questionnaire)

6. **Daily or multiple-daily masturbation to ejaculation suppresses hormones** — the prolactin spike post-ejaculation inhibits pituitary LH and FSH release, lowering testosterone. Pornography compounds the effect via the dopamine wave pool: a very high dopamine peak deepens the trough, blunting sensitivity to real-world sexual stimuli. [00:40:35](transcript.md#004035-libido-masturbation-pornography--the-dopamine-wave-pool)

7. **Vigorous exercise beyond one hour suppresses hormones** — 3-4 sessions/week of hard training is sustainable; sessions regularly exceeding one hour raise cortisol and suppress the HPG axis. Combining a caloric deficit with excessive training is synergistically harmful. Consistency at 80-90% beats perfection half the time. [00:49:46](transcript.md#004946-sustainable-exercise-regimen-for-hormone-health)

8. **Creatine raises DHT via 5-alpha reductase upregulation** and may accelerate hair loss in genetically susceptible individuals, but the effect normalizes rather than supraphysiologizes DHT. Betaine (1-3 g/day) is an alternative without the DHT effect. L-carnitine (1-5 g/day oral) increases androgen receptor density. Taking allicin (garlic, ~600 mg) with L-carnitine offsets TMAO conversion risk at high doses. [01:01:02](transcript.md#010102-supplementation-creatine--hair-loss-betaine-l-carnitine--allicin-garlic)

9. **Tongkat ali (300-1,200 mg/day) increases free testosterone** by upregulating steroidogenesis enzymes and, in those with elevated SHBG, by reducing SHBG. It also modestly raises DHEA and LH. Most potent during low-insulin or caloric deficit states. No cycling required. [01:16:39](transcript.md#011639-tongkat-ali-eurycoma-longifolia-longjack--steroid-pathways)

10. **Fadogia agrestis increases LH and testosterone dose-dependently** — the safe human-equivalent dose from rat data is 300 mg/day. Practical protocol: 600 mg every other day (or Mon/Wed/Fri). Aggressive protocol: 600 mg daily for one month, then 1-2 weeks off — only with GGT and alkaline phosphatase monitoring. Cycling is recommended; continuous high-dose use risks liver and testicular markers. [01:22:09](transcript.md#012209-fadogia-agrestis--testosterone)

11. **Growth hormone is best optimized behaviorally** — the largest GH pulse occurs during sleep; avoiding food 2 hours before bed prevents insulin-mediated blunting of that pulse. Fasting increases GH but reduces receptor sensitivity downstream. GHRH analogs (tesamorelin, sermorelin) and ghrelin agonists (ibutamoren/MK-677) can augment GH but carry appetite stimulation, blood glucose elevation, and LPS contamination risks when sourced outside licensed compounding pharmacies. [01:26:32](transcript.md#012632-optimize-growth-hormone--igf-1-diet-fasting-supplements--exercise)

12. **TRT at physiologic doses (100-120 mg testosterone cypionate/week, split 2-3x) is appropriate only when clinically indicated** — rarely justified in men in their 20s-30s with normal blood levels. Requires monitoring of hematocrit, PSA, estradiol, blood pressure, lipids, acne, and mood. Pair with HCG (500-1,500 IU, 2-3x/week) to preserve fertility and testicular volume. [01:42:06](transcript.md#014206-testosterone-therapy)

13. **Clomiphene and HCG are intermediate options before TRT** — enclomiphene (the better-tolerated isomer of clomiphene) raises endogenous LH/FSH and testosterone without suppressing spermatogenesis. HCG is an LH analog that also binds the TSH receptor, increasing thyroid hormone demand ~30-40%. Clomiphene has broad pharmacodynamic side effects (including visual changes) and is not ideal for most men long-term. [01:47:03](transcript.md#014703-prescriptions--hormones-human-choriogonadotropin-hcg-clomiphene)

14. **Low-dose tadalafil (2.5-5 mg/day) benefits prostate health, reduces nocturia by ~50%, increases androgen receptor density (similar to L-carnitine), and mildly lowers blood pressure** — independent of erectile dysfunction treatment. Doses above 10 mg/day may impair red/green color discrimination (clinically significant only for commercial pilots). [02:06:08](transcript.md#020608-prostate-health--tadalafil-prostate-specific-antigen-psa)

15. **Turmeric/curcumin suppresses DHT** — most formulations contain bioperine (black pepper extract), itself a 5-alpha reductase inhibitor and liver cytochrome modifier. Most of turmeric's beneficial actions are gut-mediated and do not require high bioavailability; avoid micellar, liposomal, or bioperine-containing formulations if DHT optimization is the goal. [02:09:56](transcript.md#020956-hair-loss--dht-turmeric--curcuminoids)

16. **BPA and phthalates are endocrine disruptors** — BPA binds estrogen-related receptor gamma (xenoestrogen); phthalates are most heavily concentrated in processed/powdered foods and pesticide-heavy rural environments, with the strongest evidence of harm during fetal development (Dr. Shanna Swan's research). Practical mitigation: glass or stainless steel containers, BPA-free cans, filtered and tested tap water. [02:18:13](transcript.md#021813-bpas-phthalates--hormone-health)

## Tools & Protocols

### Lifestyle Pillars (Foundation)

- **What:** Six behavioral pillars that directly regulate the HPG axis and hormonal milieu. Non-negotiable base before any supplement or drug.
- **How:**
  - Diet: adequate protein, healthy fats (cholesterol is a hormone precursor), fiber for gut microbiome, avoid excess alcohol and ultra-processed food. Avoid carnivore or vegan extremes during teens and early 20s — both reduce free androgens.
  - Exercise: resistance training 2-4x/week + Zone 2 cardio. Vigorous sessions should not regularly exceed one hour.
  - Sleep: 7-9 hours; the dominant natural GH pulse driver.
  - Stress management: mindfulness, prayer, therapy, time outdoors, social connection. Chronic cortisol suppresses HPG axis.
  - Sunlight/outdoor movement: UV-B for vitamin D synthesis, circadian rhythm entrainment.
  - Purpose: self-actualization (Maslow's hierarchy) reduces allostatic load.
- **When:** Continuously.
- **Ref:** [00:22:14](transcript.md#002214-diet-exercise-sleep--hormones), [00:28:23](transcript.md#002823-hormones-stress-social-connection--purpose)

---

### Blood Work Panel

- **What:** Comprehensive hormone blood panel to establish baseline and monitor progress.
- **How:** Total testosterone + SHBG (or free testosterone), estradiol (sensitive assay), LH, FSH, DHEA-S, prolactin, IGF-1, TSH, free T3, free T4, PSA (men 40+), CBC, CMP, lipids, vitamin D (25-OH). Morning draw (testosterone peaks AM). Free testosterone ideally ~2% of total (reference range 1-4%).
- **When:** First draw at 18; every 6 months minimum; every 3-6 months when actively adjusting protocols.
- **Ref:** [00:17:17](transcript.md#001717-hormone-optimization--blood-work)

---

### ADAM Questionnaire

- **What:** Androgen Deficiency in Aging Males — validated 10-question symptom screen covering libido, energy, strength, mood, and morning erections.
- **How:** Use alongside blood work and motivational interviewing — subjective symptoms and labs together drive clinical decisions. Available online (Saint Louis University).
- **When:** At any clinical visit evaluating hormonal status.
- **Ref:** [00:34:08](transcript.md#003408-determining-individual-hormone-levels-adam-questionnaire)

---

### Resistance Training Protocol

- **What:** Resistance exercise to acutely and chronically raise testosterone and GH.
- **How:** Large muscle group compound movements (squat, deadlift, press, row). Mix heavy (1-5 rep) and moderate (6-15 rep) loading. Avoid routinely training to failure. Limit vigorous sessions to under one hour. 3-4 vigorous sessions/week; lighter sessions can be added on top.
- **When:** 3-4x/week hard, additional lighter sessions optional. Allow adequate recovery.
- **Ref:** [00:49:46](transcript.md#004946-sustainable-exercise-regimen-for-hormone-health)

---

### Creatine

- **What:** Creatine monohydrate — improves strength, muscle mass, and cognition; raises DHT via 5-alpha reductase upregulation.
- **How:** 3-5 g/day. Hair loss alone is not a sufficient reason to avoid it — the DHT increase normalizes rather than supraphysiologizes androgens. If a non-responder, increase to 10 g or switch to betaine.
- **When:** Daily; no cycling needed.
- **Ref:** [01:01:02](transcript.md#010102-supplementation-creatine--hair-loss-betaine-l-carnitine--allicin-garlic)

---

### Betaine (Trimethylglycine)

- **What:** Methyl donor and creatine cousin; increases strength without raising DHT. Also lowers homocysteine.
- **How:** 1-3 g/day. May be added to creatine if homocysteine is elevated or used as a creatine alternative.
- **When:** Daily; no cycling needed.
- **Ref:** [01:01:02](transcript.md#010102-supplementation-creatine--hair-loss-betaine-l-carnitine--allicin-garlic)

---

### L-Carnitine

- **What:** Shuttle for mitochondrial fatty acid import; increases androgen receptor density in cells (similar mechanism to tadalafil). Oral bioavailability ~10%.
- **How:** Oral: 1,000-5,000 mg/day with food. Injectable (prescription): 500-2,000 mg IM. At doses above 3-4 g/day, take allicin (garlic, ~600 mg) to limit TMAO conversion risk.
- **When:** Daily; no cycling needed.
- **Ref:** [01:01:02](transcript.md#010102-supplementation-creatine--hair-loss-betaine-l-carnitine--allicin-garlic)

---

### Allicin (Garlic)

- **What:** Active compound in garlic; reduces TMAO conversion from carnitine and choline; berberine also mildly reduces TMAO but carries blood sugar-lowering side effects.
- **How:** ~600 mg garlic extract daily, or 1-2 raw cloves (crush, wait 10 minutes before eating to activate allicin). Take alongside L-carnitine or alpha-GPC at high doses.
- **When:** Daily with food.
- **Ref:** [01:01:02](transcript.md#010102-supplementation-creatine--hair-loss-betaine-l-carnitine--allicin-garlic)

---

### Vitamin D3

- **What:** Prohormone supporting testosterone production, bone mineralization, and immune function.
- **How:** 1,000-5,000 IU/day based on blood levels. Target serum 25-OH vitamin D 40-70 ng/mL. Take with K2 and a fat-containing meal.
- **When:** Daily.
- **Ref:** [01:11:45](transcript.md#011145-vitamin-d-boron-shbg--free-testosterone)

---

### Boron

- **What:** Mineral that acutely reduces SHBG, increasing free testosterone and free estradiol. Depleted in many soils; higher in Mediterranean regions (Greece, Turkey). Also a goitrogen — higher boron intake requires adequate iodine.
- **How:** 5-12 mg/day as boron glycinate. Effect is acute rather than sustained; consistent daily use maintains benefit.
- **When:** Daily.
- **Ref:** [01:11:45](transcript.md#011145-vitamin-d-boron-shbg--free-testosterone)

---

### Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia / Longjack)

- **What:** Herbal adaptogen upregulating multiple steroidogenesis enzymes; reduces SHBG in those with elevated SHBG; modestly raises DHEA and LH; increases total and free testosterone.
- **How:** 300-1,200 mg/day of standardized extract (look for eurycomanone content). Take early in the day (mild stimulant effect — may inhibit sleep if taken after 2 PM).
- **When:** Daily; no cycling needed per Dr. Gillett. Most potent during low-insulin or caloric deficit states.
- **Ref:** [01:16:39](transcript.md#011639-tongkat-ali-eurycoma-longifolia-longjack--steroid-pathways)

---

### Fadogia Agrestis

- **What:** Nigerian shrub extract that stimulates LH release from the pituitary, thereby raising testosterone dose-dependently. Not an LH mimetic. Rat data show dose-dependent elevation of GGT and alkaline phosphatase (liver/testicular stress markers).
- **How:** Conservative: 300 mg/day (safe dose from rat-to-human equivalence). Practical: 600 mg every other day or Mon/Wed/Fri. Aggressive: 600 mg daily for one month, then 1-2 weeks off — only with GGT and ALK phos monitoring.
- **When:** Cycled; not for continuous indefinite high-dose use.
- **Ref:** [01:22:09](transcript.md#012209-fadogia-agrestis--testosterone)

---

### Growth Hormone Optimization (Behavioral)

- **What:** Maximize natural GH pulsatility — largest pulse occurs during deep sleep, secondary pulse after intense exercise.
- **How:**
  - Prioritize 7-9 hours of sleep.
  - Avoid food for 2 hours before sleep — elevated insulin blunts the sleep GH pulse.
  - High-intensity or resistance exercise triggers a transient GH/IGF-1 pulse (much of this IGF-1 is released locally by muscle, not just liver-derived).
  - Fasting increases GH but reduces downstream receptor sensitivity — optimize the sleep pulse rather than prolonged fasting for GH purposes.
  - Amino acids before bed may modestly support GH release.
- **When:** Nightly and peri-workout.
- **Ref:** [01:26:32](transcript.md#012632-optimize-growth-hormone--igf-1-diet-fasting-supplements--exercise)

---

### Thyroid Optimization

- **What:** Adequate iodine intake and balance with goitrogens supports thyroid hormone production and T4-to-T3 conversion.
- **How:**
  - Consume iodized salt, seafood, and seaweed for iodine. Ocean-sourced iodine is preferable. Higher goitrogen intake (cruciferous vegetables, boron) requires higher iodine intake — they are a natural check and balance, not inherently harmful.
  - Monitor TSH, free T3, and free T4.
  - Note: starting TRT with an aromatase inhibitor (low estrogen) can acutely elevate free thyroid hormones — causing tachycardia or excessive sympathetic drive, because thyroxine binding globulin decreases when estrogen is low.
- **When:** Continuous dietary practice; labs annually.
- **Ref:** [01:31:52](transcript.md#013152-optimize-thyroid-hormone-iodine--goitrogens)

---

### Peptides: GHRH Analogs (Tesamorelin / Sermorelin / CJC-1295)

- **What:** Growth hormone releasing hormone analogs that stimulate pituitary GH release. Tesamorelin is FDA-approved for lipodystrophy; others used off-label.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection at night to mimic natural GH pulse. Source from licensed compounding pharmacies only — non-pharmacy sources risk LPS (lipopolysaccharide) contamination. Monitor IGF-1 and free IGF binding peptides (not just total IGF-1).
- **When:** Cycled, physician-supervised. Effects are individualized; many people take supraphysiologic doses off-label which carries unpredictable risks.
- **Ref:** [01:33:56](transcript.md#013356-peptides-growth-hormone-tesamorelin-ibutamoren--gut-microbiome)

---

### Ghrelin Agonists: Ibutamoren (MK-677) / Ipamorelin

- **What:** Ghrelin receptor agonists that increase GH and IGF-1. Ibutamoren is oral (long half-life, also known as MK-677); ipamorelin is injectable.
- **How:** Ibutamoren 10-25 mg orally at night (nighttime dosing to minimize hunger side effect). Ipamorelin by subcutaneous injection. Both significantly increase appetite and blood glucose — use with caution in insulin-resistant or pre-diabetic individuals.
- **When:** Cycled; monitor IGF-1 and fasting glucose. May cause anxiety.
- **Ref:** [01:33:56](transcript.md#013356-peptides-growth-hormone-tesamorelin-ibutamoren--gut-microbiome)

---

### Testosterone Therapy (Exogenous)

- **What:** Exogenous testosterone for clinical hypogonadism or medically supervised augmentation. Rarely justified for men in their 20s-30s with normal blood levels (300-900 ng/dL). All organ systems need monitoring — requires dermatology, cardiology, hematology, and lipidology awareness.
- **How:** Testosterone cypionate or enanthate 100-120 mg/week total, divided into 2-3 injections (subcutaneous or intramuscular). Monitor hematocrit, PSA, estradiol, blood pressure, lipids, acne, mood. 200 mg/week is supraphysiologic for most men.
- **When:** Only when clinically indicated. Pair with HCG if fertility preservation is desired.
- **Ref:** [01:42:06](transcript.md#014206-testosterone-therapy), [00:58:12](transcript.md#005812-testosterone-replacement-therapy-trt)

---

### HCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin)

- **What:** LH analog that stimulates Leydig cells, maintaining testicular volume and spermatogenesis during testosterone therapy. Also binds the TSH receptor — increases thyroid hormone demand ~30-40% (the normal mechanism of pregnancy; critical to adjust thyroid medication dosing accordingly).
- **How:** 500-1,500 IU subcutaneously, 2-3x/week alongside testosterone. Non-synthetic hCG is purified from first-trimester pregnant women's urine; synthetic (recombinant LH) also available.
- **When:** Throughout testosterone therapy when fertility preservation is a goal.
- **Ref:** [01:47:03](transcript.md#014703-prescriptions--hormones-human-choriogonadotropin-hcg-clomiphene)

---

### Clomiphene (Clomid) / Enclomiphene

- **What:** SERM that blocks estrogen feedback at the hypothalamus, raising endogenous LH/FSH and testosterone without suppressing spermatogenesis. Enclomiphene is the better-tolerated active isomer (faster half-life). Androxal (pure enclomiphene) did not complete FDA approval despite Clomid being approved.
- **How:** Clomiphene 25-50 mg every other day or daily. Note: Clomid affects estrogen receptors system-wide including the eye (visual disturbances at higher doses) and every other tissue. Side effects are common.
- **When:** Temporary measure when testosterone is severely low but endogenous recovery is desired. Not ideal for most men as long-term testosterone optimization. Most useful when fertility must be preserved.
- **Ref:** [01:47:03](transcript.md#014703-prescriptions--hormones-human-choriogonadotropin-hcg-clomiphene)

---

### Scrotal Temperature Management

- **What:** Reducing scrotal heat to protect spermatogenesis. Every 2°C increase in scrotal temperature = ~20% decrease in spermatogenesis and sperm viability.
- **How:** Avoid heated car seats, hot tubs, saunas (or apply cold pack to groin during sauna use), tight underwear. Standing more vs. sitting reduces scrotal temperature. Lifestyle changes alone (no medications) can restore sperm counts in men with heat-related infertility.
- **When:** Continuous practice; critical when trying to conceive.
- **Ref:** [01:52:56](transcript.md#015256-testosterone-therapy--hcg-fertility--temperature)

---

### Pelvic Floor Health

- **What:** Maintaining pelvic floor strength and blood flow for sexual function, continence, and overall hormone-adjacent health. Kegels are one tool among many — not the only answer.
- **How:** Learn the valsalva maneuver (bearing down with diaphragm braced) to engage pelvic floor correctly during compound lifts. Heavy compound leg/hip work (squats, deadlifts, hack squats) with proper form builds pelvic floor. Avoid over-contracting (excessive kegels restrict blood flow to pelvic area). The erection response is parasympathetic — requires relaxation, not constant tightening. Seek a pelvic floor physiotherapist if inguinal hernia history or incontinence exists. Prolonged bicycle riding compresses the perineum and weakens the pelvic floor — use padded shorts, recumbent bike, or stand regularly.
- **When:** Integrate into regular training; address proactively rather than reactively.
- **Ref:** [01:55:30](transcript.md#015530-hormone-health-qa-marijuana-nicotine-cycling-pelvic-floor-alcohol-fat)

---

### Low-Dose Tadalafil (Cialis)

- **What:** PDE5 inhibitor at low daily dose for prostate health, androgen receptor density upregulation (similar mechanism to L-carnitine), blood flow, and reduced nocturia.
- **How:** 2.5-5 mg/day orally. Up to 10 mg/day for stronger effects; doses above 10 mg/day may mildly impair red/green color discrimination (clinically significant only for commercial pilots). Also mildly lowers blood pressure.
- **When:** Daily. Particularly useful for men 40+ or on testosterone therapy.
- **Ref:** [02:06:08](transcript.md#020608-prostate-health--tadalafil-prostate-specific-antigen-psa)

---

### PSA Monitoring

- **What:** Prostate-specific antigen blood test for prostate health screening, especially on testosterone therapy or 5-alpha reductase inhibitors.
- **How:** Age-appropriate targets: ~0-1 ng/mL at age 20; ~1-3 ng/mL at age 40; up to ~5 ng/mL at age 80. Critical: on finasteride or dutasteride, PSA should halve — a stable or rising PSA while on a 5-ARI means the true underlying value has effectively doubled (flag immediately). Note: Huberman's personal experience showed a dramatic PSA spike when using sermorelin; it normalized after stopping.
- **When:** Baseline and annually at age 40+; every 6 months on TRT or 5-ARIs.
- **Ref:** [02:06:08](transcript.md#020608-prostate-health--tadalafil-prostate-specific-antigen-psa)

---

### Hair Loss: Topical Anti-Androgens

- **What:** Topical approaches to combat androgenic alopecia while minimizing systemic DHT reduction. Hair follicle miniaturization is the early warning sign (visible only under magnification) — intervene before the stem cell permanently leaves the follicle.
- **How:**
  - Topical ketoconazole shampoo — weak anti-androgen.
  - Topical caffeine — weak anti-androgen; crowds out androgens at the scalp receptor.
  - Topical dutasteride — minimal systemic absorption; does not meaningfully lower systemic DHT; preferred topical 5-ARI.
  - Topical finasteride — ~30% systemic DHT reduction; partially absorbed. Oral finasteride inhibits isoenzymes 1 and 2 (including genital skin isoenzyme — causes sensation and erectile side effects).
  - Oral dutasteride inhibits all three isoenzymes — higher side effect risk.
  - Saw palmetto — inhibits only some isoenzymes; incomplete coverage.
  - Avoid topical spironolactone in males — fully absorbed systemically, causing feminizing effects.
  - Combine anti-androgens with fertilizers (e.g., minoxidil) for best results.
- **When:** Continuous topical application for hair preservation.
- **Ref:** [02:09:56](transcript.md#020956-hair-loss--dht-turmeric--curcuminoids)

---

### Turmeric / Curcumin — Caution

- **What:** Turmeric reduces DHT and acts beneficially on gut microbiome and prostate. However, bioperine (black pepper extract) in most formulations is itself a 5-alpha reductase inhibitor and liver cytochrome modifier — the combination can significantly suppress DHT and vitality.
- **How:** Most of turmeric's benefit is gut-mediated — avoid micellar, liposomal, or bioperine-containing formulations. Plain turmeric is fine for gut/prostate benefits. If vitality or libido drops after starting turmeric, discontinue.
- **When:** Use cautiously in men prioritizing DHT optimization; beneficial for prostate and gut health in those who tolerate it.
- **Ref:** [02:09:56](transcript.md#020956-hair-loss--dht-turmeric--curcuminoids)

---

### BPA / Phthalate Avoidance

- **What:** BPA acts as a xenoestrogen (binds estrogen-related receptor gamma); phthalates are endocrine disruptors linked to reduced testosterone, altered fetal genital development, and declining sperm counts (Dr. Shanna Swan's research). Phthalates are most concentrated in processed/powdered foods and pesticide-heavy environments.
- **How:**
  - Use glass, stainless steel, or certified BPA-free containers.
  - Avoid canned food/beverages without BPA-free lining.
  - Filter and test tap water (even developed-country tap water varies widely in microplastic content).
  - Minimize highly processed packaged food, especially powdered mac and cheese.
  - Add processed mac and cheese to avoidance list during pregnancy.
- **When:** Continuous lifestyle practice; critical during pregnancy and fetal development.
- **Ref:** [02:18:13](transcript.md#021813-bpas-phthalates--hormone-health)

---

### Substances to Avoid

- **What:** Substances and behaviors that suppress testosterone, raise prolactin, or disrupt hormone production.
- **How:**
  - Smoked THC: lowers testosterone, raises prolactin, associated with gynecomastia. Edible THC has significantly less effect on prolactin. CBD largely neutral.
  - High-dose or sustained nicotine: vasoconstrictor; dose-dependent testosterone suppression; cardiovascular and neurodegenerative risk. Low-dose nicotine gum is less concerning but better alternatives exist (alpha-GPC, phosphatidylcholine, other cholinergic support).
  - Alcohol: activates liver aromatase (testosterone → estrogen); GABAergic — suppresses LH/FSH release similarly to opiates. Limit to no more than 3-4 standard drinks per two weeks maximum. No alcohol is better than any alcohol.
  - Prolonged bicycle riding: perineum compression reduces blood flow to pelvic floor; use padded shorts, recumbent bike, or stand regularly.
  - Very low fat diets: reduce cholesterol substrate for steroid hormone synthesis, lower free androgens and estrogens.
- **Ref:** [01:55:30](transcript.md#015530-hormone-health-qa-marijuana-nicotine-cycling-pelvic-floor-alcohol-fat), [02:06:08](transcript.md#020608-prostate-health--tadalafil-prostate-specific-antigen-psa)
