## Tools & Protocols

### Concern versus Influence Exercise

- **What:** A two-column written exercise that clarifies what you genuinely control versus what merely occupies your mental space.

- **How:** Take a standard piece of paper and draw a line down the middle. On the left, write everything occupying your waking hours and causing anxiety (concerns). On the right, write what you have direct influence over—ultimately just yourself: your thought process, self-talk, daily planning, and time management. Recognize that being scared or concerned about left-side items does not impact their outcome; only right-side actions do.

- **When:** Perform once weekly, or whenever sticky thoughts prevent sleep or hijack focus. Many people find once per month sufficient after initial practice.

- **Ref:** [00:05:01](transcript.md#000501-slug)

### Cold Plunge Protocol for Nervous System Training

- **What:** Deliberate cold water immersion to safely induce adrenaline and practice working with high-arousal states while maintaining control.

- **How:** Begin with water temperature around 40–50°F (or 34°F for more advanced practitioners). Spend 5–10 minutes total, adjusting duration and temperature based on tolerance. Do NOT hyperventilate before entering water. Practice normal breathing and lean into discomfort rather than resisting it.

- **When:** Perform on off-days or at least 6 hours after resistance training to avoid interfering with muscle recovery and hypertrophy. Best used as a standalone tool rather than immediately post-workout.

- **Ref:** [00:33:27](transcript.md#003327-slug)

### Heat-Cold Contrast Protocol ("Factory Reset")

- **What:** Alternating sauna and cold plunge exposure with unpredictable durations and temperatures to maximize nervous system adaptation and resilience.

- **How:** Spend 15–20 minutes in sauna at 210–220°F, then transition to cold plunge at 40–50°F (or cold shower as alternative). Complete 3 rounds. The key is *not knowing* how long each round lasts or how hot/cold it will be, creating maximum uncertainty and adaptive stress.

- **When:** Use occasionally (weekly or bi-weekly) rather than daily to avoid adaptation plateau.

- **Ref:** [00:35:02](transcript.md#003502-slug)

### Wing Suit Training Progression

- **What:** A structured progression from tandem skydiving → independent skydiving → wind tunnel training → wing suit skydiving → base jumping.

- **How:** Complete 3,000+ traditional skydiving jumps before attempting wing suit skydiving. Begin with tandem jumps if inexperienced with skydiving. Use wind tunnels to practice body position and flight control in a controlled environment before high-altitude jumps. Master straight jumps before attempting base jumping, which requires precise exit positioning.

- **When:** Progress only after mastering each phase; rushing this progression causes fatalities.

- **Ref:** [00:38:51](transcript.md#003851-slug)
