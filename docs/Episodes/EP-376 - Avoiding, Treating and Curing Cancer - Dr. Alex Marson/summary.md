# Episode 376: Avoiding, Treating & Curing Cancer | Dr. Alex Marson

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Alex Marson — immunologist, physician-scientist at UCSF, and Senior Investigator at the Gladstone Institutes — joins Andrew Huberman for a comprehensive deep-dive on cancer biology, practical prevention, and the cutting-edge immunotherapies already curing patients. The conversation covers how the innate and adaptive immune systems function, which everyday exposures genuinely raise cancer risk (and which are myths), and how CRISPR-engineered T cells are in active clinical trials for leukemia, solid tumors, and autoimmune diseases. Marson also explains why biology is at a genuine inflection point — a convergence of gene editing, single-cell sequencing, and AI that makes cellular medicine programmable for the first time.

## Key Takeaways

- **Cancer is ancient, not modern.** Mutations accumulate in all dividing cells throughout life; ancient mummies already show tumor evidence. Most cancers result from the interplay of time, genetic predisposition, environmental mutagens, and immune surveillance failures — not uniquely modern exposures. [00:30:51](transcript.md#003051-cancer-mutations--cell-regulation-smoking-brca-mutations-sunlight)
- **The immune system distinguishes self from non-self via probabilistic receptor generation.** T cells each carry a unique, largely randomly generated T-cell receptor; the thymus negatively selects any T cell that recognizes self-antigens. B cells generate diverse antibodies by a similar recombination process. When these checks fail, autoimmunity results (rheumatoid arthritis, type 1 diabetes, MS). [00:05:56](transcript.md#000556-immune-system-innate-vs-adaptive-immune-system)
- **The three highest-confidence mutagens are smoking, UV light, and occupational chemical/pesticide exposure.** Smoking is the dominant mutagen load. UV drives melanoma. Pesticide exposure — especially in rural agricultural areas — is seriously underestimated compared to urban pollution. Charred and smoked meats contain polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons that are genuinely mutagenic. Food dyes at laboratory-animal doses are not comparable to typical human dietary exposure. [00:38:27](transcript.md#003827-brca-mutations-mutagens-pesticides) [00:43:57](transcript.md#004357-x-rays--airport-scanners-carcinogen-vs-mutagen-charred-meat-food-dye)
- **Airport body scanners use millimeter-wave radiation, not ionizing X-rays**, but pilots have measurably higher cancer rates from atmospheric radiation exposure. Marson personally opts out of airport scanners when possible — not from formal data, but from a precautionary understanding of mechanism. [00:43:57](transcript.md#004357-x-rays--airport-scanners-carcinogen-vs-mutagen-charred-meat-food-dye)
- **BRCA1/2 mutations dramatically raise breast, ovarian, prostate, and pancreatic cancer risk** by disabling a key DNA-repair pathway. Testing is cheap and accessible; recommended whenever there is a relevant family history. Men with BRCA mutations can also develop breast cancer — this rarity is what originally helped identify the gene. Marson lost both his graduate and postdoctoral advisers to BRCA-related cancers in their 50s and early 60s. [00:38:27](transcript.md#003827-brca-mutations-mutagens-pesticides)
- **Checkpoint inhibitors remove the natural brakes on T cells, unleashing them against tumors.** PD-1 and CTLA-4 are inhibitory receptors on T cells that tumors exploit. Blocking them (e.g., pembrolizumab for PD-1, ipilimumab for CTLA-4) can cause dramatic tumor regression — Jimmy Carter's stage-IV melanoma with brain metastases went into remission on checkpoint inhibitors. [00:49:34](transcript.md#004934-immune-based-cancer-treatment-checkpoint-inhibitors-car-t-cell-therapy)
- **CAR T-cell therapy is a proven cure for certain blood cancers.** T cells are harvested, genetically engineered ex vivo with a chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) targeting a cancer surface protein, then reinfused. Emily Whitehead (treated age 8 in 2012 at UPenn) had exhausted all options for B-cell ALL; she achieved complete remission and is now pre-med at UPenn. The target CD19 is found on both cancerous and healthy B cells — fortunately, loss of B cells is tolerable. Finding similarly safe targets for solid tumors (pancreatic, brain) is the field's central challenge. [00:49:34](transcript.md#004934-immune-based-cancer-treatment-checkpoint-inhibitors-car-t-cell-therapy)
- **CRISPR-Cas9 is a bacterial immune system repurposed as a programmable DNA editor.** Bacteria evolved CRISPR to recognize and cut viral DNA. The key insight (Doudna & Charpentier, Nobel 2020) was that the RNA guide molecule can be redesigned to direct Cas9 to any DNA sequence on demand — guide RNAs are now ordered online. This enables precise cuts, deletions, or insertions at any genomic locus. David Liu at Harvard extended this further with base editors (single-nucleotide changes, no double-strand break) and epigenetic editors (turn genes on/off without any DNA sequence change). [01:08:27](transcript.md#010827-crispr-discovery--mechanism)
- **Marson's lab pioneered electroporation-based CRISPR delivery into primary human T cells.** Working with Jennifer Doudna, his lab purified Cas9 protein + guide RNA as a ribonucleoprotein complex, then used electroporation to breach cell membranes transiently and deliver the complex — a method now widely used for ex vivo T-cell editing. His student Theo Roth extended this to inserting large DNA sequences (up to ~10,000 nucleotides) at targeted genomic sites. This work spun out as Arsenal Biosciences, now in its third clinical trial for solid tumors including prostate cancer. [01:22:17](transcript.md#012217-crispr-cell-delivery-clinical-trials-treating-early-cancers--prevention)
- **Lipid nanoparticles (LNPs) are the mRNA vaccine platform extended to in vivo CRISPR delivery.** A billion people received LNP-packaged mRNA in COVID vaccines. Researchers are now engineering LNPs with T-cell-targeting ligands so that CRISPR or CAR-encoding mRNA can be injected systemically and delivered preferentially to T cells in the bloodstream — creating CAR T therapy without ever extracting cells. [01:33:47](transcript.md#013347-liposomes-engineered-viruses-lipid-nanoparticles-lnps-vaccines)
- **Antibody-drug conjugates, bispecific antibodies, and AI-designed protein engagers extend precision targeting.** Beyond CAR T cells, modular approaches attach drugs, radioactive isotopes, or cytotoxic payloads to tumor-specific antibodies. Bispecific T-cell engagers (BiTEs) use one antibody arm to grab a cancer cell and the other to recruit a T cell — no genetic modification needed. Amgen and others are now using AI to design synthetic protein engagers against any known tumor surface target. [01:49:39](transcript.md#014939-drug-delivery-to-cancer-immunotoxins-t-cell-engagers-ai-protein-targets)
- **CAR T cells are entering trials for autoimmune diseases.** The same CAR T cells used against B-cell leukemia (targeting CD19) also eliminate autoreactive B cells driving diseases like lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, type 1 diabetes, and multiple sclerosis. Early results in lupus trials are striking and may represent a new era in autoimmunity treatment. [02:10:44](transcript.md#021044-upcoming-therapeutics-autoimmunity--car-t-cells-crispr--gene-function)
- **Genome-wide CRISPR screens in 22 million primary human T cells are mapping the instruction manual for cell programming.** Marson's lab publicly released data pairing every possible T-cell gene knockout (via CRISPR) with single-cell RNA sequencing readouts. This identifies which gene edits produce stronger, more durable, tumor-resistant T cells — forming a recipe book for next-generation immunotherapies. Combined with epigenetic editing, it represents a step-function advance in what is imaginable and actionable. [02:10:44](transcript.md#021044-upcoming-therapeutics-autoimmunity--car-t-cells-crispr--gene-function)
- **Germline (embryo) gene editing is a hard ethical line for Marson.** He Jiankui edited CCR5 in human embryos ostensibly to confer HIV resistance, despite available non-editing alternatives (sperm washing). The edits were heritable, consent was inadequate, and CRISPR precision was insufficient at the time. Marson holds a clear position: somatic edits (non-heritable, in individual cells) are justified; germline edits that pass to future generations are not yet acceptable. He also raises deep concern about genetic fads via IVF selection and the loss of human diversity. [01:55:45](transcript.md#015545-crispr-embryo-modification-ethics-heritable-gene-editing-diversity)

## Tools & Protocols

### Cancer Risk Reduction (Lifestyle)
- **What:** Minimize the highest-confidence mutagen exposures.
- **How:** Avoid smoking entirely (including passive); limit UV (avoid sunburn, use SPF/protective clothing); reduce charred/smoked meat consumption; minimize unnecessary X-ray/CT scans; be aware of occupational and pesticide chemical exposure.
- **When:** Ongoing across lifespan.
- **Ref:** [00:30:51](transcript.md#003051-cancer-mutations--cell-regulation-smoking-brca-mutations-sunlight) [00:43:57](transcript.md#004357-x-rays--airport-scanners-carcinogen-vs-mutagen-charred-meat-food-dye)

### Immune Health Foundations
- **What:** Sleep and diet as modifiable levers for immune function.
- **How:** Prioritize consistent 7–9 hours of sleep — Marson cites sleep deprivation as the most reliable known suppressor of immune function. Reduce ultra-processed food; favor fiber and whole foods to support the microbiome. Avoid chronic obesity (Marson's lab showed obese mice had qualitatively different, drug-resistant inflammatory responses vs. lean controls, including resistance to anti-allergy antibody therapies).
- **When:** Ongoing.
- **Ref:** [00:16:11](transcript.md#001611-immune-system-health-sleep-diet-genes)

### Early Allergen Exposure (Children)
- **What:** Introduce allergenic foods early to prevent allergy sensitization.
- **How:** Early introduction of peanuts, eggs, and other common allergens in children not already allergic (per LEAP trial guidance). Avoid withholding these foods out of precaution in non-allergic children.
- **When:** Infancy / early childhood; consult pediatrician.
- **Ref:** [00:20:56](transcript.md#002056-childhood-exposure--allergy-prevention-autoimmune-reactions)

### BRCA / Genetic Screening
- **What:** Genetic testing for hereditary cancer predisposition.
- **How:** Seek genetic counseling and BRCA1/2 testing if personal or family history includes breast, ovarian, prostate, or pancreatic cancer. A positive result enables proactive surveillance (MRI, mammography) and discussion of prophylactic options with an oncologist.
- **When:** When family history warrants; testing is now cheap and widely available.
- **Ref:** [00:38:27](transcript.md#003827-brca-mutations-mutagens-pesticides)

### Checkpoint Inhibitor Immunotherapy (Medical)
- **What:** FDA-approved cancer immunotherapy removing inhibitory brakes on T cells.
- **How:** PD-1 inhibitors (pembrolizumab/Keytruda, nivolumab/Opdivo); CTLA-4 inhibitor (ipilimumab/Yervoy). Administered by oncologist; monitor for immune-related adverse events (colitis, pneumonitis).
- **When:** Prescribed for melanoma, NSCLC, bladder, kidney, colorectal, and other cancers. Not a lifestyle protocol.
- **Ref:** [00:49:34](transcript.md#004934-immune-based-cancer-treatment-checkpoint-inhibitors-car-t-cell-therapy)

### CAR T-Cell Therapy (Medical)
- **What:** Ex vivo genetic engineering of a patient's own T cells to target cancer.
- **How:** Leukapheresis → lentiviral or CRISPR-electroporation gene delivery at a certified facility → cell expansion and quality testing → freezing → shipping back → thaw and reinfusion. Monitor for cytokine release syndrome (CRS). Currently approved for CD19+ B-cell leukemia/lymphoma and BCMA+ multiple myeloma; clinical trials ongoing for solid tumors (prostate cancer, etc.) and autoimmune diseases.
- **When:** For patients with relevant hematologic malignancies; best accessed at major academic cancer centers.
- **Ref:** [00:49:34](transcript.md#004934-immune-based-cancer-treatment-checkpoint-inhibitors-car-t-cell-therapy) [01:22:17](transcript.md#012217-crispr-cell-delivery-clinical-trials-treating-early-cancers--prevention)

## Resources Mentioned

- **The Emperor of All Maladies** — Book by Siddhartha Mukherjee; a biography of cancer as a disease tracing its full history. Marson recommends it for historical and biological context.
- **The Case Against Perfection** — Book by Harvard philosopher Michael Sandel; a meditation on the ethics of genetic enhancement and the value of human chance and diversity. Recommended in the context of embryo editing.
- **LEAP Trial** — Learning Early About Peanut Allergy trial; demonstrated that early peanut exposure dramatically reduces peanut allergy risk in children not yet allergic.
- **Emily Whitehead case (2012)** — First pediatric patient treated with CAR T cells (CD19-targeted) at University of Pennsylvania; complete remission from relapsed/refractory B-cell ALL; now pre-med at UPenn.
- **He Jiankui experiment (2018)** — Chinese scientist who used CRISPR to knock out CCR5 in human embryos claiming HIV-resistance rationale; consent was inadequate; edits were heritable; sparked global bioethics debate; he was sentenced to prison and reportedly restarting a lab outside China.
- **CRISPR-Cas9** — Originally described as bacterial immune defense against bacteriophages; co-developed as a gene-editing tool by Jennifer Doudna and Emmanuelle Charpentier (2020 Nobel Prize in Chemistry).
- **AlphaFold (DeepMind)** — AI protein structure prediction enabling rapid identification of tumor-surface target proteins for antibody or CAR T design.
- **Arsenal Biosciences** — Biotech company spun out of Marson's lab; uses large-fragment CRISPR electroporation to engineer CAR T cells for solid tumors; currently in third clinical trial; prostate cancer trial enrolling.
- **Gladstone UCSF Institute of Genomic Immunology** — Philanthropically funded institute Marson directs; conducting academic CRISPR CAR T trial for multiple myeloma.
- **Lipid Nanoparticles (LNPs)** — mRNA vaccine delivery platform (COVID-19 vaccines); now being engineered with T-cell-targeting ligands for systemic in vivo CRISPR and CAR delivery without extracting cells.
- **Orchid Health** — Bay Area company offering deep embryo genome sequencing as part of IVF pre-implantation testing; discussed in context of statistical vs. causal mutation interpretation and selection ethics.
- **Operation Warp Speed** — US government program that accelerated COVID-19 vaccine development; cited by Marson as a scientific and regulatory success story.
- **Function Health** — Comprehensive lab testing service (sponsor); discussed in context of broad biomarker monitoring.
