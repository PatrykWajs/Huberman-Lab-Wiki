# Episode 190: AMA #16: Sleep, Vertigo, TBI, OCD, Tips for Travelers, Gut-Brain Axis & More

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Recorded live from Sydney, Australia during the Huberman Lab team's Australian tour, this 16th Ask Me Anything episode answers premium subscriber questions across a wide range of health topics. Huberman covers how to maintain health pillars while traveling (morning sunlight, red light bulbs, NSDR, movement, local meal timing), how to evaluate and improve sleep quality using the QQRT framework, a practical guide to diagnosing vertigo via vestibulo-ocular reflex anchoring and the Epley maneuver, strategies for recovering brain function after TBI or chronic poor lifestyle including creatine monohydrate and glymphatic optimization via elevated-feet sleep, the neurology of OCD and why compulsions worsen the obsessive loop, how to shift circadian rhythms using temperature minimum for cross-timezone travel, fish oil EPA dosing for mood and brain health, hormone monitoring schedules across the lifespan, how to optimize the gut-brain axis through fermented foods and microbiome diversity, and proper tongue-cleaning technique based on input from five dentists and a periodontist.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Health protocols are tools to mesh with life, not rigid rules.** Huberman views science-based protocols as designed to enhance mental and physical health alongside normal life — including socializing, irregular schedules, and travel. The core travel health stack: morning sunlight, red light bulbs in the evening, movement, NSDR, and local meal timing to shift the circadian clock quickly. [00:02:34](transcript.md#000234-achieving-key-health-pillars-while-traveling)

2. **Five to six hours of sleep is not automatically insufficient.** The clinical definition of insomnia is excessive daytime sleepiness from lack of night sleep. If you feel functional throughout the day, your personal sleep need may simply be lower. If sleep feels short, add a 10–30 minute NSDR session upon waking to recover lost sleep. [00:07:11](transcript.md#000711-improving-sleep-quality)

3. **QQRT is the correct framework for evaluating sleep.** Quality, Quantity, Regularity, and Timing — coined by Matt Walker — are the four dimensions that determine whether sleep is actually serving you. Regularity (consistent bed/wake times, ±1 hour on 5–6 days/week) is often the highest-leverage lever. Sleep trackers are useful but scores can create nocebo effects — check your subjective state first, then the score. [00:07:11](transcript.md#000711-improving-sleep-quality)

4. **Vertigo and lightheadedness are neurologically distinct.** True vertigo is a spinning/rotational sensation caused by inner ear or visual-vestibular mismatch; lightheadedness is a straight-down faint sensation. Distinguishing them while safely seated is the first diagnostic step. To acutely reduce vertigo or motion sickness, anchor the vestibulo-ocular reflex: fixate on a distant point or slowly move a finger toward the nose to force the visual system to dominate over incorrect inner ear signals. [00:13:11](transcript.md#001311-understanding-and-managing-vertigo)

5. **BPPV (displaced otoliths) is the most common cause of vertigo and is self-treatable.** The Epley maneuver repositions calcium carbonate crystals in the semicircular canals. Most cases resolve with one to three repetitions done at home. Confirm BPPV with a clinician before self-treating. [00:13:11](transcript.md#001311-understanding-and-managing-vertigo)

6. **The brain is highly plastic and can recover from years of poor lifestyle or mild TBI.** Biological systems are robust. Focus on sleep (critical for glymphatic debris clearance), exercise, sunlight, and nutrition. Post-TBI: elevate feet 5–15 degrees during sleep to increase glymphatic flow; consider 5–10 g creatine monohydrate daily for forebrain energy; consider hyperbaric oxygen. Avoid excessive curcumin/turmeric supplementation — it can reduce DHT and often contains lead contaminants. [00:20:44](transcript.md#002044-enhancing-brain-function-post-tbi)

7. **OCD is a basal ganglia miswiring problem where compulsions worsen obsessions.** Unlike scratching an itch, OCD compulsions exacerbate the obsessive loop. Behavioral treatments (exposure + response prevention) help, but pharmacological intervention (SSRIs and others) is often necessary for severe cases — all effective treatments work via neuroplasticity, not neurochemical correction alone. Neuromodulators like serotonin act as a wedge to open the plasticity window so that fewer behavioral-resistance trials are needed to achieve rewiring. [00:26:58](transcript.md#002658-getting-closer-to-unraveling-ocd)

8. **To shift circadian rhythm for east-bound travel, use temperature minimum and early light.** Temperature minimum = normal wake time minus 2 hours. Wake at your temperature minimum (or 1–2 hours before normal wake) in the 2 days before departure, get 5–10 minutes of bright light (artificial or sunlight), then return to sleep if needed. Do NOT view bright light before your temperature minimum. On arrival, seek morning light at local time. [00:30:44](transcript.md#003044-adjusting-circadian-rhythms-for-travel)

9. **Fish oil dosing: target 1–2 g of EPA per day, not total fish oil.** EPA specifically — not DHA — has demonstrated mild-to-moderate antidepressant effects in clinical trials above 1 g/day. High-dose EPA is an FDA-approved prescription drug. Most fish oil servings contain less than 1 g EPA — check the supplement label and take two servings if needed to reach the EPA threshold. [00:34:58](transcript.md#003458-optimal-dosage-for-fish-oil-supplements)

10. **Hormone monitoring schedule: establish baselines early and test annually from age 40.** Ideal checkpoints: late teens (post-puberty), mid-20s, ~30, ~35, then annually from 40 onward. Key markers: IGF-1, testosterone (total + free), estradiol, DHT, SHBG, LH, FSH, cortisol (fasted morning), creatinine, LDL, ApoB, ALT. For women add progesterone and prolactin, and standardize blood draw to the same menstrual cycle phase each time. Ratios (e.g., testosterone-to-estrogen, free vs. total testosterone) matter as much as absolute values. [00:40:17](transcript.md#004017-monitoring-hormone-levels)

11. **Gut-brain axis optimization: sleep, fermented foods, fiber, and microbiome diversity.** One to four servings of low-sugar fermented foods per day (kimchi, sauerkraut, kefir, natto) plus adequate prebiotic and probiotic fiber from vegetables supports gut health. Avoid overuse of antiseptic mouthwashes and hand sanitizers. Allow reasonable microbial exposure — pets, outdoor contact, not over-sanitizing hands in clean environments — to increase microbiome diversity. [00:46:09](transcript.md#004609-optimizing-the-gut-brain-axis)

12. **Tongue cleaning: use a separate soft toothbrush, brush gently — no hard scraping.** Hard tongue scraping damages mucosal tissue. Use a dedicated soft brush (separate from the teeth brush) and replace it every few weeks to months. Baking soda is acceptable — it is low on the dental abrasion scale. Antiseptic/alcohol-based mouthwash should be avoided. Oil pulling is not clearly beneficial per the dentists consulted, but is not harmful if preferred. [00:52:04](transcript.md#005204-best-practices-for-tongue-cleaning)

## Tools & Protocols

### Morning Sunlight While Traveling

- **What:** Natural light exposure as early as possible after waking, even on overcast days.
- **How:** Go outside within the first hour of waking. On cloudy days, still go out — cloud-filtered sunlight delivers far more lux than indoor lighting. Combine with movement, social engagement, and caffeine (or breakfast) to amplify the morning cortisol/catecholamine spike and accelerate circadian shifting to local time.
- **When:** Daily, immediately after waking.
- **Ref:** [00:02:34](transcript.md#000234-achieving-key-health-pillars-while-traveling)

### Red Light Bulbs for Evening Wind-Down

- **What:** Replace overhead lighting with red-spectrum bulbs in the evening to suppress cortisol and accelerate sleep onset.
- **How:** Travel with a portable screw-in red light bulb unit plus travel adapter. Switch off standard overhead lights after dark; use red bulbs only.
- **When:** From sunset onward until sleep.
- **Ref:** [00:02:34](transcript.md#000234-achieving-key-health-pillars-while-traveling)

### NSDR (Non-Sleep Deep Rest)

- **What:** A zero-cost protocol using guided relaxation (yoga nidra-style) to replenish mental and physical vigor and recover lost sleep.
- **How:** 10–30 minute sessions — upon waking if sleep felt insufficient, post-workout, or any point mid-day. Use the Huberman NSDR script on YouTube (free) or any yoga nidra audio track. Regular NSDR practice also improves subsequent ability to fall and stay asleep.
- **When:** Morning upon waking (to recover lost sleep) or afternoon after demanding training or cognitive work.
- **Ref:** [00:02:34](transcript.md#000234-achieving-key-health-pillars-while-traveling) and [00:46:09](transcript.md#004609-optimizing-the-gut-brain-axis)

### QQRT Sleep Framework

- **What:** Evaluate and improve sleep across four dimensions — Quality, Quantity, Regularity, and Timing.
- **How:** Assess each dimension independently. Regularity (consistent bed/wake times, ±1 hour on 5–6 days/week) is often the highest-leverage lever. Use sleep trackers as one data point but check your subjective state at mid-day before looking at the score to avoid nocebo effects. Slow wave sleep dominates the first half of the night (growth hormone release); REM dominates the second half (emotional processing).
- **When:** Ongoing daily habit; reassess weekly.
- **Ref:** [00:07:11](transcript.md#000711-improving-sleep-quality) — framework coined by Matt Walker.

### Vestibulo-Ocular Reflex Anchoring (Vertigo / Motion Sickness)

- **What:** Override incorrect vestibular error signals by forcing the visual system to dominate, reducing vertigo and nausea.
- **How:** Fixate on a point 3–4 feet away, then slowly move your finger from arm's length toward your nose, stopping near the cross-eyed point, then move back out. Repeat several times. Alternatively, go outside and fixate on the farthest visible point and walk toward it.
- **When:** At onset of vertigo, motion sickness, or after transitioning between closed environments (car, indoor gym, etc.).
- **Ref:** [00:13:11](transcript.md#001311-understanding-and-managing-vertigo)

### Epley Maneuver for BPPV Vertigo

- **What:** A repositioning technique that moves displaced otoliths (calcium carbonate crystals) back into the correct position within the semicircular canals of the inner ear.
- **How:** Confirm BPPV diagnosis with a clinician first. Standard Epley: sit upright, turn head 45° toward affected ear, lie back quickly with head hanging, hold 30 seconds, rotate head 90° to opposite side, hold 30 seconds, roll body and head another 90° toward floor, hold, then sit up slowly. Repeat 1–3 times.
- **When:** At onset of BPPV-type rotational vertigo.
- **Ref:** [00:13:11](transcript.md#001311-understanding-and-managing-vertigo)

### TBI / Brain Recovery Protocol

- **What:** Multi-pronged approach to maximize glymphatic clearance and neuroplasticity following TBI or years of poor lifestyle.
- **How:** Prioritize sleep quality; elevate feet 5–15 degrees during sleep (do not sleep upright in a chair — worst position for glymphatic flow). Take 5–10 g creatine monohydrate daily mixed in water to support forebrain creatine phosphate metabolism. Consider hyperbaric oxygen therapy if accessible. Train at 80–90% max output to avoid depleting recovery capacity. Avoid excessive curcumin/turmeric supplementation (can reduce DHT; risk of lead contamination); a little culinary use is fine.
- **When:** Ongoing post-injury or post-lifestyle-correction protocol.
- **Ref:** [00:20:44](transcript.md#002044-enhancing-brain-function-post-tbi)

### Circadian Rhythm Shift for East-Bound Travel

- **What:** Pre-shift sleep-wake cycle before traveling east to reduce jet lag.
- **How:** Calculate temperature minimum = normal wake time minus 2 hours. In the 2 days before departure: set an alarm for your temperature minimum (or 1–2 hours before normal wake); get 5–10 minutes of bright light (artificial or sunlight), then return to sleep if needed. Do NOT view bright light before your temperature minimum. On arrival, seek morning light at local time. Alternatively, force yourself onto the local schedule using caffeine, exercise, and social engagement — allow a 30–90 minute nap late morning if needed.
- **When:** 2 days before eastward travel.
- **Ref:** [00:30:44](transcript.md#003044-adjusting-circadian-rhythms-for-travel)

### Fish Oil (EPA) Supplementation

- **What:** Daily omega-3 EPA supplementation for brain health, inflammation reduction, and mood support.
- **How:** Target 1–2 g of EPA specifically per day — check the supplement label for EPA content per serving, not total fish oil or total omega-3. Most servings contain less than 1 g EPA; two servings are often needed. Liquid lemon-flavored form preferred at home; capsules (AG1, Momentous, or equivalent) work when traveling. Verify low contaminants via the Rhonda Patrick fish oil comparison chart (linked in that episode's show notes).
- **When:** Daily with food (e.g., in a protein shake).
- **Ref:** [00:34:58](transcript.md#003458-optimal-dosage-for-fish-oil-supplements)

### Hormone Level Monitoring Protocol

- **What:** Periodic blood work to track key hormones and metabolic markers across the lifespan.
- **How:** Establish baselines at late teens (post-puberty), mid-20s, ~30, ~35, then annually from age 40+. Key markers: IGF-1 (growth hormone proxy), testosterone (total + free), estradiol, DHT, SHBG, LH, FSH, cortisol (fasted morning), creatinine, LDL, ApoB, ALT. For women add progesterone and prolactin; standardize collection to the same menstrual cycle phase each time. Interpret ratios (testosterone-to-estrogen, free vs. total testosterone) not just absolute values. Go in fasted in the morning for cortisol accuracy.
- **When:** At life-stage checkpoints above; annually from age 40+.
- **Ref:** [00:40:17](transcript.md#004017-monitoring-hormone-levels)

### 80–90% Intensity Training Rule

- **What:** Train at 80–90% of maximum output and duration rather than going to failure, to sustain energy, prevent injury, and maintain long-term consistency.
- **How:** End workouts with 10–20% of capacity remaining. Avoid taking every set to failure. Lingering fatigue the next day signals excessive load — reduce intensity or duration. Apply the same principle to focused cognitive work: determine your consistent sustainable daily output and commit to it.
- **When:** Every training session; applies equally to cognitive work sessions.
- **Ref:** [00:46:09](transcript.md#004609-optimizing-the-gut-brain-axis)

### Gut-Brain Axis Optimization

- **What:** Maintain microbiome diversity and gut health to support mood, cognition, and overall physiology.
- **How:** Consume 1–4 servings of low-sugar fermented foods daily (kimchi, sauerkraut, kefir, natto, yogurt). Eat adequate prebiotic and probiotic fiber from vegetables and fruits. Avoid unnecessary antiseptic mouthwash and excessive hand sanitizers. Allow reasonable environmental microbial exposure (pets, outdoor contact, not over-sanitizing before meals in clean environments). Take prescribed antibiotics fully but avoid unnecessary use.
- **When:** Daily dietary habit.
- **Ref:** [00:46:09](transcript.md#004609-optimizing-the-gut-brain-axis) — based on work with Dr. Justin Sonnenburg (Stanford).

### Tongue Cleaning Protocol

- **What:** Proper tongue hygiene to reduce harmful bacteria while preserving the oral microbiome.
- **How:** Use a dedicated soft toothbrush (separate from the teeth brush) to gently brush the tongue surface. Do not scrape hard — aggressive scraping damages mucosal tissue. Optional: small amount of baking soda acceptable (low on the dental abrasion scale). Avoid antiseptic/alcohol-based mouthwash. Replace tongue brush every few weeks to months. Oil pulling is not clearly beneficial but is not harmful.
- **When:** Part of daily oral hygiene, especially before sleep.
- **Ref:** [00:52:04](transcript.md#005204-best-practices-for-tongue-cleaning)
