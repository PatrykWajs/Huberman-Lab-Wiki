# Episode 221: Supplements for Longevity & Their Efficacy | Dr. Peter Attia

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Peter Attia joins Andrew Huberman to systematically evaluate the scientific evidence for supplements commonly promoted as longevity interventions, organizing interventions into four tiers: foundational behaviors, disease-targeting drugs, geroprotective molecules, and the kitchen-sink approach exemplified by Bryan Johnson's Blueprint. They trace the NAD pathway in depth — NAD's dual role as electron shuttle and sirtuin substrate, the contested sirtuin/caloric-restriction hypothesis tested across multiple yeast strains, a 2012 SIRT6 transgenic mouse study showing sex-specific lifespan extension, and the ITP's null results for both resveratrol and NR. Human clinical data for NMN/NR is examined critically: the fatty liver RCT (Basis/NR) showed no primary-endpoint benefit, the NMN glucose disposal study was statistically significant but clinically meaningless, and the most compelling signal is a 60–80% reduction in skin cancer recurrence. Rapamycin stands entirely apart — the only pharmacological agent with robust cross-species lifespan extension — and the 2014 Mannick/Klickstein everolimus study showing immune enhancement with intermittent low dosing is discussed as a pivotal moment that shifted Peter's view from "interesting" to actionable. Both hosts share their full supplement stacks, Peter explains why he stopped metformin, biological age tests are critiqued as poor predictors relative to simple chronological age, low-level radiation risk is dismissed, and the episode closes with the Titanic analogy: behavioral foundations are the deck; supplements are merely deck chairs.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Four tiers of longevity approaches.** Tier 1: foundational behaviors (sleep, exercise, nutrition, genetics). Tier 2: disease-targeting drugs (statins, GLP-1 agonists, PCSK9 inhibitors). Tier 3: geroprotective molecules targeting hallmarks of aging (rapamycin). Tier 4: kitchen-sink polypharmacy. Most longevity supplements sit in Tier 3–4 with weak evidence. [00:06:38](transcript.md#000638-categories-of-longevity-approaches)

2. **Rapamycin: only molecule with robust cross-species lifespan extension.** Extends lifespan in yeast, worms, flies, and mice — the only pharmacological intervention alongside caloric restriction to do so across all four eukaryotic model organisms. Peter takes 8 mg once weekly and co-funded the Dog Aging Project (expected results 2026). [00:17:22](transcript.md#001722-peters-supplements-rapamycin--research-data)

3. **NAD's primary role is redox, not simply "energy."** NAD and NADH shuttle electrons through 500–600 metabolic pathways; NAD is tightly regulated like pH. What declines with age is not NAD quantity per se but redox potential: NADH rises as NAD falls, keeping total pool roughly constant, reflecting impaired electron transport chain function. [00:54:18](transcript.md#005418-nad--nadh-reactive-oxygen-species-ros-mitochondrial-health)

4. **Sirtuins consume NAD as a substrate for DNA repair, mitochondrial biogenesis, and SASP suppression.** The mechanistic chain motivating NR/NMN: declining NAD → less substrate for sirtuins → impaired DNA repair → accelerated aging. This chain has not been validated end-to-end in humans. [00:43:42](transcript.md#004342-dna-repair-sirtuins-cancer-resveratrol)

5. **The sirtuin/caloric-restriction story is strain-dependent and the pathways are independent.** Kaeberlein and Kennedy's yeast experiments demonstrated that sir2 overexpression and caloric restriction extend lifespan via parallel, independent pathways — not a shared mechanism. The hypothesis that caloric restriction works through sirtuins does not hold across strains. [00:32:25](transcript.md#003225-yeast-sirtuins-caloric-restriction--lifespan)

6. **SIRT6 overexpression extended male mouse lifespan 10–15% but not female.** A 2012 transgenic study showed a real but sex-specific effect. Sex differences in longevity research are common; Peter always checks Kaplan-Meier curves and whether both sexes were reported before accepting a longevity claim. [00:38:56](transcript.md#003856-sirtuins-transgenic-mice-gender--lifespan)

7. **Resveratrol failed the ITP completely.** Tested at doses specified by its original researchers in the original mouse model, resveratrol produced zero lifespan extension in the NIH-funded Interventions Testing Program. The original result was an artifact of a bizarre high-fat-diet model where mice died from liver fat compressing the thorax — resveratrol rescued that specific pathology, not aging. [00:43:42](transcript.md#004342-dna-repair-sirtuins-cancer-resveratrol)

8. **NR crosses cell membranes directly; oral NMN is effectively NR at the cellular level.** NMN's extra phosphate group prevents direct uptake — it is dephosphorylated in the gut to NR before absorption. The commercial NMN-vs-NR debate is therefore scientifically hollow. The ITP ran NR at 500–1,000 mg/kg in mice and found no lifespan or healthspan benefit. [01:11:33](transcript.md#011133-nr-vs-nmn-doses-side-effects-interventions-testing-program)

9. **Human NMN/NR clinical data: thin and effect sizes are clinically insignificant.** The Basis/NR fatty liver RCT (3-arm, ~30 per arm) showed no primary-endpoint benefit; a low-dose subanalysis showed marginal hepatic fat reduction still 3× above clinical thresholds. The NMN glucose disposal RCT (Washington University, 2021–22) showed statistically significant but clinically meaningless insulin sensitivity improvement. Statistical significance is not clinical significance. [01:17:43](transcript.md#011743-fatty-liver-disease--nr-nmn--glucose-clinical-significance)

10. **Skin cancer reduction is the most compelling human signal for NR/NMN.** A dermatology study found 60–80% reduction in basal and squamous cell carcinoma recurrence. Mechanistically plausible: skin shows the greatest NAD decline (~60% lifetime) and keratinocytes renew every 28 days, providing a constantly renewable population expressing fresh sirtuins. Peter wants replication before acting on this universally. [01:25:17](transcript.md#012517-safety--fda-nmn--nr-supplementation-skin-cancer-benefits)

11. **Rapamycin boosts immune function at intermittent low doses.** Mannick/Klickstein 2014: 65+-year-olds given low-dose everolimus for six weeks showed enhanced influenza vaccine response. Chronic daily rapamycin suppresses immunity; weekly pulsed dosing enhances it — making it an immune modulator, not simply a suppressant. This study converted Peter from "interesting" to "I should take this." [01:41:00](transcript.md#014100-rapamycin--immune-function)

12. **Biological age tests add little predictive power over chronological age.** Epigenetic clocks and movement-age tests have poor test-retest reliability. An actuary using just sex, age, and smoking status makes a strong longevity prediction; biological age tests have not been shown to meaningfully improve on that. Peter tracks "vigor" (0–10 subjective scale, logged every ~2 months) as a more actionable signal. [01:44:37](transcript.md#014437-biological-aging-tests-chronologic--biologic-age-vigor)

13. **Low-level background radiation is not meaningfully carcinogenic.** The linear no-threshold model is contested in radiation biology. High-altitude residents (Denver vs. Miami) face more background radiation but do not show higher cancer rates. Peter considers flight radiation a negligible health risk. [01:55:24](transcript.md#015524-radiation--cancer-risk)

14. **Peter's supplement stack: lean and evidence-anchored.** Omega-3s (2 g EPA/DHA), vitamin D3 (targeting 40–60 ng/mL 25-OH-D), three-form magnesium (~1 g elemental/day for sleep), B-complex, methylfolate, lithium orotate (5–10 mg, neuroprotective signal), NR (anti-inflammatory). Prescriptions: PCSK9 inhibitor (evolocumab), bempedoic acid, SGLT2 inhibitor, rapamycin 8 mg/week. Stopped metformin — it blunted exercise adaptations. [02:11:22](transcript.md#021122-peters-supplements)

15. **Andrew's supplement stack emphasizes NAD pathway and sleep support.** Multivitamin (some days), AG1 (1–2×/day), vitamin D3, magnesium threonate + bisglycinate (at night), omega-3s (2–3 g EPA/DHA), NMN 1,500 mg sublingual (morning), inositol (occasional, sleep), lithium orotate 1 mg (neuroprotective), ashwagandha (evening, stress/testosterone), tongkat ali (testosterone/mood). Reports faster hair/nail growth and increased energy on sublingual NMN — acknowledges n=1. [02:18:46](transcript.md#021846-andrews-supplements)

16. **Supplements are deck chairs; behaviors are the deck.** No supplement compensates for poor sleep, inadequate exercise, or suboptimal nutrition. Time and money spent on NAD drips would yield far greater return invested in structured exercise. Supplements are a marginal optimization on top of a solid behavioral foundation — not a substitute for it. [02:24:34](transcript.md#022434-tool-supplement-use-vs-critical-behaviors-titanic-analogy)

## Tools & Protocols

### Rapamycin — Weekly Intermittent Dosing (Peter Attia)

- **What:** Rapamycin (sirolimus), mTOR inhibitor, taken off-label for geroprotection.
- **How:** 8 mg once per week, oral. Intermittent to avoid chronic immunosuppression and preserve mTORC2 signaling. In practice ~2 months on / 1 month off due to aphthous ulcers (~10% of users).
- **When:** Ongoing for several years; not recommended without physician oversight — monitor lipids, glucose, wound healing.
- **Ref:** [00:17:22](transcript.md#001722-peters-supplements-rapamycin--research-data)

### NMN Sublingual (Andrew Huberman)

- **What:** NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide), NAD precursor.
- **How:** 1,500 mg dissolved under the tongue each morning to bypass gut dephosphorylation and improve absorption.
- **When:** Daily, first thing in the morning alongside other supplements.
- **Ref:** [01:11:33](transcript.md#011133-nr-vs-nmn-doses-side-effects-interventions-testing-program)

### NR for Skin Cancer Risk Reduction

- **What:** NR (nicotinamide riboside) for potential reduction in non-melanoma skin cancer recurrence.
- **How:** ~1,000 mg/day (dermatology trial dose). Most relevant for individuals with prior basal/squamous cell carcinoma or high chronic sun exposure.
- **When:** Daily; clinical trial effect observed over months.
- **Ref:** [01:25:17](transcript.md#012517-safety--fda-nmn--nr-supplementation-skin-cancer-benefits)

### Three-Form Magnesium Stack (Peter Attia)

- **What:** Magnesium supplementation targeting sleep quality and overall repletion.
- **How:** Magnesium L-threonate + magnesium oxide + magnesium citrate, totaling ~1 g elemental magnesium per day.
- **When:** Daily; sleep-supporting forms (L-threonate, glycinate variants) taken in the evening.
- **Ref:** [02:11:22](transcript.md#021122-peters-supplements)

### Exercise Timing for Energy Management

- **What:** Scheduling demanding workouts in the morning or early afternoon rather than evening.
- **How:** Complete high-intensity or long-duration training before energy declines. For sleep-sensitive individuals avoid vigorous exercise after ~5 pm. Individual variability is high — self-experiment.
- **When:** Daily habit; increasingly important from the 50s onward as recovery capacity declines.
- **Ref:** [02:07:12](transcript.md#020712-tool-exercise-timing--energy-levels)

### Vigor Tracking (Peter Attia)

- **What:** Subjective biologic health metric tracked every ~2 months as an alternative to unreliable biological age tests.
- **How:** Score 0–10 (0 = near death, 10 = best ever), adjusted for known stressors and positive life events. Log date, score, and context.
- **When:** Ongoing; compare trend across years and decades, not single data points.
- **Ref:** [01:44:37](transcript.md#014437-biological-aging-tests-chronologic--biologic-age-vigor)

### Foundational Behavior Prioritization (Both Hosts)

- **What:** Treating sleep, exercise, nutrition, and emotional health as non-negotiable before layering any supplement or drug.
- **How:** Set behavioral commitments as fixed — not optimizations. Accept reduced energy output by restructuring the day rather than compensating with stimulants or supplements.
- **When:** Lifelong; especially critical in the 50s–70s when energy reserves and recovery capacity decline fastest.
- **Ref:** [02:24:34](transcript.md#022434-tool-supplement-use-vs-critical-behaviors-titanic-analogy)
