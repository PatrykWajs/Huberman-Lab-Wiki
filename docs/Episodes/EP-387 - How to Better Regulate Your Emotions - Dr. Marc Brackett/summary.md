# How to Better Regulate Your Emotions | Dr. Marc Brackett

## Key Findings

- Emotion regulation is not eliminating feelings — it is using emotions wisely to achieve goals. The formula: ER = goals + strategies, as a function of emotion + person + context. — *[00:00:00](transcript.md#000000-intro)*
- The PRIME framework captures the five goals of emotion regulation: **P**revent unwanted emotions, **R**educe difficult ones, **I**nitiate positive emotions, **M**aintain good states (savoring), **E**nhance emotions. — *[00:05:24](transcript.md#000524-what-is-er)*
- Emotion regulation is NOT a constant activity — emotions live in the background most of the time. The trigger for regulation is a shift in environment or relationship, not a continuous self-monitoring loop. — *[00:10:49](transcript.md#001049-mindset)*
- A **fixed mindset about emotions** ("this is just how I am") is the single biggest barrier to emotional growth. A growth mindset — "I can learn to work with my emotions differently" — is the prerequisite for all change. — *[00:21:37](transcript.md#002137-mindset)*
- Boys and men are systematically taught that emotional vulnerability equals weakness. This creates suppression habits that drive relationship failure, professional dysfunction, and dramatically elevated suicide risk. — *[00:23:15](transcript.md#002315-boys-men)*
- **Emotional granularity** — the ability to distinguish between similar emotions (e.g., frustrated vs. overwhelmed vs. irritated) — is a trainable skill. Higher granularity predicts better regulation outcomes. The Feeling Wheel is a practical tool for expanding emotional vocabulary. — *[00:49:26](transcript.md#004926-emotional-granularity)*
- The **RULER** program (Recognizing, Understanding, Labeling, Expressing, Regulating emotions), developed at Yale, has been implemented in thousands of schools and shown to improve academic performance, reduce bullying, and improve teacher satisfaction. — *[01:49:04](transcript.md#014904-ruler)*
- Between stimulus and response there is space — but the skill is **building that space** deliberately. The automatic response is habit-driven and usually unhelpful. Moving from automatic reaction to conscious response is the core practice. — *[01:59:58](transcript.md#015958-self-regulation-tools)*
- **Checking in** — asking a partner, child, or colleague "how are you feeling on the mood meter?" — takes seconds and fundamentally changes the quality of the interaction that follows. The mood meter plots emotions on a 2-axis grid: energy (high/low) + pleasantness (high/low). — *[01:32:54](transcript.md#013254-checking-in)*
- The **Emotion Charter** is a group contract that answers: what emotions do we want to feel here? What do we agree to do — and not do — to create that environment? Used in schools, couples, and companies to set emotional norms explicitly. — *[01:06:01](transcript.md#010601-charter)*
- Technology and social media do not cause emotional dysregulation — but they accelerate it in people who lack foundational self-regulation skills. Teaching kids emotion regulation before heavy screen use is the intervention. — *[01:43:35](transcript.md#014335-disconnection)*
- **Reappraisal** (reframing the meaning of a situation) is one of the most evidence-backed regulation strategies. It works upstream of the emotional response and produces less physiological cost than suppression. — *[02:05:30](transcript.md#020530-self-regulation-tools)*
- Suppression is not the same as regulation. Suppressing an emotion increases physiological arousal and impairs memory of the conversation for the person doing the suppressing — and for the people they're talking to. — *[01:54:27](transcript.md#015427-emotion-suppression)*
- Emotional intelligence can be taught at any age. The most powerful intervention point is childhood, but adults who engage with the concepts and practice the skills show measurable improvement in weeks. — *[00:33:13](transcript.md#003313-ruler)*

## Tools & Protocols

### The Mood Meter (Daily Check-In)
- **What:** A 2-axis grid plotting emotional state — horizontal axis = pleasantness (unpleasant → pleasant), vertical axis = energy (low → high). Four quadrants: Red (high energy, unpleasant), Yellow (high energy, pleasant), Blue (low energy, unpleasant), Green (low energy, pleasant).
- **How:** Ask yourself — or someone else — "Where are you on the mood meter right now?" Takes seconds. The act of labeling moves the response from automatic to conscious.
- **When:** Before difficult conversations, when returning home from work, at the start of meetings, with kids after school.
- **Ref:** [01:32:54](transcript.md#013254-checking-in)

### Emotion Charter (Group/Couples/Family)
- **What:** A written agreement that answers: What emotions do we want to cultivate here? What behaviors support those emotions? What behaviors undermine them? What do we commit to doing when things go sideways?
- **How:** Sit down with partner, family, or team. Each person answers the questions. Write down the agreements. Revisit when conflict arises.
- **When:** At the start of a relationship, new job, new school year, or after a major conflict.
- **Ref:** [01:06:01](transcript.md#010601-charter)

### Reappraisal (Cognitive Reframing)
- **What:** Consciously reinterpreting the meaning of a triggering event before or during the emotional response. Changes the upstream meaning rather than suppressing the downstream feeling.
- **How:** When triggered, pause and ask: "Is there another way to see this? What would I think if I were calm? What would I tell a friend in this situation?"
- **When:** In conflict, before high-stakes interactions, when caught in a rumination loop.
- **Ref:** [02:05:30](transcript.md#020530-self-regulation-tools)

### Stimulus-Response Space (Pause Practice)
- **What:** Deliberately inserting a pause between a triggering stimulus and your response to shift from automatic/habitual reaction to deliberate/conscious response.
- **How:** When activated, note the activation ("wow, that just triggered me"), breathe, and before responding ask: "What response will actually get me what I want here?"
- **When:** Anytime you feel flooded — anger, anxiety, overwhelm — especially in conversation.
- **Ref:** [01:59:58](transcript.md#015958-self-regulation-tools)
