## [00:11:43] Testosterone & Women, SHBG

I want to touch on testosterone in women. Is knowing a woman's testosterone of equal, less than, or more value than knowing progesterone and estrogen levels? For health optimization, testosterone is just as important to know. For pathology prevention — for example breast cancer and osteoporosis — estrogen and progesterone are more important to know.

Women think they have such a tiny amount of testosterone because most people test free testosterone, which is by far the smallest proportion. Any androgen is bound by lots of different steroid-binding proteins, the most pertinent being SHBG — sex hormone-binding globulin. Women have more testosterone than estrogen and significantly more DHEA than either.

## [00:13:34] Dihydrotestosterone (DHT), Androgens; Turmeric & Black Pepper; Hair Loss

What is DHT doing? DHT is a very androgenic hormone. DHT helps effort feel good; it can be motivating. There are lots of dietary changes and supplementation that affect your DHT. ## [00:16:47] Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome (PCOS), Symptoms, Metformin, Inositol

PCOS — polycystic ovarian syndrome — is one of those conditions that is underdiagnosed. Its prevalence is much higher than we think; some studies say 10%, some say 20%. It's not completely clinically penetrant, so most people don't know they have PCOS until they have infertility or subfertility. Most women find out they have PCOS in their 30s, especially because it's on a spectrum — you can have a weaker or a very severe version.

For diagnosis, there's the Rotterdam criteria, looking for androgen excess, insulin resistance, and polycystic ovaries — though you don't actually have to have polycystic ovaries or get an ultrasound to be diagnosed. Symptoms of androgen excess include androgenic or hormonal acne, hirsutism — hair growth on the chin — deepening of the voice, and female pattern baldness. Insulin resistance manifests as obesity, pre-diabetes, high fasting insulin over six. Oligomenorrhea — more than 35-day intervals between periods or fewer than nine per year — is a very common sign. Infertility under age 35 after more than a year of trying, or over 35 after six months, is also a very common presenting complaint.

For treatment, optimizing body composition and decreasing body fat can help for those on the insulin resistance spectrum. Metformin is one tool for insulin sensitization, but not everyone with PCOS needs it. Other tools include myo-inositol, an insulin sensitizer, and D-chiro-inositol, a weak anti-androgen. Many inositol products contain both. Depending on whether you're female or male and on inositol, the type of inositol does matter.

## [00:20:13] Cannabis, Alcohol, Testosterone

Does marijuana reduce testosterone to a significant degree? Cannabinoids themselves — whether THC or CBD — are not going to reduce testosterone by themselves. If it's smoked marijuana, then it's very likely to increase aromatase, which increases estrogen, and that aromatization from testosterone will decrease testosterone. When you have increased estradiol, that works on the pituitary to make less LH and less FSH, which will subsequently decrease testosterone. Smoked marijuana acts similarly to opiates in decreasing LH and FSH.

As far as alcohol — high alcohol will decrease testosterone, as will any very potent GABA agonist. Whether it's a barbiturate, a benzodiazepine, a non-benzo, or alcohol, they're definitely going to suppress testosterone. The mechanism is direct suppression of the HPG axis, which means the downstream effects on LH and FSH are consistent regardless of which GABA-potentiating substance is being used.

## [00:23:04] Prolactin, Dopamine "Wave Pool", Tool: Casein & Gluten

Just as testosterone and estrogen need to be in proper ratios, dopamine and prolactin need to be in appropriate ratios. The way I describe it is the dopamine wave pool — if you're increasing your dopamine too much, you're going to overflow and then have that wave crash too much. You want nice even waves that don't go too far above the pool. Prolactin and estrogen are quite close cousins — estrogen upregulates the PRL gene, which directly increases prolactin synthesis. Prolactin will also inhibit the release of testosterone from the pituitary. If you're using a dopamine agonist, you're going to help decrease the prolactin-producing cells.

If someone is concerned about elevated prolactin, they can eliminate things that increase it — such as casein or gluten, which are mu-opioid receptor agonists in the gut. Casein — milk protein — can increase prolactin. This is a relatively simple dietary intervention that doesn't require medication and can meaningfully shift the dopamine-prolactin balance for people who are sensitive to these dietary compounds.

## [00:26:22] Peptides, Growth Hormone & Risk; BPC 157, Sourcing & LPS

Peptides are very heterogeneous — there are very dangerous ones and very safe ones. My favorite peptide is the original peptide: insulin. And yet insulin can kill you at the incorrect dose. Just like insulin should ideally be prescribed by a doctor, peptides should be prescribed by doctors as well, and several are FDA approved. Growth hormone itself is a peptide hormone, not a steroid hormone. The risks of taking a growth hormone releasing hormone peptide include tumor growth and cancer. Type 1 diabetics have very high incidences of various cancers and very high growth hormone but low IGF-1 paradoxically. The benefits — lipolysis, decreased body fat, increased lean body mass — can generally be achieved through other means. So a lot of people just don't need GHRPs.

BPC-157 is body protective compound 157, bioidentical to the gastric protective compound produced in the stomach. It increases VEGF — vascular endothelial growth factor — which makes blood vessels grow. Avastin, on the WHO's essential medications list for cancer, works as a VEGF inhibitor — the opposite mechanism. So if you have cancer or a high cancer risk, avoid BPC-157. It is not FDA approved but is one of the most commonly used peptides, and anecdotally and in some clinical literature it's fairly well tolerated for short periods. The major benefit comes from taking it early on because it allows the body to increase blood flow to an injured area — particularly useful for cartilage and ligaments, which have horrible blood flow especially as people age.

Non-prescription forms carry a specific danger: they often will contain what they claim — BPC-157 in this case — but are not adequately cleaned of LPS, lipopolysaccharide, which can cause inflammation. In the laboratory, LPS is used deliberately to induce fever and inflammation to study systemic inflammation. If you're interested in peptides, work with a physician and get them from a compounding pharmacy that cleans out the LPS. Many people experience a mild fever or tingling from non-pharmacy-grade peptides and think they can feel it working — that's probably LPS action, which is not good for the brain.

## [00:32:02] Melanotan, Uses & Risks

There are actually three FDA-approved indications for melanotan, believe it or not. One is hypoactive sexual desire disorder, more in women — that's for bremelanotide, also known as PT-141. Those are women who have essentially no libido whatsoever with other hormones in check, classically before menopause. When given this peptide, it helps significantly. It's used as a nasal spray — it goes straight into the central nervous system and acts centrally. You can also inject it or take it via troches. It's approved for women but can also help men and is relatively safe.

The only relative contraindication: if you have a family history of melanoma or potentially an undetected melanoma, it's going to increase alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone and could theoretically grow a melanoma. So be very careful about long-term administration. Melanotan is also approved for lipodystrophy — the same indication as tesamorelin, also known as Egrifta. And it's approved for the rare genetic condition where melanocytes don't proliferate as well, causing hypopigmentation and associated with morbid obesity in childhood.
