# Episode 288: Contracts of Love & Money That Make or Break Relationships - James Sexton

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Divorce attorney James Sexton, who has spent 25 years in family law, argues that prenuptial agreements — far from being unromantic — are one of the most effective tools for deepening connection, fostering honest communication, and strengthening marriages. Drawing on thousands of divorces and prenup consultations, Sexton reframes legal contracts as invitations to vulnerability and self-knowledge, and explains why the couples who complete prenups tend to stay married far more often than those who don't. The conversation spans the nature of intimacy, how social media and advertising structurally erode authentic connection, why infidelity reveals disconnection rather than desire, gender differences in how divorce is experienced, and practical tools for keeping long-term relationships vibrant.

## Key Takeaways

- **Prenups dramatically predict marital success:** People who complete prenuptial agreements are far more likely to stay married; Sexton estimates he has done the divorce for only approximately 5 out of hundreds of prenup clients. The self-selecting effect is key — the kind of people willing to have hard conversations required by a prenup are the same people likely to have a successful marriage. [Prenups & Strengthening Marriage](transcript.md#003356-prenups--strengthening-marriage)
- **Every married couple already has a prenup:** It was either written by the state legislature — subject to change without notice or consent — or co-authored by the two people in love. Two people in an abundance of optimism toward each other are better positioned to write that ruleset than unnamed politicians. [Contracts, Business, Marriage Celebration, Prenups](transcript.md#001441-contracts-business-marriage-celebration-prenups)
- **The U.S. divorce rate is approximately 56%, making conscious non-planning legally reckless:** Each subsequent marriage carries an even higher divorce rate. Consciously disregarding a substantial and unjustifiable risk of serious harm meets the legal definition of recklessness. An additional estimated 10–20% stay together for the kids or religious reasons, never appearing in the statistics. [Marriage Traditions; Divorce Rates, Religion](transcript.md#003819-marriage-traditions-divorce-rates-religion)
- **Gender differences in divorce are systematic:** Women fight harder for custody because motherhood is perceived as identity — a woman without daily custody is socially judged in a way a man is not. Men express pain primarily through anger (their only socially permitted emotion). Women tend to be more forgiving inside unhappy marriages but become decisive and "weaponized" once they commit to leaving. [Divorce & Breakups, Men vs Women, Perception; Infidelity](transcript.md#000219-divorce--breakups-men-vs-women-perception-infidelity)
- **Infidelity is a symptom, not a cause:** It appears in some form in over 90% of divorces, but almost always has a deep backstory of disconnection. Esther Perel's finding that 90% of people who have affairs describe feeling "alive" during them confirms that affairs are about seeking reconnection with aliveness, not purely desire. [Divorce & Breakups, Men vs Women, Perception; Infidelity](transcript.md#000219-divorce--breakups-men-vs-women-perception-infidelity)
- **The "soulmate" ideal demands the impossible:** Expecting one person to be simultaneously your best friend, co-parent, roommate, travel partner, sexual partner, confidant, and financial partner — who also happens to live nearby — is logically untenable. The early intoxication of love is neurochemically unsustainable by design; if it lasted, civilization would perish. [Marriage & Underlying Problems, Love, Successful Marriages](transcript.md#010237-marriage--underlying-problems-love-successful-marriages)
- **Intimacy is the ability to be completely yourself with another person:** Small authentic moments — like putting too much cream in coffee — build lasting emotional memory more reliably than grand gestures, because they represent true vulnerability rather than performance. These memories are permanently yours and don't create yearning the way peak experiences do. [Intimacy, Tool: Early Framework for Hard Discussions](transcript.md#012926-intimacy-tool-early-framework-for-hard-discussions)
- **Social media and advertising are structurally anti-intimacy:** Advertising is "the dream life of a culture" and simultaneously tells you that you're not OK. Rom-coms function like pornography for relationships — showing only the idealized peak and ending before reality, setting impossible standards. This constant barrage makes people afraid to reveal authentic flaws, eroding the vulnerability necessary for real intimacy. [Ideals, Social Media & Advertising, Simplicity, Dogs](transcript.md#011627-ideals-social-media--advertising-simplicity-dogs)
- **Bravery in relationships means giving the other person the power to hurt you and doing it anyway:** Withholding vulnerability is not bravery. Real bravery is revealing real flaws, not leverageable ones. Divorce is intimacy weaponized: the person to whom you whispered your deepest fears in trust can use them against you in a public courtroom. [Revealing Flaws, Bravery, Prenups & Expectations, Money](transcript.md#022243-revealing-flaws-bravery-prenups--expectations-money)
- **How a relationship ends powerfully shapes how the entire relationship is remembered:** One-trial learning means pain etches itself deeply and can overwrite 20 years of positive memories with six months of ugly litigation. A prenup substantially reduces this trauma and protects the good memories from being contaminated. [Fond Memories & Ending Relationship, Pain, Divorce](transcript.md#020230-fond-memories--ending-relationship-pain-divorce)
- **Post-nups can be legally fragile:** Courts sometimes find they lack valid consideration — "staying married" is not consideration because it is legally assumed. But the underlying conversations they encourage are just as valuable for couples already in long-term relationships. ["Postnup", Rekindling or Ending Relationships, Tool: Leave a Note](transcript.md#031658-postnup-rekindling-or-ending-relationships-tool-leave-a-note)
- **Longevity is not the measure of a successful marriage:** A 60-year marriage that was miserable for 45 years is not a success. The measure is whether the coupling made both people's lives better. Every marriage ends in death or divorce; accepting impermanence and choosing your partner every day, rather than staying out of obligation, is what makes long-term love meaningful. [First vs Second Marriages, Love & Impermanence](transcript.md#004444-first-vs-second-marriages-love--impermanence)

## Tools & Protocols

### Tool: Early Framework for Hard Discussions
- **What:** Establish a conversational norm — from the start of a relationship or at any point in an existing one — where both partners can openly flag things that landed wrong, hurt, or feel off, before resentment accumulates.
- **How:** Agree from the beginning that either person can say "that hit me the wrong way" without triggering defensiveness. Practice saying hard things out loud rather than carrying them. Apply the same directness used with close friends: assume good intent, acknowledge the impact, and move through it. Sexton frames this as "learning how to fight before you're in a fight."
- **When:** Ideally at the start of a serious relationship; if already in one, start now. The best time is always the present.
- **Ref:** [Intimacy, Tool: Early Framework for Hard Discussions](transcript.md#012926-intimacy-tool-early-framework-for-hard-discussions)

### Tool: Leave a Note
- **What:** Leave a brief handwritten note for your partner — or send an unprompted mid-day text — expressing appreciation, fondness, or that you were thinking of them.
- **How:** When leaving the house in the morning, take 30 seconds to write something like: "Had so much fun with you on the couch last night. Can't wait to see you." No cost, no special occasion needed. Do it consistently over several weeks until it becomes a normal pattern; initial partner skepticism ("are you having an affair?") typically gives way to increased warmth and connection within three to four weeks.
- **When:** Any morning or any moment during the day when a partner crosses your mind. Particularly valuable after years of marriage when small affirmations tend to fade.
- **Ref:** ["Postnup", Rekindling or Ending Relationships, Tool: Leave a Note](transcript.md#031658-postnup-rekindling-or-ending-relationships-tool-leave-a-note)

### Tool: Weekly Walk and Talk
- **What:** A structured weekly check-in walk with your partner to share wins, express appreciation, and surface friction before it becomes resentment.
- **How:** Once a week, go for a walk or hike together. Each person shares two to three things that were a big win or made them feel loved that week, and one or two things where they could have done better or where wires got crossed. Use a praise sandwich — positive, constructive feedback, positive. The goal is real-time course correction and continuous reinforcement of what the other person is doing right.
- **When:** Weekly, on a consistent schedule. Works equally well early in a marriage or after decades together.
- **Ref:** ["Postnup", Rekindling or Ending Relationships, Tool: Leave a Note](transcript.md#031658-postnup-rekindling-or-ending-relationships-tool-leave-a-note)

### Tool: Prenuptial Agreement as Relationship Framework
- **What:** Use the prenup process not merely as a legal document but as a structured, guided opportunity for both partners to discuss values, fears, financial expectations, and the rules they want to govern their shared life — while they are still feeling generous and optimistic toward each other.
- **How:** Each partner retains separate legal counsel. Together, build a custom ruleset covering division of assets, alimony, property, and optionally aspirational guidelines for the relationship. TrustedPrenup.com (co-founded by Sexton) offers a democratized version starting around $600–$700. Key questions: What do we mean to each other? What do we owe each other? What value do we each bring that the other does not have? The simplest structure is "yours, mine, and ours" — each party keeps their own pre-marital assets and liabilities; shared assets divide 50/50. Note: in community property states like California, absent a prenup, all separate property becomes marital property after 7 years.
- **When:** Before marriage, ideally while both partners are in the early, optimistic phase when fair and loving agreements are easiest to make.
- **Ref:** [Prenup Consultation, Legal Defaults, Reasons for Marriage](transcript.md#013706-prenup-consultation-legal-defaults-reasons-for-marriage); [Alimony, Prenups & Creating Rulesets, Yours, Mine & Ours, Adultery, Pets](transcript.md#014737-alimony-prenups--creating-rulesets-yours-mine--ours-adultery-pets)
