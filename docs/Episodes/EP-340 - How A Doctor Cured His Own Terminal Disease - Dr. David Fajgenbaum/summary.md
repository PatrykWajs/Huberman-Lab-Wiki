# Episode 340: How A Doctor Cured His Own Terminal Disease | Dr. David Fajgenbaum

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. David Fajgenbaum, MD — professor of translational medicine and human genetics at the University of Pennsylvania and founder of Every Cure — joins Andrew Huberman to discuss the science and practice of drug repurposing: using already-approved medications to treat diseases beyond their original indication. Dr. Fajgenbaum shares his extraordinary personal story of being diagnosed with idiopathic multicentric Castleman disease (iMCD) during medical school, surviving five near-fatal relapses, and ultimately discovering that sirolimus (rapamycin) — a drug approved for organ-transplant rejection — could keep his disease in remission.

The conversation covers the structural incentives in pharmaceutical development that leave repurposing opportunities untapped, the mechanisms of autoimmune and inflammatory disease, case studies of specific patients saved by repurposed drugs, the AI-powered approach Every Cure uses to systematically find new uses for existing medicines, and the neuroscience of resilience, hope, and the "impact circuit."

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Drug repurposing is structurally neglected.** There are 4,000 FDA-approved drugs for ~4,000 diseases, yet 14,000 diseases have no treatment. 80% of approved drugs are already generic, meaning no company is financially incentivized to find new uses for them. [00:04:06](transcript.md#0004-06-self-agency-in-healthcare-new-uses-for-old-medicines)
- **Each approved drug binds 20–30 proteins.** A small molecule approved for one condition is biologically active in many other pathways, creating vast unexplored repurposing potential. [00:06:44](transcript.md#0006-44-other-uses-of-aspirin-viagra-drug-development-approved-use)
- **Aspirin and colon cancer:** Aspirin reduces recurrence risk in colon cancer patients who carry a mutation in the mTOR pathway — yet it is widely underused for this indication because there is no financial incentive to promote it. [00:06:44](transcript.md#0006-44-other-uses-of-aspirin-viagra-drug-development-approved-use)
- **Lidocaine and breast cancer:** A 1,600-patient RCT in India showed a 29% reduction in 5-year mortality when lidocaine was injected around the tumor 8–10 minutes before breast cancer surgery. The drug costs pennies; uptake remains near zero because no entity is incentivized to promote a generic. Published in the *Journal of Clinical Oncology*. [00:08:53](transcript.md#0008-53-lidocaine-breast-cancer-pharmaceutical-companies-incentives)
- **iMCD (idiopathic multicentric Castleman disease)** is a rare cytokine-storm-driven inflammatory disorder in which the immune system produces excess cytokines — including IL-6 — that attack vital organs. It mimics lymphoma but is a distinct atypical lymphoproliferative disorder. Benjamin Castleman first described it after noting cases that looked like lymphoma but were histologically different. [00:40:41](transcript.md#0040-41-davids-medical-career-journey-glioblastoma-castleman-disease)
- **Rapamycin (sirolimus) as iMCD treatment:** After five near-fatal relapses, Fajgenbaum analyzed his own tissue samples and identified hyperactivated mTOR signaling. He proposed sirolimus — approved for organ-transplant rejection — to his physician, and it has kept him in remission for over 11.75 years. He takes it at transplant-level doses, far higher than longevity protocols. [01:03:32](transcript.md#0103-32-medical-school-relapse-overtime-finding-a-new-treatment-rapamycin)
- **DADA2 and TNF inhibitors:** Children with DADA2, a genetic disorder causing dozens of strokes from birth, stop having strokes when given TNF inhibitors — a connection discovered accidentally ~20 years ago. For ~10 years the knowledge sat unpropagated while children died. Fajgenbaum helped Dr. Chip Chambers codify the treatment protocol and disseminate it globally. [00:18:40](transcript.md#0018-40-tools-finding-reliable-health-sources-asking-questions-disease-organizations-dada2-treatment)
- **Pembrolizumab and angiosarcoma:** In 2016, Fajgenbaum's lab found a 2013 paper showing elevated PD-L1 in angiosarcoma tumors and treated patient "Michael" — previously declared terminal — with a PD-1 inhibitor. He achieved a 9-year remission and walked his daughter down the aisle. The drug now benefits ~18–20% of angiosarcoma patients. [00:24:36](transcript.md#0024-36-drug-repurposing-thalidomide-pembrolizumab)
- **Bachmann-Bupp syndrome and DFMO:** Children born with elevated ODC1 enzyme — wheelchair-bound and on feeding tubes — can sit up and play with siblings when given DFMO, a drug developed for African sleeping sickness that covalently binds ODC1. [00:33:51](transcript.md#0033-51-every-cure-database-programs-bachmann-bupp-syndrome-colchicine-heart-disease)
- **Colchicine and cardiovascular disease:** Used for gout for ~3,000 years, colchicine reformulated at a slightly different dose has been proven in RCTs to significantly reduce recurrent heart attacks, especially in patients with prior MI and diabetes. [00:33:51](transcript.md#0033-51-every-cure-database-programs-bachmann-bupp-syndrome-colchicine-heart-disease)
- **Every Cure's AI approach:** The nonprofit uses biomedical knowledge graphs and machine learning to score every approved drug against every disease (4,000 × 18,000), surfacing the highest-probability matches for rigorous laboratory and clinical validation. Co-founders Grant Mitchell and Tracey Sikora built the AI infrastructure. Approximately half the funding comes from ARPA-H (US government). [01:23:19](transcript.md#0123-19-business-school-castleman-disease-treatment-repurposing-drugs-ai)
- **The "Overtime" mindset:** After his fifth relapse, Fajgenbaum reframed survival as living in "overtime" — extra time beyond what medicine predicted. Physical scars and a periodic chest-port infusion serve as tangible reminders to stay grateful and purposeful even as the acute intensity fades over 11+ years. [01:20:29](transcript.md#0120-29-social-support-overtime-gratitude)
- **The anterior mid-cingulate cortex and the "impact circuit":** Huberman describes research by neurosurgeon Joe Parvizi (Stanford) showing that stimulating the anterior mid-cingulate cortex makes awake patients feel a challenge bearing down and want to lean into it. This structure retains volume in "super-agers" and is inversely correlated with depression. Fajgenbaum describes the same circuit as: hope → action → impact → more hope. [01:46:18](transcript.md#0146-18-healthcare-children-hope-action-impact-circuit-challenge-super-agers)
- **GLP-1 agonists as a repurposing frontier:** Emerging evidence suggests GLP-1s (e.g., semaglutide) may reduce risk of Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and breast cancer — possibly through metabolic, anti-inflammatory, or direct neuroprotective effects. [01:35:32](transcript.md#0135-32-nicotine-compounds-for-preventive-health-glp-1-agonists)

## Tools & Protocols

### Patient Self-Advocacy Framework
- **What:** A three-step approach for patients facing serious disease.
- **How:** (1) Find the disease-specific foundation or advocacy group — they know what's being tried globally. (2) Identify the world's leading expert and seek access. (3) Keep asking: "Is there another approved drug, used elsewhere, targeting this pathway?"
- **When:** At any point after diagnosis, especially before accepting "no options."
- **Ref:** [00:18:40](transcript.md#0018-40-tools-finding-reliable-health-sources-asking-questions-disease-organizations-dada2-treatment)

### Every Cure Database
- **What:** Free AI drug-repurposing platform cross-referencing 4,000 drugs × 18,000 diseases.
- **How:** Submit a drug-disease idea at everycure.org/ideas; sign up as a domain expert at everycure.org/experts; donate at everycure.org/donate. Every Cure cross-references submissions against machine-learning predictions and prioritizes the most promising matches.
- **When:** If you or a patient have an off-label drug observation, or if you're a researcher with a hypothesis.
- **Ref:** [01:52:50](transcript.md#0152-50-get-involved-with-every-cure)

### Rapamycin (Sirolimus) for iMCD
- **What:** mTOR inhibitor, approved for organ-transplant rejection, repurposed by Fajgenbaum for iMCD remission maintenance.
- **How:** Fajgenbaum takes it at transplant-level doses (much higher than longevity protocols). He identified the target by running flow cytometry, serum proteomics (1,000 proteins), and lymph node immunohistochemistry on himself between relapses.
- **When:** Only under physician supervision; dose and monitoring protocol depend on indication. Not recommended for general longevity use given potent immunosuppressive effects.
- **Ref:** [01:03:32](transcript.md#0103-32-medical-school-relapse-overtime-finding-a-new-treatment-rapamycin)

### Colchicine for Cardiovascular Risk Reduction
- **What:** Anti-inflammatory gout drug, reformulated at a distinct dose for cardiovascular prevention.
- **How:** Discuss with physician for post-MI patients, particularly those with diabetes. Available generically at low cost.
- **When:** Secondary prevention after myocardial infarction, especially with comorbid diabetes.
- **Ref:** [00:33:51](transcript.md#0033-51-every-cure-database-programs-bachmann-bupp-syndrome-colchicine-heart-disease)

### Perioperative Lidocaine Injection for Breast Cancer Surgery
- **What:** Injection of lidocaine around the tumor 8–10 minutes before surgical resection.
- **How:** 1,600-patient Indian RCT showed 29% reduction in 5-year mortality. Every Cure is conducting further laboratory validation. Discuss with oncology and anesthesiology team.
- **When:** Before breast cancer surgery, if evidence is sufficiently validated by treating team.
- **Ref:** [00:08:53](transcript.md#0008-53-lidocaine-breast-cancer-pharmaceutical-companies-incentives)

### The "Overtime" Gratitude Practice
- **What:** A cognitive reframe in which each day is treated as unearned bonus time.
- **How:** Use physical reminders of mortality (scars, medical devices) to reconnect with gratitude. Frame challenges as survivable one minute or one hour at a time rather than as an overwhelming whole.
- **When:** Applicable to anyone facing serious illness or long-term adversity.
- **Ref:** [01:20:29](transcript.md#0120-29-social-support-overtime-gratitude)
