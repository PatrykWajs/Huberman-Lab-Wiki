# Episode 234: The Effects of Microplastics on Your Health

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Andrew Huberman provides a thorough, science-grounded review of microplastics and nanoplastics — what they are, how they differ in size, and why their ubiquity in food, water, air, and manufactured goods makes complete avoidance impossible. He walks through the growing body of correlative human data linking microplastic accumulation to IBS, hormonal disruption (reduced testosterone, sperm quality, endocrine disruption via BPA/BPS/phthalates), cardiovascular plaque formation, and potential neuroinflammation. The episode covers the primary excretion pathways available to the body — liver phase-two detoxification, bowel transit, and sweating — and identifies specific, actionable behavioral changes to reduce both intake and bioaccumulation. Huberman is explicit that no direct causal human data currently exist, that the tone should be non-alarmist, and that pregnant women and young children are the highest-risk population warranting the most aggressive protective action.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Microplastics vs. nanoplastics defined** — Microplastics range from 1 micron to 5 mm in diameter; nanoplastics are smaller than 1 micron. Both are now ubiquitous in the environment, in food, water, and virtually every human tissue examined. The smaller nanoplastics are especially concerning because they cross biological barriers that larger particles cannot. [00:05:40](transcript.md#000540-microplastics--nanoplastics-ingestion)

2. **Brain accumulation** — Postmortem human brain tissue contains approximately 0.5% of its total weight in microplastic — roughly a teaspoon's worth. Nanoplastics cross the blood-brain barrier and are found lodged in dopaminergic and other neural circuits; there is also evidence they activate microglia, potentially creating a chronic neuroinflammatory state. [00:09:38](transcript.md#000938-microplastics-in-human-tissues-pregnancy-young-kids-bpa)

3. **Reproductive tissue penetration** — Microplastics and nanoplastics cross the blood-testicular barrier and the blood-follicular barrier. They have been found in every human testicle analyzed postmortem and in human semen, correlated with reduced sperm counts and lower sperm motility. They also cross the placenta and appear in meconium within 24 hours of birth. [00:09:38](transcript.md#000938-microplastics-in-human-tissues-pregnancy-young-kids-bpa)

4. **BPA/BPS/phthalates are endocrine disruptors** — BPA and BPS mimic or block estrogen receptors; BPA can also bind androgen receptors. Phthalates are associated with decreased testosterone in men, women, and children — with the strongest inverse signal in the 40–60 age range, suggesting cumulative bioaccumulation over decades. BPA is already banned in sippy cups and children's food containers in the US and Europe. [00:45:27](transcript.md#004527-testosterone-phthalates-bpa--bps-women-men--sperm-health)

5. **IBS correlation** — A 2021 study in the Journal of Environmental Science and Technology found significantly higher levels of microplastics in the stool of people diagnosed with IBS vs. controls. Causality is unestablished — it is possible IBS patients are less efficient at filtering microplastics — but the correlation makes dietary plastic reduction especially relevant for GI sufferers. [00:41:29](transcript.md#004129-nanoparticles--tissues-irritable-bowel-syndrome-ibs)

6. **Cardiovascular association** — A 2024 New England Journal of Medicine study found polyethylene microplastics in the carotid artery plaques of ~58% of 150 patients, with jagged plastic particles visible via electron microscopy among plaque macrophages. Whether plastic caused or contributed to plaque formation is unclear, but it may form physical substrate impeding blood flow. [00:55:25](transcript.md#005525-polyethylene--plaques-pfas-forever-chemicals-microplastic-excretion)

7. **PFAS "forever chemicals"** — Perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) do not break down in the body, cause liver damage and immune dysfunction, and persist indefinitely. Microplastics act as vectors that carry PFAS, BPA, BPS, and phthalates into tissues. [00:55:25](transcript.md#005525-polyethylene--plaques-pfas-forever-chemicals-microplastic-excretion)

8. **Bottled water is a major, controllable source** — A 2024 PNAS study using SRS (stimulated Raman scattering) microscopy found an average of ~240,000 nanoplastic/microplastic particles per liter in bottled water, up from the previously estimated 30,000. Hot or sun-exposed plastic bottles leach even more. Switching to glass, stainless steel, or ceramic and installing a reverse osmosis filter are the highest-leverage interventions. [00:19:21](transcript.md#001921-tools-plastic-water-bottles-water-filters-alternative-water-bottles)

9. **Canned soup causes massive BPA spike** — A randomized crossover trial found that one serving of canned soup per day for five days caused a >1,000% increase in urinary BPA vs. fresh soup. Labels saying "no BPA" often still contain BPS or other endocrine disruptors. [00:30:40](transcript.md#003040-tool-canned-soup-bpa-bps-phthalates)

10. **Liver phase-two detoxification is the primary excretion route** — The liver's phase-two conjugation pathway attaches molecules to toxins, making them water-soluble for urinary excretion. Sulforaphane from cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower) enhances this pathway. Huberman started taking 50 mg sulforaphane/day after researching this episode. [01:00:02](transcript.md#010002-liver-controlled-detoxification-tool-cruciferous-vegetables-sulforaphane)

11. **Dietary fiber and sweating also remove microplastics** — Adequate fiber binds lipophilic molecules (BPA, BPS, phthalates, PFAS) in the gut and facilitates bowel excretion. Regular sweating via exercise or sauna provides an additional excretion pathway for these compounds. [01:08:32](transcript.md#010832-tools-fiber-intake-non-stick-pans-carbonated-water-microplastics--cancer)

12. **Cancer vulnerability via gene expression disruption** — BPAs, BPSs, and phthalates do not merely block or activate hormone receptors — they control gene expression. Tissues with high cell-turnover rates (testes, ovarian follicles, breast tissue) are especially vulnerable because endocrine disruption during cell cycling increases cancer risk. [01:08:32](transcript.md#010832-tools-fiber-intake-non-stick-pans-carbonated-water-microplastics--cancer)

13. **Receipts are a direct skin-absorption source** — Thermal paper receipts are coated in BPA or BPS, absorbed directly through skin — increased by lotion or hand sanitizer. Declining or minimizing receipt handling substantially reduces this exposure route. [01:32:19](transcript.md#013219-tool-receipts--bpas-minimizing-microplastic-exposure)

14. **Pregnant women and young children are highest-risk** — The developing fetus is exposed via placental transfer; BPA appears in meconium within 24 hours of birth. Because the developing nervous and endocrine systems are most sensitive to disruption, this population warrants the most aggressive reduction in exposure. [00:09:38](transcript.md#000938-microplastics-in-human-tissues-pregnancy-young-kids-bpa)

15. **Developing brain: speculation, not established causation** — Arguments linking microplastics to ADHD and autism have been made publicly but Huberman does not find the data strong enough. He notes that microplastics are lodged in dopaminergic circuits and can activate microglia, which is biologically plausible as a concern, but no human causal data exist. [01:27:47](transcript.md#012747-developing-brain--microplastics-adhd-autism)

16. **Measurement tools are rapidly improving** — The jump from 30,000 to 240,000 particles/liter in bottled water was driven by better SRS microscopy, not worse contamination. As imaging improves, microplastic counts in tissues will likely increase further, meaning current figures underestimate true exposure. [00:37:34](transcript.md#003734-measurement-tools--advancements)

## Tools & Protocols

### Reverse Osmosis Water Filter

- **What:** Install a home reverse osmosis filtration system with remineralization to remove microplastics and nanoplastics from tap water.
- **How:** Units cost $300–$600. Choose a model that also remineralizes the filtered water to restore lost minerals. Fill reusable glass or stainless steel bottles from it. Likely saves money vs. ongoing bottled water costs.
- **When:** Ongoing; replaces bottled water purchases.
- **Ref:** [00:19:21](transcript.md#001921-tools-plastic-water-bottles-water-filters-alternative-water-bottles)

### Non-Marine Salt

- **What:** Replace sea salt with pink Himalayan salt or other non-marine-source salt.
- **How:** Swap your salt shaker and cooking salt. Sea salt sourced from the ocean carries measurable microplastic contamination.
- **When:** Immediately; low cost and effort.
- **Ref:** [00:26:57](transcript.md#002657-tool-sea-salt)

### Avoid Canned Soup

- **What:** Eliminate or minimize canned soup consumption.
- **How:** Shift to fresh-made soups. If using canned, look for products labeled no BPA, no BPS, and no phthalates — "no BPA" alone does not exclude BPS or other endocrine disruptors.
- **When:** Immediately.
- **Ref:** [00:30:40](transcript.md#003040-tool-canned-soup-bpa-bps-phthalates)

### Avoid Microwaving in Plastic

- **What:** Never heat food in plastic containers.
- **How:** Transfer food to glass, ceramic, or stainless steel before microwaving. "Microwave safe" only means the container won't melt — not that it won't leach BPA, BPS, phthalates, or nanoplastics.
- **When:** Every time you use a microwave.
- **Ref:** [00:34:55](transcript.md#003455-tools-plastic-containers--microwave-paper-cups--hot-liquids)

### Ceramic or Stainless Steel Beverage Containers

- **What:** Use ceramic mugs, stainless steel tumblers, or glass instead of paper cups or plastic lids for hot beverages.
- **How:** Carry a reusable mug. Paper cup linings leach BPA/BPS into hot liquids at temperatures as low as 100°F; plastic lids add additional exposure.
- **When:** Daily.
- **Ref:** [00:34:55](transcript.md#003455-tools-plastic-containers--microwave-paper-cups--hot-liquids)

### Cruciferous Vegetables / Sulforaphane Supplementation

- **What:** Increase sulforaphane intake to enhance phase-two liver detoxification of microplastics and associated chemicals.
- **How:** Eat several servings of broccoli or cauliflower per week, lightly steamed or pan-cooked in olive oil (preserves sulforaphane). Alternatively, supplement with sulforaphane. Huberman takes 50 mg/day. The examine.com human-equivalent dose from rat studies is ~1.1–7.2 mg depending on body weight; higher doses may provide further benefit.
- **When:** Ongoing, several times per week or daily if supplementing.
- **Ref:** [01:00:02](transcript.md#010002-liver-controlled-detoxification-tool-cruciferous-vegetables-sulforaphane)

### Dietary Fiber

- **What:** Consume adequate dietary fiber to support bowel excretion of microplastics and endocrine-disrupting chemicals.
- **How:** Eat a variety of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains daily. Fiber binds lipophilic molecules (BPA, BPS, phthalates, PFAS) in the gut and carries them out in stool.
- **When:** Every day; part of a normal healthy diet.
- **Ref:** [01:08:32](transcript.md#010832-tools-fiber-intake-non-stick-pans-carbonated-water-microplastics--cancer)

### Avoid Non-Stick Cookware

- **What:** Replace Teflon/PTFE non-stick pans with cast iron or ceramic cookware.
- **How:** Check that ceramic pans are explicitly BPA-, BPS-, and PFAS-free — look at the packaging. Non-stick coatings shed PFAS when scratched or overheated.
- **When:** Replace as pans wear out or immediately if scratched.
- **Ref:** [01:08:32](transcript.md#010832-tools-fiber-intake-non-stick-pans-carbonated-water-microplastics--cancer)

### Sweating for Toxin Removal

- **What:** Regular aerobic exercise or sauna sessions to increase sweating and facilitate excretion of BPA, BPS, and phthalates.
- **How:** Aim for regular cardiovascular exercise several times per week. Sauna use provides additional sweating opportunity. Exercise confers additional cardiovascular, metabolic, and neurological benefits beyond detoxification.
- **When:** Several times per week.
- **Ref:** [01:15:05](transcript.md#011505-tool-sweating--toxin-removal)

### Reduce Synthetic Packaged Foods and Clothing Shedding

- **What:** Shift toward fresh whole foods and natural-fiber clothing to reduce microplastic ingestion and fiber shedding.
- **How:** Buy produce and meat fresh rather than in plastic packaging. For laundry, use a microplastic-catching laundry bag (e.g., Guppy Bag) for synthetic garments, wash at lower temperatures, and choose natural fabrics (cotton, wool, linen) when purchasing new clothing.
- **When:** Ongoing lifestyle shifts.
- **Ref:** [01:18:21](transcript.md#011821-tools-packaged-foods-clothing-overconsumption--laundry)

### Avoid Microwave Popcorn

- **What:** Replace microwave popcorn with stovetop or air-popped popcorn.
- **How:** Microwave popcorn bags are lined with PFAS for grease resistance; these leach into the popcorn when heated. Pop kernels in a pot with oil on the stovetop or use an air popper.
- **When:** Whenever you eat popcorn.
- **Ref:** [01:25:11](transcript.md#012511-tools-microwave-popcorn-toothpastes)

### Check Toothpaste for Microbeads

- **What:** Avoid toothpastes that contain plastic microbeads as abrasives.
- **How:** Check ingredient lists for "polyethylene" or "polypropylene." Many countries have banned microbeads in rinse-off cosmetics but enforcement varies; some products still contain them.
- **When:** Next time you purchase toothpaste.
- **Ref:** [01:25:11](transcript.md#012511-tools-microwave-popcorn-toothpastes)

### Minimize Receipt Handling

- **What:** Decline or minimize handling of thermal paper receipts, which are coated in BPA or BPS.
- **How:** Opt for email or digital receipts when available. If you must handle a thermal receipt, do not do so after applying lotion or hand sanitizer (both increase dermal absorption). Wash hands afterward.
- **When:** Daily, at point of purchase.
- **Ref:** [01:32:19](transcript.md#013219-tool-receipts--bpas-minimizing-microplastic-exposure)
