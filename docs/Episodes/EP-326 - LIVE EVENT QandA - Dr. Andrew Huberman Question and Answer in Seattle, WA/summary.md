# Episode 326: LIVE EVENT Q&A: Dr. Andrew Huberman Question & Answer in Seattle, WA

## Episode Overview

This episode is a recording of the Q&A session from "The Brain Body Contract" live lecture event in Seattle, WA in 2022. Audience members asked Andrew Huberman questions spanning his personal protocols, mental health treatment, performance enhancement, memory, ADHD, supplements, neuroplasticity, and the future of the podcast. It is not a standard lecture episode — it is an informal, conversational format where Huberman shares both scientific knowledge and personal anecdotes from his life in research.

## Key Takeaways

- **Most-used personal protocol:** Morning sunlight viewing and 10–30 minutes of Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR/Yoga Nidra) every single day, usually in the early afternoon or after poor sleep.
- **Seasonal sunlight:** In winter or high-latitude locations, aim for 30 minutes of sunlight viewing and try to catch sunlight before sunset; light controls the dopamine-melanin pathway via tyrosine.
- **Waking up depleted:** If drive is gone upon waking, you may be waking mid-sleep cycle. Try waking at the end of a 90-minute ultradian cycle (6 or 7.5 hours total) rather than mid-cycle.
- **Memory consolidation:** Spike adrenaline after acquiring information — a cold shower, ice bath, or sprint immediately after studying dramatically improves memory consolidation. This is the mechanism behind one-trial traumatic learning.
- **Social media addiction:** Scrolling when you are not even interested is the hallmark of addiction. The dopamine hit from social media depletes quickly; the solution is scheduled phone-free blocks each day.
- **Performance enhancement beyond basics:** For elite performers who already have sleep and nutrition dialed in, the next frontier is creativity — accessing virtuosity by introducing unpredictable sensory input to destabilize normal brain states.
- **Mental health treatment future:** Behavioral tools combined with carefully directed use of MDMA and psilocybin in clinical settings are the most exciting frontier; macrodose guided sessions outperform microdosing per Matthew Johnson's research at Johns Hopkins.
- **Neuroplasticity at any age:** There is no evidence neuroplasticity disappears with age. Even terminally ill elderly patients are still producing new neurons in the hippocampal dentate gyrus (Rusty Gage, Salk Institute).
- **How to read papers:** Four questions — What are they asking? What did they do? What did they find? What did they conclude? — then check whether the conclusion actually answers the original question.
- **Dopamine and learning:** Dopamine is a powerful trigger of neuroplasticity; whatever you pair with a dopamine spike gets deeply encoded. This is why focus-enhancing drugs are a slippery slope and why directed use of plasticity-opening compounds matters.
- **ADHD:** For medicated individuals, timing the dose in the first 8–9 hours of the day is critical, when cortisol/dopamine/epinephrine systems are most active. For unmedicated individuals, focus can be trained through deliberate, uncomfortable narrow-aperture visual attention practice.
- **Supplements foundation:** Essential fatty acids (fish oil or fatty fish) and fermented foods (kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi) for gut microbiome health are the most fundamental starting points, based on Stanford data from Justin Sonnenburg and Chris Gardner's labs.
