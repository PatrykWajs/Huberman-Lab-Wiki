# Episode 237: How Relationships Shape Your Brain | Dr. Allan Schore

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Allan Schore, clinical psychoanalyst and world expert in the neuropsychology of early development, joins Andrew Huberman to discuss how the first 24 months of life shape right-brain circuitry responsible for emotional processing, attachment, and the unconscious mind. The conversation covers how child-parent interactions build the neural infrastructure that governs adult relationships, how attachment styles form and can be repaired, and what practical steps individuals can take to improve emotional regulation and connection throughout life. Dr. Schore argues that the right brain — not the left — is the dominant hemisphere for the highest aspects of human nature: love, compassion, creativity, intuition, and morality.

The episode runs approximately 2 hours 8 minutes and is structured around Schore's Regulation Theory, which integrates neuroscience, psychoanalysis, and developmental psychology into a unified model of how the brain and body are shaped by early relational experience.

## Key Takeaways

- **The unconscious mind is the right brain.** Dr. Schore proposes that 90–95% of human motivation and behavior is governed by unconscious processes operating in the right hemisphere, not by conscious left-brain reasoning. The right brain is always reading the emotional environment beneath awareness. [00:05:49](transcript.md#thoughts--unconscious-mind)

- **The first 24 months wire the emotional brain.** During early infancy, the right brain develops in direct response to attunement experiences with the primary caregiver. Six major neuroscience studies across multiple countries confirm right hemisphere dominance from the last trimester of pregnancy through the second to third year of life. The quality and consistency of this early co-regulation determines the template for all later attachment — secure, avoidant, anxious, or disorganized. [00:07:36](transcript.md#right-vs-left-brain-child-development-attachment)

- **Attachment styles are regulatory strategies, not personality traits.** Secure attachment develops when caregivers reliably attune to the infant's emotional states and engage in rupture-and-repair cycles. Avoidant (dismissive) infants learn to over-rely on auto-regulation; anxious (ambivalent) infants constantly seek external co-regulation. Disorganized (Type D) infants — linked to later PTSD and borderline personality disorder — develop neither strategy and instead shut down the attachment system via dissociation. [00:13:19](transcript.md#attachment-styles--development-emotions--physiology)

- **Psychobiological attunement is the core mechanism of early development.** The caregiver's right brain literally synchronizes with the infant's autonomic nervous system — matching arousal states, then guiding the infant between up-states (sympathetic excitement) and down-states (parasympathetic calm quiescence). The key neuromodulators being regulated are dopamine (reward), noradrenaline/adrenaline (arousal and stress), and serotonin/oxytocin (calm bonding). Rupture-and-repair cycles are as important as attunement itself. [00:18:12](transcript.md#intuition-arousal-emotional-regulation--attachment)

- **Right brain-to-right brain communication underlies all deep human connection.** In adult relationships and therapeutic settings, emotional resonance occurs through implicit, non-verbal channels: facial expression, tone of voice (prosody), body posture, gesture, tactile contact, eye contact, and smell. The right temporal parietal junction is the hub where these inputs integrate and synchronize between two people. Hyperscanning neuroimaging — scanning two brains simultaneously — has confirmed that right hemispheres synchronize during genuine emotional interaction. [01:19:26](transcript.md#right-brain-synchronization-eye-connection-empathy)

- **Wide-ranging (evenly suspended) attention is the right brain's mode of listening.** The left brain uses narrow, focused attention — tracking words sequentially. The right brain uses wide-ranging attention (Freud's "evenly suspended attention") that simultaneously monitors the external environment and internal somatic states. Intentionally widening one's gaze and softening analytical focus switches the brain from left to right — achievable in roughly 100 milliseconds — enabling true emotional attunement. [01:15:32](transcript.md#right-vs-left-brain-attention)

- **The right brain is the seat of the highest human capacities.** Dr. Schore's new book on human nature identifies the following as right-brain-dominant: intuition, creativity, processing of novelty, metaphor, imagination, humor, music, poetry, visual art, morality, compassion, spirituality, and love. Left-brain verbal intelligence is overemphasized in Western culture. Ian McGilchrist's framing — that the left hemisphere (the "emissary") has betrayed the right (the "master") — is directly relevant here. [01:42:18](transcript.md#right-brain-dominance--activities-tool-fostering-the-right-brain)

- **Rupture and repair is more important than never rupturing.** Perfect attunement is neither possible nor necessary. What matters is the caregiver's (or therapist's, or partner's) ability to recognize disconnection and re-establish co-regulation. This repair process itself builds the resilience and distress-tolerance that constitute secure attachment. [00:23:13](transcript.md#psychobiological-attunement-repair-insecure--anxious-attachment)

- **Therapy works through right-brain rewiring, not left-brain insight.** Effective psychotherapy operates through the same right brain-to-right brain attunement mechanisms as early development. The therapist must "surrender" — releasing left-brain analytical control — to enter right-brain receptivity and resonate with the patient's unconscious emotional state. Heightened affective moments (lasting only 50–60 seconds) within a session are when genuine rewiring occurs; these require shared synchronized emotional presence, not verbal interpretation. [00:35:51](transcript.md#surrender-therapy-patient-synchronization)

- **Two types of love — quiet and excited — both need integration.** Donald Winnicott distinguished quiet love (parasympathetic, down-regulated calm) from excited love (sympathetic, high-arousal passion). Both are essential and must be integrated. An overemphasis on down-regulating negative states misses the equal importance of up-regulating positive states — joy, enthusiasm, excitement — which are served by dopamine and related systems. [00:57:46](transcript.md#integrating-positive--negative-emotions-quiet-vs-excited-love)

- **Splitting in borderline personality disorder reflects a failure to integrate positive and negative affect.** The inability to hold both good and bad aspects of a person simultaneously reflects a developmental failure to integrate right-brain systems, rooted in early trauma or disorganized attachment. Narcissistic personality disorder also comes in two forms: vulnerable (anxiously-attached, constantly seeking praise) and egotistical (avoidant-attached, dismissing the need for co-regulation). [01:03:33](transcript.md#splitting-borderline-therapy--emotions)

- **In utero development shapes the right brain before birth.** Evidence shows right-hemisphere lateralization in the fetus, with early memories stored in the right amygdala. Synchronization occurs across the placenta — maternal autonomic states regulate fetal autonomic states. High maternal cortisol during pregnancy can disrupt critical period development of the right amygdala, insula, and cingulate. [00:50:51](transcript.md#mdma-right-brain-fetal-development)

- **Music, dogs, and nature are legitimate right-brain affect regulation tools.** Music engages the same right-brain subcortical and cortical circuits as early attachment prosody. Dogs communicate through touch, vocal prosody, and smell — bypassing the left hemisphere entirely. Nature and physical ambulation with panoramic (wide-ranging) vision shift the brain into right-hemisphere dominance. These are forms of auto-regulation that counterbalance left-dominant daily life. [01:25:39](transcript.md#music--dogs-resonance)

- **Maternal and paternal leave policy has direct neurobiological consequences.** The US returns most parents to work at 6 weeks — the precise onset of critical period development for the autonomic nervous system, amygdala, insula, and cingulate. A 2021 UNICEF poll of 36 rich nations ranked the US last in childhood emotional well-being. A London School of Economics study found the best predictor of adult life satisfaction is childhood emotional regulation — above conduct and far above IQ. [01:59:31](transcript.md#paternal-leave-parent-child-relationships-attachment)

- **Text messaging strips out nearly all right-brain relational content.** Right-brain communication relies on facial expression, vocal prosody, gaze, body language, and touch. Text removes all of these; emojis are neurologically inadequate substitutes. People with hypertrophied left brains find text comfortable — it fits their mode — but for emotional repair and connection it is insufficient. [01:36:47](transcript.md#tool-text-message-communication-relationships)

- **MDMA-assisted therapy may work by facilitating right-brain synchrony.** Empathogens like MDMA, by lowering defensive barriers and enhancing oxytocin-mediated openness, may temporarily amplify the therapist-patient right-brain co-regulation that drives therapeutic change — especially when paired with a therapist trained in right-brain dynamics. (MDMA-assisted therapy was not FDA-approved as of the recording date.) [00:50:51](transcript.md#mdma-right-brain-fetal-development)

## Tools & Protocols

### Seek Vulnerability and Openness with Trusted Others
- **What:** Forming genuine right brain-to-right brain connections outside therapy by finding people with whom you can be emotionally open, showing shortcomings, and receiving theirs.
- **How:** Prioritize relationships that allow mutual emotional openness. Allow and create "heightened affective moments" — brief windows (~50–60 seconds) of shared emotional presence that encode into autobiographical memory as the substrate of lasting neural change.
- **When:** Ongoing; most potent during stress or transition when the attachment system is activated.
- **Ref:** [01:09:24](transcript.md#tool-right-brain-vulnerability--repair)

### Prioritize Face-to-Face and Voice-to-Voice Communication
- **What:** Replacing text-dominant communication with in-person interaction or at minimum voice calls for emotionally significant relationships.
- **How:** Reserve text for logistics. For anything emotionally significant — repair, connection, conflict — default to phone calls or in-person meetings.
- **When:** Always; especially during conflict, repair, or connection-building.
- **Ref:** [01:36:47](transcript.md#tool-text-message-communication-relationships)

### Practice Wide-Ranging (Evenly Suspended) Attention
- **What:** Intentionally widening gaze and softening analytical listening to shift from left-brain narrow attention to right-brain wide-ranging attention.
- **How:** During conversations, periodically release focus on word content. Widen the visual field. Let the emotional atmosphere of the other person come in. Notice somatic responses — what you feel in your own body. The shift occurs in approximately 100 milliseconds.
- **When:** In any interpersonal interaction where emotional resonance or attunement is the goal.
- **Ref:** [01:15:32](transcript.md#right-vs-left-brain-attention)

### Cross-Modal Priming (Right-Brain Activity Before Creative Work)
- **What:** Engaging a right-brain activity such as drawing, painting, or playing music before transitioning to creative or intellectual work.
- **How:** Spend time in a non-verbal creative activity (visual art, music, free movement) before writing, planning, or any domain requiring novel insight. The goal is to access the right hemisphere's generative, pre-verbal, imagistic processing mode.
- **When:** At the start of a creative work session.
- **Ref:** [01:53:14](transcript.md#creativity-accessing-the-right-brain-insight)

### Engage Right-Brain-Dominant Activities Regularly
- **What:** Building right-brain resilience and emotional flexibility through regular non-verbal, embodied, novelty-seeking activities.
- **How:** Music (listening and playing), poetry, visual art, humor, meditation, nature walks with panoramic vision, travel and novel experiences, exercise, restorative sleep. Pursue curiosity and novelty — the right brain processes novel information first and releases a burst of noradrenaline in response.
- **When:** Daily or as frequent as possible; these serve as auto-regulation between interpersonal interactions.
- **Ref:** [01:42:18](transcript.md#right-brain-dominance--activities-tool-fostering-the-right-brain)

### Repair Ruptures Quickly in All Relationships
- **What:** Recognizing disconnection in any significant relationship and actively re-establishing co-regulation.
- **How:** Apologize, re-engage, and reconnect as soon as possible after a misattunement or conflict. The repair — not conflict avoidance — is what builds relational resilience.
- **When:** After any rupture; applies equally to parenting, romantic relationships, friendships, and therapy.
- **Ref:** [00:23:13](transcript.md#psychobiological-attunement-repair-insecure--anxious-attachment)

### For New Parents: Early Bodily Attunement Is Paramount
- **What:** Providing the right-brain regulatory experience an infant's developing neural architecture requires during the biological critical period.
- **How:** Sustained eye contact; skin-to-skin contact; matching the infant's vocal and facial expressions; prompt responses to distress; both up-regulating positive states (excited play) and down-regulating negative states (soothing). Fathers should be especially present in the second year for rougher, more arousing play that builds autonomy and risk tolerance.
- **When:** Birth through 24 months; the left brain does not come online until the end of the second year.
- **Ref:** [01:59:31](transcript.md#paternal-leave-parent-child-relationships-attachment)
