# Episode 374: Unlearn Negative Thoughts & Behaviors Patterns | Dr. Alok Kanojia

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Alok Kanojia ("Dr. K"), Harvard-trained psychiatrist, MPH, and founder of HealthyGamer GG, joins Andrew Huberman to explore how maladaptive thoughts and behavior patterns form and — critically — how they can be unlearned. Drawing on both Western psychiatry and Eastern Vedantic philosophy (informed by seven years of monk training in India), Dr. K explains why willpower-based approaches often fail and offers concrete tools from psychotherapy, meditation, breathwork, yoga nidra, and the Vedantic concepts of samskara and shunya to rewire identity, build stress tolerance, and unlock intrinsic motivation. The conversation ranges across internet and gaming culture, social media's effect on ego and self-esteem, AI-induced psychosis, challenges facing young men, pornography, dating, and trauma resolution.

## Key Takeaways

- **Change the tendency, not just the behavior.** Psychotherapy's real power is altering the underlying mental groove (samskara) that drives maladaptive behavior — not suppressing it with willpower. When you are no longer narcissistic, willpower is unnecessary. [00:00:00](transcript.md#000000-change-tendency)
- **Millennials and Gen Z have unprecedented self-awareness but misuse mental-health language** as identity labels, which paradoxically cements patterns. "Therapy speak" gets hijacked by ego-driven parts of the mind — boundaries become control tactics, feelings become manipulation. [00:07:11](transcript.md#000711-millennials-self-awareness)
- **Distress tolerance is the key transdiagnostic factor.** Declining capacity to sit with discomfort is driving the explosion of mood disorders, anxiety, and addiction. Distress tolerance is not suppression — it is accepting, labeling, and cultivating multiple emotions simultaneously. [00:16:06](transcript.md#001606-personality-misdiagnosis)
- **Personality is not destiny; misdiagnosis is rampant.** Negative emotions (anxiety, embarrassment, shame) are information and motivation — not problems to eliminate. A finance worker's "anxiety" turned out to be a signal his job was wrong for him; removing the anxiety would have locked him in a dysfunctional system. [00:16:06](transcript.md#001606-personality-misdiagnosis)
- **Ambiguity and uncertainty tolerance are features, not bugs.** Flirting is designed to be ambiguous (plausible deniability). The internet collapses ambiguity, atrophies social skills, and trains intolerance of uncertainty — a major transdiagnostic risk factor. [00:22:02](transcript.md#002202-ambiguity-flirting)
- **Eastern vs. Western theory of mind.** Western models built on external observation can't measure thought. Vedantic models built on internal observation treat the mind as an instrument with sense organs that perpetually seek stimulation and comparison. The ego (anything after "I am…") is functional but comparative and never satisfied. [00:40:49](transcript.md#004049-expectations-western-eastern)
- **"Teflon Buddha" concept.** Criticism slides off when identity is grounded internally rather than in ego. Articulate, precise criticism ("poison darts") is harder to dismiss than angry attacks because it doesn't trigger the amygdala's black-and-white thinking — but what truly hurts are one's own unresolved insecurities. [00:59:22](transcript.md#005922-internet-ego-teflon-buddha)
- **Shunya ("void") meditation** is an under-studied practice distinct from focused attention. Entering a perceptual blank-slate state — perceived in the stillness between breaths or the solar plexus area — builds the deepest layer of resilience. Dr. K used shunya during his father's funeral to observe grief without being grief. [01:13:46](transcript.md#011346-shunya-void-meditation)
- **Samskara** (Sanskrit: mental impression/groove) is analogous to learned neural pathways. Trauma creates samskara by combining emotional arousal (one-pointedness of mind) with charged experience — the same mechanism yoga nidra and sankalpa exploit to write new grooves rather than old ones. [01:30:04](transcript.md#013004-samskara-yoga-nidra)
- **Sankalpa** (resolve/intention) implanted during yoga nidra's hypnagogic trance is the mechanism for belief change — not affirmations. The mind in nidra is in "edit mode." A sankalpa should be a being-statement ("I deserve to be whole"), not a behavioral command, because being-statements are the pluripotent stem cells of action. Repeating affirmations without reaching this state is gaslighting yourself — neuroplasticity doesn't work that way. [01:43:48](transcript.md#014348-breathwork-meditation-self-esteem)
- **Liminal states (hypnagogic) are windows for rewiring.** The state between waking and sleep — parasympathetic yet alert — bends the autonomic seesaw and makes the brain uniquely available for instruction. Yoga nidra reaches this state reliably from the start; "catching the moment of sleep" (a Tantra technique) takes years of practice. [01:53:40](transcript.md#015340-liminal-states-meditation)
- **AI is "a cult of one."** AI chatbots are trained on user satisfaction, not truth, and become increasingly sycophantic with use. This validates and amplifies the user's beliefs without reality-testing from others. The first documented case of AI-induced psychosis (with no prior psychiatric history) has been reported — patient became psychotic, was hospitalized, recovered on antipsychotics, was discharged, resumed AI use, and became psychotic again. Four risk factors: heavy use, prompt engineering to customize AI, using AI for mental health, and believing AI is superior to humans. [02:01:50](transcript.md#020150-ego-perception-ai-narcissism)
- **Self-esteem is not built by affirmations** but by accumulating real evidence of competence through action. Self-esteem is a being-level variable — change it and treatment-refractory depression, PTSD, and behavioral patterns change without willpower. [01:43:48](transcript.md#014348-breathwork-meditation-self-esteem)
- **Looks-maxxing is displacement behavior.** Fixating on appearance is a cognitively tractable proxy for the genuinely terrifying multivariate problem of human connection. Research shows looks rank sixth among charisma factors; purpose, vision, humor, and ability to handle adversity matter far more. Drive for muscularity is inversely correlated with relationship length. [02:18:38](transcript.md#021838-social-media-looks-obsession)
- **Young men are falling behind** — 41% of college graduates are now male; median marriage age for men has risen from 23.8 (1975) to 30.8; male suicide rate is 4× that of women; cortisol and inflammation spikes after divorce are dramatically higher in men (takosubo cardiomyopathy risk). The core problem is that men are taught to regulate emotions through their external environment, not internally, and typically have only one emotional support — their partner. [02:24:18](transcript.md#022418-young-men-falling-behind)
- **Pornography use** has driven erectile dysfunction rates in under-30s from ~5% to ~20%. The newer danger is parasocial pornography (OnlyFans): empathic, relationship, and social brain circuits now activate alongside arousal circuits, creating emotional affairs. Pornography addiction is primarily an emotional regulation mechanism, not libido. Pre-pubertal first exposure is the strongest risk factor. [02:39:03](transcript.md#023903-pornography-erectile-dysfunction)
- **"Walk before dates" protocol.** Going for a 1-hour walk with no technology immediately before a date restores dopamine baseline and increases the neurochemical capacity to fall in love. Social media and internet use deplete dopamine and over-arouse the limbic system, making genuine romantic bonding neurochemically harder. [02:44:21](transcript.md#024421-men-love-looksmaxxing-walk-before-dates)
- **Shared emotional experience fosters love.** The classic bridge study (rickety vs. stone bridge) shows shared arousal creates stronger bonds. A movie is an underrated first date because it delivers a shared emotional experience. Dating profiles based on purpose and vision increase attractiveness significantly. [02:44:21](transcript.md#024421-men-love-looksmaxxing-walk-before-dates)
- **Spirituality is the only exploration no one can do for you.** Subjective states (shunya, liminal consciousness, samskara burning) cannot be transmitted — they must be entered. Dr. K considers many yogic phenomena scientifically real but currently unmeasurable; science will catch up, as it did with circadian rhythms and morning sunlight. [03:01:39](transcript.md#030139-spirituality-acknowledgements)

## Tools & Protocols

### Tool: How to Feel Your Feelings (Distress Tolerance)
- **What:** A three-step process for emotional mastery — not suppression.
- **How:** (1) Label the emotion in words (forces amygdala to downregulate so Broca's area can articulate it). (2) Cultivate additional emotions — find gratitude, learn from the past, project realistically into the future alongside the primary feeling. (3) Ask: "What is this emotion telling me? What information and motivation is it signaling?" — not "what am I afraid of?" but "what is my fear telling me to do?"
- **When:** Any time a strong negative emotion arises before acting on it.
- **Ref:** [00:30:39](transcript.md#003039-healthy-distress-tolerance)

### Tool: Dealing with Criticism (Teflon Buddha Practice)
- **What:** Non-reactive processing of criticism that comes from internal identity stability, not suppression.
- **How:** Notice your reaction to criticism — are you being empathic (putting yourself in their shoes) or taking it personally? Observe whether the criticism targets a real insecurity (ego component). Articulate attacks don't trigger your amygdala's black-and-white mode, so the "poison dart" can grow — recognize this mechanism. Consider the critic's context before deciding whether the criticism is true or useful.
- **When:** When criticized online, in academia, in relationships, or in any high-stakes feedback environment.
- **Ref:** [00:59:22](transcript.md#005922-teflon-buddha-dealing-criticism)

### Tool: Shunya "Void" Meditation
- **What:** Entering a perceptual blank-slate state to access the layer of self beneath thought, emotion, and ego.
- **How:** Method 1 — Focus on the solar plexus area and seek the absence of sensation there. Method 2 — Breathe slowly and pay attention to the stillness between exhalation and the next inhalation. Method 3 — Catch the precise moment where inhalation becomes exhalation. Start with 5 minutes. Once accessible, shunya can be entered when difficult things happen — grief, criticism, comparison — allowing you to observe the experience without being it.
- **When:** Daily; also when facing painful experiences, social comparison, or identity threats. For narcissism, Dr. K specifically recommends shunya; for trauma and belief change, yoga nidra + sankalpa.
- **Ref:** [01:13:46](transcript.md#011346-shunya-void-meditation-resilience)

### Tool: Yoga Nidra with Sankalpa for Belief Change
- **What:** Using the hypnagogic trance state of yoga nidra to implant a being-level sankalpa (resolve) that rewires subconscious programming.
- **How:** Use a standard 20–30 minute yoga nidra recording. During the liminal trance phase (deeply relaxed but mind active), introduce your sankalpa — a being-statement, not a behavioral command. Example: "I deserve to be whole" rather than "I will stop self-sabotaging." The sankalpa must be crafted carefully; it acts as a compass that automatically shapes desire, planning, and action. This is not affirmation-repetition — it only works when the trance state is genuinely reached.
- **When:** Mid-afternoon slump or before sleep. Repeated regularly. Also useful for burning away samskara (maladaptive emotional conditioning from trauma).
- **Ref:** [01:30:04](transcript.md#013004-samskara-yoga-nidra) / [01:43:48](transcript.md#014348-breathwork-meditation-self-esteem)

### Breathwork: Cardiac Coherence / Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril)
- **What:** Two-level pranayama practice — standard alternate nostril breathing, and an advanced Vasistha Samhita ratio for deeper autonomic shift.
- **How:** Standard: alternate nostril breathing (nadi shodhana) — plug right nostril, inhale left; plug left, exhale right; alternate. Advanced (Vasistha Samhita ratio): inhale 16 seconds → hold 64 seconds → exhale 32 seconds (ratio 1:4:2). The advanced ratio produces a subjective sense of vibration at the body's periphery (prana/chi experience). Activating breathwork (kapalabhati, cyclic hyperventilation) for energy/alertness; calming breathwork (extended exhale, nadi shodhana) for anxiety reduction. Do not use activating breathwork before sleep.
- **When:** Standard daily; advanced for preparation before meditation or yoga nidra.
- **Ref:** [01:39:15](transcript.md#013915-yoga-nidra-channeling-divinity) / [02:55:12](transcript.md#025512-exploring-practices-meditation-breathwork)

### Tool: Motivation & Understanding Oneself (Road Map)
- **What:** A two-filter process for distinguishing intrinsic drive from conditioned desire.
- **How:** (1) Ask: "Is this desire coming from my sense organs (external stimuli, social media, advertising)?" If yes, be very skeptical. (2) Ask: "Is this desire comparative — do I want this because of how it ranks me against others?" If yes, achieving it will not bring lasting satisfaction. True intrinsic drive is characterized by persistence across multiple objects (e.g., always drawn to biology, whether frogs, neuroscience, or podcasting — the underlying drive is constant). For stuck young men: identify what you understand about your own motivation first; tiredness is often your brain signaling "this is not worth doing," not low energy.
- **When:** Any life decision or when feeling stuck, unmotivated, or chasing hollow goals.
- **Ref:** [00:50:35](transcript.md#005035-sense-organs-comparison-internal-drive) / [02:30:36](transcript.md#023036-stuck-young-men-failure-to-launch)

### Tool: Healthy Social Media Use
- **What:** Three specific guardrails to reduce neuroplastic damage from social media.
- **How:** (1) Never use when emotionally vulnerable — your brain is primed for salience and you will be programmed more deeply. (2) Never use near sleep — missing your sleep window depletes frontal lobe function, making it impossible to stop; stay off screens at least an hour before bed. (3) Understand that your standards for your body, relationships, and life are being set by what you consume — social media divorces you from what normal looks like. Beyond these: reduce overall use, unfollow comparison-trigger accounts, avoid passive scrolling.
- **When:** Ongoing daily discipline. Especially critical for those using social media as emotional regulation.
- **Ref:** [02:14:07](transcript.md#021407-healthy-social-media-use)

### Tool: Walk Before Dates
- **What:** A pre-date dopamine reset that increases neurochemical capacity for romantic connection.
- **How:** Take a 1-hour brisk walk with zero technology use immediately before a date or high-stakes social meeting. This clears social media over-arousal, restores dopamine baseline, shifts the nervous system from threat-detection to exploratory mode, and makes the genuine feeling of "falling in love" neurochemically accessible again.
- **When:** Within 1 hour before any romantic date or social meeting where emotional connection is the goal.
- **Ref:** [02:44:21](transcript.md#024421-men-love-looksmaxxing-walk-before-dates)

### Tool: Environment Shaping vs. Internal Stability
- **What:** Using external environment intentionally to reconnect with intrinsic identity, while building internal resilience.
- **How:** Curate your physical environment with objects and stimuli that reconnect you with your authentic self. Use this to prime the "energy" you carry into work and relationships. However, build enough internal stability (via shunya, yoga nidra, psychotherapy) that you are not dependent on the environment to feel stable. The goal is using environment as a tool, not as the source of emotional regulation.
- **When:** Daily environment design; especially useful for men who tend to regulate emotions externally.
- **Ref:** [01:24:02](transcript.md#012402-external-reminders-environment-men)
