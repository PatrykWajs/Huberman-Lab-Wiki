# Episode 85: Exercise, Nutrition, Hormones for Vitality & Longevity | Dr. Peter Attia

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Peter Attia joins Andrew Huberman for a deep dive into the science of extending both lifespan and healthspan. They cover how to assess your true health status through blood work, DEXA scans, and functional tests, then move through exercise physiology, hormone replacement therapy for men and women, cholesterol and cardiovascular disease risk, peptides, GLP-1 drugs, and metabolomics. The conversation is anchored by Attia's "back-casting" framework — defining what you want your final decade of life to look like, then reverse-engineering the exercise and nutritional protocols to get there.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Back-cast from your "Marginal Decade."** Define in exquisite detail what physical and cognitive performance you want at age 80–90, then reverse-engineer the habits required today. The term "back-casting" comes from Annie Duke's work. VO2 max declines ~8–10% per decade, so calculate what you need now to arrive at your target. [00:32:16](transcript.md#003216-back-casting-defining-your-marginal-decade)

2. **VO2 max and muscular strength are the two biggest all-cause mortality levers.** Moving from the bottom to top quartile of VO2 max reduces mortality ~5X vs. bottom 25%. Low muscle strength is a ~3.5X hazard ratio for ACM — stronger than smoking's ~1.4X hazard ratio. [00:38:31](transcript.md#003831-all-cause-mortality-smoking-strength-vo2-max)

3. **Exercise first — before any supplement or dietary optimization ("Attia's Rule").** Don't argue supplement nuances until you can: dead hang 60 sec (women) / 2 min (men) at age 40, air squat at 90° for 2 min, farmer carry your bodyweight for 2 min (men) / 75% BW for women, and VO2 max at ≥75th percentile. Exercise dwarfs supplementation in longevity impact. [00:44:43](transcript.md#004443-attias-rule-of-supplementation-centenarian-decathlete-physical-goals)

4. **DEXA scans reveal four critical metrics BMI cannot.** Body fat %, visceral adipose tissue (VAT), limb-segmented lean mass (appendicular lean mass index / fat-free mass index), and bone mineral density (BMD). Attia ranks body fat as the least interesting of the four. Annual DEXA is recommended over scale weight. [00:16:01](transcript.md#001601-dexa-scan-lean-mass--fat-bone-mineral-density--osteoporosis)

5. **Power lifting is the best intervention for bone mineral density.** Power lifting creates shear force through tendon onto bone, activating osteoblasts. It outperforms running for BMD. Strength training is effective at any age — even women in their 60s–70s starting for the first time showed BMD improvement in an Australian RCT. Corticosteroids (inhaled or systemic, including some asthma inhalers) impair BMD development during the critical window (birth to ~age 25). [00:22:33](transcript.md#002233-bone-mineral-density--age-related-decline-strength-training-corticosteroids)

6. **The Women's Health Initiative HRT study was deeply flawed — four ways.** (1) Women enrolled 10–12+ years post-menopause. (2) Disproportionately unhealthy, smoking, obese patient population. (3) No symptomatic women included. (4) Used synthetic MPA (not bioidentical progesterone) and oral conjugated equine estrogen. The headline "27% increase in breast cancer risk" was a relative risk; the absolute risk increase was 0.1% (5 vs. 4 cases per 1,000). The estrogen-only arm showed a non-significant 24% *reduction* in breast cancer, suggesting MPA drove the negative result, not estrogen. [01:10:10](transcript.md#011010-hormone-replacement-therapy-menopause--breast-cancer-risk)

7. **ApoB, not LDL-C, is the lipid marker to track and target.** ApoB counts every atherogenic particle (LDL + VLDL). Attia targets <60 mg/dL for high-risk patients; aspirational <30 mg/dL (only achievable pharmacologically). 80% of his patients over 45 are on ApoB-lowering medications. Atherosclerosis begins in teenagers — autopsy studies on soldiers dying in their 20s confirm this. [02:02:51](transcript.md#020251-cholesterol--dietary-cholesterol-saturated-fat-ldl--hdl-apolipoprotein-b)

8. **Free testosterone and estradiol together determine how men feel — not total testosterone.** Free T should be ~2% of total T. Suppressing estradiol below 25 pg/mL causes fat accumulation, joint pain, memory issues, and libido loss. Target estradiol 30–50 pg/mL on TRT. A landmark NEJM study of five testosterone doses ± anastrozole confirmed higher T + estradiol in the 30–50 pg/mL range produced the best outcomes. [01:59:26](transcript.md#015926-total-testosterone-vs-free-testosterone)

9. **Lp(a) should be tested once in early adulthood.** 8–20% of the population carries genetically elevated Lp(a), a potent early atherosclerosis driver. It is genetically fixed, only needs testing once, and most physicians are unaware of it. [00:13:59](transcript.md#001359-blood-testing-best-frequency)

10. **GLP-1 agonists are the most significant obesity intervention in decades, but muscle loss is the critical risk.** Semaglutide acts ~80% centrally (hypothalamus) to suppress appetite. Must be paired with aggressive resistance training and high protein. Alcohol and liquid calories can circumvent appetite suppression. Weight regain occurs on cessation; next-generation versions aim to preserve loss off-drug. [02:40:44](transcript.md#024044-glp-1--weight-loss)

11. **Non-smoked nicotine enhances cognitive focus via nicotinic acetylcholine receptors.** Attia has cycled low-dose nicotine lozenges for 10 years and reports no addiction. He also uses 300 mg Alpha-GPC 1–2x/week before cognitive work or workouts. He does not prescribe stimulants (Adderall, Vyvanse) for cognitive enhancement. Lozenges deliver 4–8 mg per bolus — far more than one cigarette (~1 mg); dose carefully. [00:55:23](transcript.md#005523-nicotine--cognitive-focus)

12. **Clomid raises desmosterol — a potential long-term cardiovascular concern.** Attia's practice stopped prescribing clomiphene after observing near-universal desmosterol elevation. Desmosterol is the cholesterol precursor implicated in the withdrawal of triparanol (the first cholesterol drug, pulled in the 1960s when cardiovascular events rose despite falling cholesterol). HCG is now preferred over Clomid to preserve testicular function on TRT. [01:37:23](transcript.md#013723-clomid-pituitary-testosterone--cholesterol-anastrozole-hcg)

## Tools & Protocols

### DEXA Scan
- **What:** Dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry — body composition imaging.
- **How:** Annual scan; track body fat %, visceral adipose tissue (VAT), appendicular lean mass index / fat-free mass index, and bone mineral density (BMD). Discard BMI as too crude. Use Z-score (vs. age/sex peers) to track BMD trend; T-score for osteopenia/osteoporosis diagnosis.
- **When:** Starting in early adulthood; annually thereafter.
- **Ref:** [00:16:01](transcript.md#001601-dexa-scan-lean-mass--fat-bone-mineral-density--osteoporosis)

### Comprehensive Blood Work Panel
- **What:** Annual/semi-annual metabolic and hormonal assessment.
- **How:** Full panel: ApoB, Lp(a) (once in early adulthood), fasting glucose/insulin, HbA1c, CBC, hsCRP, full hormone panel (testosterone, free testosterone, estradiol, SHBG, LH, FSH), thyroid (TSH, free T3/T4), comprehensive metabolic panel, lathosterol and desmosterol (for cholesterol synthesis monitoring), phytosterols (to gauge ezetimibe response). For women on a cycle: standardize hormone draw on day 5 of menstrual cycle.
- **When:** Annually for most; 2–4x/year for patients in active intervention. Lp(a) once in early adulthood.
- **Ref:** [00:07:29](transcript.md#000729-lifespan-bloodwork--biomarkers-testing-the-4-horsemen-of-disease)

### VO2 Max Testing (CPET)
- **What:** Cardiopulmonary exercise test measuring peak aerobic capacity.
- **How:** Graded treadmill or cycling protocol to exhaustion. Minimum goal: 75th percentile for age/sex. Elite goal: top 2.5% ("Exceptional"). Bottom-to-top quartile jump = ~5X ACM difference. Use published VO2 max estimator tables if formal CPET unavailable.
- **When:** Annual assessment; train year-round.
- **Ref:** [00:38:31](transcript.md#003831-all-cause-mortality-smoking-strength-vo2-max)

### Zone 2 Cardio Training
- **What:** Sustained aerobic training at lactate ~2 mmol/L (conversational pace).
- **How:** 3–4 sessions/week, 45–60 min each. Minimum threshold for significant Alzheimer's risk reduction: 15 MET hours/week (~3 brisk 1-hour walks) = ~50% risk reduction vs. sedentary baseline.
- **When:** Year-round, as the aerobic training base.
- **Ref:** [00:49:24](transcript.md#004924-importance-of-exercise-brain-health-met-hours)

### Strength Training — "Centenarian Decathlete" Framework
- **What:** Resistance training benchmarks for marginal-decade functional capacity, developed by Beth Lewis at Attia's practice.
- **How:** Benchmark targets at age 40: dead hang — 1.5 min (women), 2 min (men); air squat at 90° — 2 min (both); farmer carry — bodyweight for 2 min (men), 75% BW (women). Also tracks vertical jump, ground contact time, grip strength, leg extension load. Power lifting movements (deadlift, good mornings, leg press at heavy loads) for BMD. Note: "deadlift bodyweight x10" is not an official Attia benchmark — he explicitly said he made it up off-the-cuff.
- **When:** Begin immediately; effective at every age. Never stop.
- **Ref:** [00:44:43](transcript.md#004443-attias-rule-of-supplementation-centenarian-decathlete-physical-goals)

### Back-Casting / Marginal Decade Planning
- **What:** Goal-setting framework anchored to end-of-life physical and cognitive function. Term coined by Annie Duke.
- **How:** Write in exquisite detail every physical and cognitive task you want to perform at age 80–90 (use ~50 prompting questions). Quantify required VO2 max, strength, and stability metrics. Factor in ~8–10% VO2 max decline per decade. That calculation gives your training target today.
- **When:** Ongoing exercise; revisit annually or at major life changes.
- **Ref:** [00:32:16](transcript.md#003216-back-casting-defining-your-marginal-decade)

### ApoB Reduction Protocol
- **What:** Reduce atherogenic particle count to minimize atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) risk.
- **How:** Nutrition — lower saturated fat AND carbohydrates simultaneously; restricting carbs cuts triglycerides (VLDL); restricting saturated fat cuts LDL particles; pure dietary intervention cannot reach <30 mg/dL. Pharmacological ladder: (1) **Ezetimibe** — blocks Niemann-Pick C1-like 1 transporter; phytosterol elevation on labs predicts response. (2) **Statins** — inhibit cholesterol synthesis globally, upregulate hepatic LDL receptors; ~5% reversible muscle soreness; no RCT evidence of cognitive harm. (3) **Bempedoic acid** — liver-selective cholesterol synthesis inhibitor; fewer side effects than statins. (4) **PCSK9 inhibitors** — most potent; biannual injectable dosing available; founded on Hobbs et al. 2006 NEJM discovery. Monitor desmosterol to avoid over-suppression.
- **When:** Dietary first; add pharmacotherapy if ApoB remains above target. High-risk target <60 mg/dL; aspirational <30 mg/dL.
- **Ref:** [02:17:42](transcript.md#021742-apolipoprotein-b-diet-statins--other-cholesterol-prescriptions) | [02:02:51](transcript.md#020251-cholesterol--dietary-cholesterol-saturated-fat-ldl--hdl-apolipoprotein-b)

### Hormone Replacement Therapy — Women
- **What:** Bioidentical estrogen + bioidentical progesterone to replace declining hormones at menopause.
- **How:** Transdermal estradiol via Vivelle-Dot patch or gel (avoids first-pass liver metabolism and oral-estrogen clotting risk). Micronized progesterone (Prometrium) 100–200 mg if uterus intact; for women intolerant of systemic progesterone, use a progesterone-coated IUD for local uterine protection instead. Low-dose topical testosterone for women with very low testosterone complaining of low libido and difficulty gaining muscle — replace to physiologically normal female range only. Annual endometrial ultrasound + pap smear. Avoid synthetic progestins (MPA). Progesterone supplementation during the luteal phase (or full luteal phase) eliminates PMS symptoms caused by the sharp progesterone drop in the final 7 days of the cycle.
- **When:** Initiate at or near menopause onset for maximum brain and cardiovascular protection ("window of opportunity").
- **Ref:** [01:10:10](transcript.md#011010-hormone-replacement-therapy-menopause--breast-cancer-risk) | [01:22:06](transcript.md#012206-estrogen-progesterone--testosterone-therapies-in-women)

### Hormone Replacement Therapy — Men (TRT)
- **What:** Testosterone cypionate injections with optional anastrozole and/or HCG.
- **How:** Subcutaneous testosterone cypionate twice weekly (e.g., 50 mg x2 = physiologic 100 mg/week). Free testosterone is the primary target (~2% of total T, or 80th–90th percentile of physiologic range). Monitor estradiol (target 30–50 pg/mL), hematocrit, PSA. Anastrozole (compounded 0.1 mg) only if estradiol exceeds ~55–60 pg/mL; ~20% of men need it. Never suppress estradiol below 25 pg/mL. HCG (LH analog) to preserve testicular function if desired. Avoid TRT in men still wanting fertility — use HCG alone and bank sperm. Avoid Clomid long-term (desmosterol risk). For needle-phobic patients: Xyosted pre-loaded pen. Check SHBG drivers before adding testosterone: estradiol raises SHBG; insulin lowers it; thyroid dysfunction alters it. Youngest patient Attia placed on TRT: ~40 years old.
- **When:** After confirming hypogonadism via repeat fasting labs, and after optimizing sleep, exercise, and nutrition.
- **Ref:** [01:26:35](transcript.md#012635-hormone-replacement-therapy-in-men-shbg--testosterone-insulin) | [01:37:23](transcript.md#013723-clomid-pituitary-testosterone--cholesterol-anastrozole-hcg) | [01:52:06](transcript.md#015206-testosterone-replacement-therapy--fertility)

### Nicotine (Non-Smoked) for Cognitive Focus
- **What:** Low-dose nicotine via lozenge or patch for acute cognitive enhancement via nicotinic acetylcholine receptors.
- **How:** Nicotine lozenge in targeted high-demand cognitive sessions. Attia cycles on/off; reports no personal addiction. Caution: lozenges deliver 4–8 mg per bolus (vs. ~1 mg per cigarette) — high for naive users. Never via smoking or vaping. Attia also uses 300 mg Alpha-GPC (1–2x/week max) before cognitive work or workouts.
- **When:** Situational use. Not for adolescents, those with cardiovascular disease, or addiction-prone individuals.
- **Ref:** [00:55:23](transcript.md#005523-nicotine--cognitive-focus)

### Rapamycin (Investigational Longevity Protocol)
- **What:** mTOR inhibitor used off-label for potential longevity benefits; also explored for ovarian reserve preservation in women.
- **How:** Intermittent weekly dosing; exact schedule evolving. Supported by data across multiple model organisms. Animal data show preserved ovarian lifespan in mice. Requires physician supervision and monitoring for immunosuppression.
- **When:** Investigational; not standard of care.
- **Ref:** [01:47:46](transcript.md#014746-fadogia-agrestis-supplements-rapamycin)

### GLP-1 Agonists for Metabolic Intervention
- **What:** Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) for obesity and metabolic dysfunction.
- **How:** Weekly subcutaneous injection; titrate over ~16 weeks (studied dose: 2.4 mg/week; Attia often achieves sufficient benefit at 1–2 mg). Mechanism: ~80% central hypothalamic appetite suppression; also improves peripheral insulin sensitivity. Pair with high protein intake and resistance training to mitigate muscle loss. Eliminate liquid calories (especially alcohol) — easy to circumvent the drug's appetite suppression with caloric drinks. Expect weight regain upon cessation with current formulations.
- **When:** For genuine obesity/metabolic dysfunction; not cosmetic weight loss in lean individuals.
- **Ref:** [02:40:44](transcript.md#024044-glp-1--weight-loss)

### BPC-157 (Peptide — Injury Rehabilitation)
- **What:** Synthetic gastric pentadecapeptide with animal model evidence for soft tissue healing.
- **How:** Injectable (subcutaneous or intramuscular near injury site). No established human clinical dosing protocol. Attia personally tried it for ~2 months with a colleague and noticed no effect.
- **When:** Post-injury for tendon/ligament recovery. Evidence base is anecdotal and rodent-model; Attia uncertain of efficacy.
- **Ref:** [02:28:53](transcript.md#022853-peptides-stem-cells-bpc157-prp-platelet-rich-plasma-injury-rehabilitation)

### PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)
- **What:** Patient's own platelets concentrated and injected into injury site to promote healing.
- **How:** Blood draw, centrifuge, ultrasound-guided injection into tendon/joint. Contains few to no stem cells — distinct from stem cell therapy. Possible efficacy signals for early hair loss and certain joint conditions.
- **When:** Tendon, ligament, and joint injuries; possible early hair loss.
- **Ref:** [02:28:53](transcript.md#022853-peptides-stem-cells-bpc157-prp-platelet-rich-plasma-injury-rehabilitation)
