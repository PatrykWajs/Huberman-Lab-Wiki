## [01:29:09] Kratom Alkaloids, Mood & Stimulant Effects, Multiple Pathways for Pain Relief

## [01:38:17] Plant Alkaloids & Chemical Defense, Kratom & Antifungal Alkaloids; Geckos

**Andrew Huberman:** Why do plants make alkaloids in the first place?

**Chris McCurdy:** Plants make alkaloids primarily as a chemical defense mechanism — they're essentially the immune system and defense armament of the plant. They use these compounds to ward off insects, fungi, bacteria, and other predators. The fact that these molecules happen to interact with our neurotransmitter systems is something of a coincidence of evolution — the nitrogen-containing structures that work against insects also happen to bind to receptors in the mammalian nervous system that evolved for entirely different purposes. Kratom, for example, also contains antifungal alkaloids. Animals in nature are often very good at finding plants that contain particular compounds they need — by smell, taste, or other means. Non-human mammals have a much more powerful sense of smell than we do, and many of the compounds in plants have an aroma that serves as a signal. There's something almost remarkable about how biological motifs are used over and over again throughout nature.

## [01:58:03] Kratom, Drug Interactions & Seizure, Opioids

**Andrew Huberman:** Are there drug interactions people should know about, including the risk of seizure?

**Chris McCurdy:** Yes, this is an important area. Kratom alkaloids, particularly mitragynine, are metabolized by the cytochrome P450 enzyme system in the liver — specifically CYP3A4 and others. This means kratom can interact with many medications that use the same metabolic pathways: certain antidepressants, antifungals, HIV medications, and others. When those pathways are inhibited, the levels of kratom alkaloids in the blood can rise unexpectedly, increasing risk. Seizures have been reported in cases involving kratom, particularly when combined with other substances. The seizure risk appears more associated with concentrated products and with polydrug use. Anyone taking prescription medications should consult with a physician before using any kratom product.

## [02:28:44] Nicotine; Natural Products & Career Journey, Salvia divinorum, Kratom
