# Episode 195: Benefits & Risks of Peptide Therapeutics for Physical & Mental Health

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Andrew Huberman provides a comprehensive framework for understanding therapeutic peptides — short chains of amino acids (2–100) with pleiotropic effects used for tissue repair, metabolic enhancement, longevity, and vitality. The episode covers peptide biology basics, sourcing and safety considerations including the underappreciated LPS (lipopolysaccharide) contamination risk, and a systematic review of the most commonly used peptides organized by purpose: tissue rejuvenation (BPC-157, thymosin beta-4/TB-500), growth hormone and metabolism (sermorelin, tesamorelin, CJC-1295, ipamorelin, hexarelin, GHRP-2/-3/-6, MK-677), longevity (epitalon), and vitality/libido (melanotan 1–5, PT-141/Vyleesi, kisspeptin). Huberman discloses his own personal experience with sermorelin (stopped due to REM sleep suppression) and emphasizes throughout that therapeutic peptides are potent compounds with real risks — including tumor growth potential — not innocuous alternatives to hormone therapy. None should be used without physician oversight, clean sourcing, and regular blood monitoring.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Peptides are small proteins (2–100 amino acids) with pleiotropic effects — they rarely do just one thing.** Most activate multiple pathways simultaneously (cellular growth, cell migration, angiogenesis, neuromodulation), making dosing, cycling, and risk assessment inherently complex. Rarely if ever does a peptide have a single targeted effect. [00:07:44](transcript.md#000744-what-is-a-peptide-effects)

2. **Three tiers of peptide access: prescription, gray market, black market.** Prescription sourcing via a board-certified physician — through a pharmaceutical company or compounding pharmacy — is the only route that guarantees LPS removal and compound purity. Black market peptides are frequently contaminated or mislabeled. [00:12:06](transcript.md#001206-peptide-sourcing-lipopolysaccharide-lps)

3. **LPS (lipopolysaccharide) contamination is a serious and underappreciated risk.** LPS accumulates during peptide manufacturing and causes cumulative immune responses that become problematic over time. Gray and black market sources often skip LPS removal. Only pharmaceutical companies and compounding pharmacies reliably clean it out. [00:12:06](transcript.md#001206-peptide-sourcing-lipopolysaccharide-lps)

4. **BPC-157 promotes tissue repair primarily through angiogenesis (VEGF/eNOS upregulation) and fibroblast migration.** Animal data (dating to 1993) is strong; human clinical data is essentially absent. The same mechanism that drives healing — VEGF upregulation — is the exact pathway that cancer drugs like bevacizumab block, creating a real tumor-growth risk. [00:14:48](transcript.md#001448-rejuvenation--tissue-repair-bpc-157-angiogenesis)

5. **BPC-157 typical protocol: 300–500 mcg subcutaneously, 2–3x/week, ~8-week cycles with 8–10 weeks off.** The LD50 is extremely high (~2 g/kg) but that does not justify high doses. Daily continuous use is not recommended. Contraindicated with any concern about existing tumors or cancer history. [00:27:53](transcript.md#002753-bpc-157-safety-doses-cycling-tumor-risk)

6. **Thymosin beta-4 / TB-500 promotes wound repair through stem cell proliferation and extracellular matrix formation — a different mechanism than BPC-157.** It does not stimulate the growth hormone pathway (not growth-promoting). The thymus secretes thymosin beta-4 in youth; as it disappears with age, tissue healing becomes less robust. TB-500 is a truncated synthetic version. Often combined with BPC-157. Primarily animal data; strong anecdotal human reports. [00:36:43](transcript.md#003643-tissue-repair-thymosin-beta-4-tb-500)

7. **Growth hormone declines ~15% per decade after age 30.** It is secreted primarily during early sleep and promotes fat loss, muscle growth, ATP production, mood, and overall vitality. Secretagogue peptides stimulate endogenous GH release rather than replacing it directly, avoiding direct pituitary negative-feedback suppression — but still carry tumor-growth and receptor-desensitization risks. [00:40:49](transcript.md#004049-growth--metabolism-growth-hormone-igf-1-risks)

8. **Category 1 GH secretagogues (sermorelin, tesamorelin) are FDA approved and generally safer than Category 2.** Sermorelin increases deep non-REM sleep but may reduce REM sleep — Huberman stopped using it for this reason. Tesamorelin is longer-acting (~3x/week). CJC-1295 had a cardiovascular-related death in clinical trials and should be avoided until safety issues are resolved. [00:45:25](transcript.md#004525-secretagogues-sermorelin-tesamorelin-cjc-1295)

9. **Category 2 GH secretagogues work via ghrelin mimicry and carry more side-effect risk.** Ipamorelin increases GH directly and suppresses somatostatin (removing the brake). Hexarelin produces GH pulses 2–3x greater than other peptides but can permanently desensitize GH releasing hormone receptors — a potentially irreversible consequence. GHRP-3 can more than double circulating cortisol. All Category 2 options raise hunger; several raise cortisol and prolactin. [00:53:44](transcript.md#005344-ipamorelin-hexarelin-ghrp-3-mk-677-risks--timing)

10. **MK-677 is an oral ghrelin mimetic with the same Category 2 risk profile as injectable GHRPs.** It raises GH and IGF-1 without injections but also elevates cortisol and prolactin, and significantly increases hunger. Cycled use with monitoring is essential. [00:53:44](transcript.md#005344-ipamorelin-hexarelin-ghrp-3-mk-677-risks--timing)

11. **GH secretagogues are always taken at night, at least 90 minutes after eating, 20–30 minutes before sleep.** Elevated blood sugar or food in the gut reduces potency. Physicians often combine Category 1 and Category 2 compounds (e.g., sermorelin + ipamorelin) for complementary mechanisms, but relative dosing must be managed to avoid excess prolactin and cortisol. [00:59:09](transcript.md#005909-peptides-for-growth-hormone--igf-1-risk-combinations--dosing)

12. **Augmenting growth hormone — via direct injection or secretagogues — increases tumor and cancer risk.** GH and IGF-1 are indiscriminate: they promote growth of all tissues including existing tumors. Combining GH secretagogues with BPC-157 compounds this risk by adding vascularization on top of tissue growth stimulation. Anyone under 30 has little reason to augment GH. [00:59:09](transcript.md#005909-peptides-for-growth-hormone--igf-1-risk-combinations--dosing)

13. **Epitalon (epithalon) is the primary longevity-focused peptide, mimicking epithalamin from the pineal gland.** Animal data suggests it suppresses tumor growth, extends telomere length, and recalibrates melatonin and circadian rhythms that degrade with age. Epithalamin — like melatonin — decreases with age, associated with increased tissue inflammation. No human clinical trials demonstrate life extension. [01:06:12](transcript.md#010612-longevity-thymosin-beta-4-epitalon-epithalon)

14. **Melanotan peptides target the melanocortin system to increase skin pigmentation, mood, libido, and decrease appetite.** Sunlight (UVB) naturally activates this pathway via hypothalamus → pituitary → MSH → melanocytes + dopamine release. Melanotan 1 does not cross the blood-brain barrier (tanning only); melanotans 2–5 cross it and additionally increase mood and libido. Contraindicated with active melanoma. Side effects: nausea, flushing, blood pressure elevation. [01:12:09](transcript.md#011209-vitality-melanotan-pt-141-vyleesi-risks)

15. **PT-141 (Vyleesi/bremelanotide) is FDA approved for premenopausal hypoactive sexual desire disorder in women.** It crosses the blood-brain barrier, activates melanocortin receptors, and increases libido and mood. Also prescribed off-label for men. Same side-effect and contraindication profile as other melanotans. [01:12:09](transcript.md#011209-vitality-melanotan-pt-141-vyleesi-risks)

16. **Kisspeptin sits upstream of the GnRH → LH/FSH → testosterone/estrogen cascade.** It activates puberty and in adults supports vitality and libido via downstream steroid hormones. Prescribed for hypothalamic amenorrhea; kisspeptin antagonists treat menopausal vasomotor symptoms (night sweats). Pleiotropic effects are not yet fully mapped — use only with experienced physicians. [01:17:21](transcript.md#011721-vitality-kisspeptin)

## Tools & Protocols

### BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)

- **What:** A 15-amino-acid peptide originally found in gastric juice. Promotes tissue repair through angiogenesis (VEGF/eNOS upregulation), fibroblast migration, and growth hormone receptor upregulation at injury sites. Originally derived from the observation that gastric milieu preserves and rejuvenates tissues.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection (most common; typically a few inches from the belly button), intramuscular injection, or oral. Can be delivered systemically or locally near the injury site — anecdotal reports favor local, but no controlled evidence. Dose: 300–500 mcg per injection.
- **When:** 2–3 injections per week. Cycle: ~8 weeks on, 8–10 weeks off. Do not use continuously without cycling. Contraindicated if concerned about any existing tumor, cancer history, or elevated VEGF.
- **Ref:** [Achilles tendon study](https://bit.ly/3TYHRoB) | [Musculoskeletal healing review](https://bit.ly/3xcwRLc) | [eNOS / vasomotor pathway](https://go.nature.com/43DYYz2) | [Wound healing](https://bit.ly/3J0sS77) | [00:14:48](transcript.md#001448-rejuvenation--tissue-repair-bpc-157-angiogenesis) | [00:21:50](transcript.md#002150-bpc-157--tissue-injury-mode-of-delivery) | [00:27:53](transcript.md#002753-bpc-157-safety-doses-cycling-tumor-risk)

---

### TB-500 / Thymosin Beta-4

- **What:** TB-500 is a truncated synthetic version of thymosin beta-4, a peptide naturally secreted from the thymus during youth. Promotes tissue repair via stem cell proliferation and extracellular matrix formation. Does not affect the growth hormone pathway.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection, often combined with BPC-157 for synergistic tissue repair.
- **When:** Cycled similarly to BPC-157. Primarily used during active injury recovery.
- **Ref:** [00:36:43](transcript.md#003643-tissue-repair-thymosin-beta-4-tb-500)

---

### Sermorelin (Category 1 GH Secretagogue)

- **What:** Synthetic analog of growth hormone releasing hormone (GHRH). FDA approved for short stature; used off-label to increase GH and IGF-1. Increases deep non-REM sleep; may reduce REM sleep (Huberman stopped using it for this reason).
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection at night, at least 90 minutes after last meal and 20–30 minutes before sleep. Do not eat for 30+ minutes after dosing. Dose: 200–400 mcg per injection.
- **When:** 3–5 nights per week. Daily use for extended periods causes receptor desensitization. Huberman took it 1–2 nights/week.
- **Ref:** [00:45:25](transcript.md#004525-secretagogues-sermorelin-tesamorelin-cjc-1295)

---

### Tesamorelin (Category 1 GH Secretagogue)

- **What:** Synthetic GHRH analog. FDA approved (brand name: Egrifta) for reduction of visceral adiposity in HIV patients. Longer-acting than sermorelin due to slight amino acid sequence differences.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection at night, at least 90 minutes after eating.
- **When:** Approximately 3 times per week due to longer duration of action. Cycle and monitor similarly to sermorelin.
- **Ref:** [00:45:25](transcript.md#004525-secretagogues-sermorelin-tesamorelin-cjc-1295)

---

### CJC-1295 (Category 1 GH Secretagogue — Use With Caution)

- **What:** A GHRH variant with a drug affinity complex (DAC) that makes it very long-lasting. Had a cardiovascular-related death in clinical trials, possibly linked to fluid retention and increased fluid volume. Huberman advises against choosing CJC-1295 when safer alternatives (sermorelin, tesamorelin) exist.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection. Prescription only.
- **When:** Once or twice per week due to long duration. Avoid until safety issues are resolved.
- **Ref:** [00:45:25](transcript.md#004525-secretagogues-sermorelin-tesamorelin-cjc-1295)

---

### Ipamorelin (Category 2 GH Secretagogue)

- **What:** A ghrelin-mimicking peptide that increases GH release via two mechanisms: direct GH stimulation and somatostatin suppression (removing the natural brake on GH release). Improves deep sleep. Lower cortisol and prolactin effects compared to other Category 2 options. Do not confuse with tesamorelin (Category 1).
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection before bed, at least 90 minutes after eating. Often combined with a Category 1 secretagogue.
- **When:** Typically 3–5x/week before sleep. Cycle to avoid desensitization.
- **Ref:** [00:53:44](transcript.md#005344-ipamorelin-hexarelin-ghrp-3-mk-677-risks--timing)

---

### Hexarelin (Category 2 GH Secretagogue — High Risk)

- **What:** The most potent GH secretagogue — GH pulses 2–3x greater than other options. Also raises prolactin (suppresses libido, causes fluid retention, malaise). Most dangerous Category 2 option: can permanently desensitize GH releasing hormone receptors, potentially shutting down the entire GH axis indefinitely.
- **How:** Prescription only. Low doses only. Must be used under physician supervision with prolactin monitoring.
- **When:** Use with extreme caution; not at high doses. Physician oversight and regular monitoring essential.
- **Ref:** [00:53:44](transcript.md#005344-ipamorelin-hexarelin-ghrp-3-mk-677-risks--timing)

---

### GHRP-2, GHRP-3, GHRP-6 (Category 2 GH Secretagogues)

- **What:** Different amino acid sequences all designed to increase GH via ghrelin pathway stimulation. GHRP-3 in particular can more than double circulating cortisol and potently increases prolactin. All raise hunger. Receptor desensitization risk applies.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection at night, 90+ minutes after eating.
- **When:** Cycled; monitor cortisol and prolactin levels closely. Cortisol elevation is problematic when taken late at night (want cortisol low at night).
- **Ref:** [00:53:44](transcript.md#005344-ipamorelin-hexarelin-ghrp-3-mk-677-risks--timing)

---

### MK-677 (Oral GH Secretagogue)

- **What:** An oral ghrelin mimetic. Increases GH and IGF-1 similarly to injectable GHRPs without injections. Raises cortisol and prolactin; significantly increases hunger. Same Category 2 risk profile applies.
- **How:** Oral tablet at night.
- **When:** Cycled; monitor cortisol and prolactin. Avoid if sensitive to hunger-stimulating or cortisol-raising compounds.
- **Ref:** [MK-677 (catabolism reversal)](https://bit.ly/3PInRnA) | [MK-677 (fat-free mass and energy expenditure)](https://bit.ly/3PJKrfB) | [00:53:44](transcript.md#005344-ipamorelin-hexarelin-ghrp-3-mk-677-risks--timing)

---

### Epitalon (Epithalon)

- **What:** Synthetic tetrapeptide mimicking epithalamin, naturally secreted from the pineal gland early in life. Proposed mechanisms: telomere elongation, tumor suppression, recalibration of circadian rhythms and melatonin patterns that degrade with age. Epithalamin — like melatonin — decreases with age, and its loss is associated with increased tissue inflammation. Entirely animal data — no human longevity trials.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection. Available via compounding pharmacies. No standardized human clinical regimen established.
- **When:** Used as a longevity/anti-aging agent; typically cycled. Often taken at night given its pineal/melatonin context.
- **Ref:** [Age-related pineal gland changes](https://bit.ly/3TGYzHv) | [01:06:12](transcript.md#010612-longevity-thymosin-beta-4-epitalon-epithalon)

---

### Melanotan Peptides (Melanocortin System)

- **What:** Five synthetic melanocyte-stimulating hormone (MSH) analogs — melanotan 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 — each activating different melanocortin receptors to varying degrees. The melanocortin system is naturally activated by sunlight (especially UVB), driving skin pigmentation and dopamine release. Melanotan 1 does not cross the blood-brain barrier (tanning only). Melanotans 2–5 cross the blood-brain barrier and additionally increase mood, libido, and decrease appetite. Side effects: nausea (MSH receptors throughout the gut), flushing, blood pressure increases.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection. Available by prescription. Dosing varies by compound.
- **When:** As needed or on a cycle, depending on goal (pigmentation vs. libido/mood). Contraindicated with active melanoma.
- **Ref:** [01:12:09](transcript.md#011209-vitality-melanotan-pt-141-vyleesi-risks)

---

### PT-141 / Vyleesi (Bremelanotide)

- **What:** A melanocortin receptor agonist. FDA approved (brand name: Vyleesi) for premenopausal hypoactive sexual desire disorder in women. Prescribed off-label for men. Increases libido and mood by crossing the blood-brain barrier. Also causes skin pigmentation. Side effects: nausea, flushing, blood pressure elevation.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection, typically 45 minutes before anticipated sexual activity. Physician prescription required.
- **When:** As needed; not a daily compound. Contraindicated with active melanoma.
- **Ref:** [01:12:09](transcript.md#011209-vitality-melanotan-pt-141-vyleesi-risks)

---

### Kisspeptin

- **What:** Naturally occurring hypothalamic peptide upstream of the GnRH → LH/FSH → testosterone/estrogen cascade. Activates puberty; in adults promotes vitality and libido via downstream steroid hormone stimulation. Prescribed for hypothalamic amenorrhea. Kisspeptin antagonists treat menopausal vasomotor symptoms (e.g., night sweats). Pleiotropic effects not yet fully characterized.
- **How:** Synthetic kisspeptin administered by injection. Physician prescription required.
- **When:** Prescribed context. Limited clinical data for off-label vitality use — work only with experienced physicians familiar with its pleiotropic effects.
- **Ref:** [Kisspeptin signalling in humans](https://bit.ly/4cBfMeh) | [01:17:21](transcript.md#011721-vitality-kisspeptin)
