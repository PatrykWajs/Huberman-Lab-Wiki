# Episode 232: Peptide & Hormone Therapies for Health, Performance & Longevity | Dr. Craig Koniver

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Craig Koniver, M.D. (Brown University / Thomas Jefferson University), joins Andrew Huberman to provide a comprehensive map of peptide and hormone therapies used in performance medicine. The conversation covers GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) for weight loss and inflammation — including microdosing strategies to preserve muscle mass — followed by BPC-157 and its post-ban analog Pentadeca Arginate (PDA) for tissue repair. Growth hormone secretagogues (ipamorelin, GHRP-6, hexarelin, tesamorelin, sermorelin, CJC-1295) are mapped for sleep optimization and body composition, alongside compounds for cognition and immune function including methylene blue, cerebrolysin, and thymosin alpha-1. Foundational supplements — CoQ10, methylated B vitamins, NAD/NMN/NR, glycine, and pinealon — are covered for mitochondrial health, methylation, and REM sleep. Sourcing standards (compounding pharmacy vs. gray market), LPS contamination risks, FDA Category 2 dynamics, testosterone therapy, and the necessity of physician-guided baseline labs are recurring themes throughout.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Peptides are chains of 40 or fewer amino acids; the body makes ~300,000 of them.** Only ~150 have been used therapeutically. Unlike steroids, peptides generally do not suppress endogenous hormone production via negative feedback. [00:07:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=460s)(transcript.md#000740-what-is-a-peptide)

2. **GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) were first approved for type 2 diabetes, then repurposed for weight loss.** Anti-inflammatory and emerging cognitive benefits are also observed — including reduced Hashimoto's antibody levels in autoimmune patients. [00:09:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=577s)(transcript.md#000937-glp-1-agonists-semaglutide-weight-loss-brain-health)

3. **Microdosing GLP-1s (< 2 lbs/week loss) prevents muscle loss.** Compounded tirzepatide/semaglutide enables custom low starting doses. Pair with protein and resistance training; sermorelin can be co-compounded with tirzepatide to offset muscle loss via GH stimulation. [00:15:49](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=949s)(transcript.md#001549-glp-1-microdoses-muscle-loss-inflammation)

4. **BPC-157 is a systemic anti-inflammatory that upregulates GH receptors and repairs tendons and ligaments.** Injectable subcutaneous delivery provides broader systemic benefit than oral capsules; can be injected directly into tendons (unlike steroids) and combined with PRP/PRF. Oral form is best for GI conditions. Protocol: 500–5,000 mcg/day, 5 days on / 2 days off. [00:18:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=1123s)(transcript.md#001843-bpc-157-inflammation) / [00:23:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=1407s)(transcript.md#002327-bpc-157-injection--oral-forms-injury-repair)

5. **Compounding pharmacy sourcing is non-negotiable — gray-market "research use only" peptides contain LPS.** Lipopolysaccharide (endotoxin) accumulates cumulatively and drives chronic inflammation; anaphylactic reactions have occurred. Compounding pharmacies are FDA- and state Board of Pharmacy-regulated and allow custom dose combinations including multi-peptide stacks in a single injection. [00:34:48](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=2088s)(transcript.md#003448-black--gray-market-compounding-pharmacies-purity)

6. **Pentadeca Arginate (PDA) is the BPC-157 substitute available post-FDA Category 2 ban.** One amino acid substitution (acetate → arginate) on the same 15-amino-acid gastric-derived sequence. Early clinical results show near-identical anti-inflammatory effects. Starting dose: 250–500 mcg/day injected Monday–Friday. [00:43:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=2580s)(transcript.md#004300-bpc-157-pentadeca-arginate-pda-side-effects--doses)

7. **Ipamorelin is the preferred entry-point GH secretagogue — pure GH pulse without cortisol or prolactin elevation.** Taken at bedtime with no carbohydrates in the prior 45 minutes at 100–300 mcg. GHRP-6 adds a strong hunger signal via ghrelin — useful for mass gain, counterproductive otherwise. Stack either with a GHRH (tesamorelin, sermorelin, CJC-1295) for synergistic pulse + amplitude effect. [00:46:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=2795s)(transcript.md#004635-ipamorelin-ghrp-6-sleep-appetite-tool-sleep--growth-hormone)

8. **Hexarelin is an energy-endurance GH secretagogue best taken in the morning.** Unlike GHRP-6 it does not spike appetite. Provides clean, non-jittery energy; has cardioprotective effects via CD36 receptor activation. Dose: 100 mcg subcutaneous. [01:19:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=4772s)(transcript.md#011932-hexarelin-growth-hormone-secretagogues-dosing)

9. **Tesamorelin targets visceral fat reduction; sermorelin covers a broad dosing range.** Tesamorelin (FDA-approved for HIV lipodystrophy) appears more effective in females; 2 mg/night. Sermorelin: 200–3,000 mcg/night. CJC-1295 (DAC) extends peptide half-life and was recently re-added to the compounding-approved list. Stacking: combine 3–7 peptides in one daily injection. [00:54:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=3257s)(transcript.md#005417-tesamorelin-sermorelin-cjc-1295-stacking-peptides)

10. **CoQ10 is an electron carrier at cytochrome 3 and active across all five mitochondrial cytochrome complexes.** Most people bottleneck at cytochrome 1 (NAD) but some bottleneck at cytochrome 3 (CoQ10) or cytochrome 4 (methylene blue). CoQ10 is safe up to 2,400 mg/day; fat-soluble — take with a fat-containing meal. [01:01:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=3714s)(transcript.md#010154-coenzyme-q10-coq10-mitochondrial-health)

11. **Methylated B vitamins plus TMG normalize homocysteine — a strong cardiovascular and cognitive risk marker.** The MTHFR SNP impairs methylation; pre-methylated forms bypass this. Take in the morning; use again at the 3pm energy slump instead of coffee. [01:21:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=4870s)(transcript.md#012110-methylated-b-vitamins-homocysteine)

12. **Pinealon dramatically increases REM sleep — Huberman reported doubling REM from ~1 hour to ~3 hours when combined with injectable glycine.** Pinealon stimulates pinealocytes (melatonin-producing cells) and improves sleep even on nights not taken. Epitalon (companion peptide for telomere support and circadian rhythm) is now on the do-not-compound list. [01:24:47](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=5087s)(transcript.md#012447-peptides-for-sleep-pinealon-epitalon)

13. **Methylene blue works at cytochrome 4 — donates electrons directly to cytochrome C oxidase, bypassing complex I–III blockages.** Used for cognitive enhancement, post-COVID brain fog, mitochondrial dysfunction. Must be pharmaceutical grade. Starting dose: 1–2 mcg/kg body weight (~0.5–1 mg for most people). Do NOT combine with SSRIs, SNRIs, or MAOIs (serotonin syndrome risk). Turns urine and mouth blue transiently. [01:44:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=6257s)(transcript.md#014417-peptides-for-cognitive-function-methylene-blue-doses)

14. **NAD IV produced dramatic post-COVID recoveries; NMN is the more efficient oral precursor over NR.** IV: 250–1,000 mg over 2–4 hours; initial course of 5–10 infusions over a few weeks, then taper to monthly or quarterly. NMN: 500–1,000 mg/day. NMN is one biosynthetic step from NAD; NR is two steps — NMN is more direct and efficient. [01:50:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=6620s)(transcript.md#015020-covid-nad-infusion-nmn--nr-supplements) / [01:57:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=7033s)(transcript.md#015713-nutritional-deficiencies-nad-dose--regimen-nmn--nr)

15. **PT-141 (bremelanotide / Vyleesi) acts centrally in the brain to increase libido in both sexes.** 0.5–1 mg subcutaneous 30–45 min before activity. Main side effect: nausea (manage with hydration and anti-nausea medication). FDA-approved as Vyleesi for premenopausal women; compounded form no longer available. [02:07:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=7673s)(transcript.md#020753-pt-141-vyleesi-libido-nausea)

16. **FDA Category 2 removals are driven by both legitimate safety concerns and pharmaceutical lobbying.** Off-patent compounded peptides compete at a fraction of pharmaceutical prices. Three recently re-allowed: CJC-1295, ipamorelin, thymosin alpha-1. Testosterone and nandrolone (Deca) are the only officially prescribable androgens; most other anabolic steroids cannot be legitimately prescribed. [02:10:57](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=7857s)(transcript.md#021057-fda-approval--removal-pharmaceutical-companies)

## Tools & Protocols

### GLP-1 Microdosing (Tirzepatide / Semaglutide)
- **What:** GLP-1 / GIP receptor agonist for weight loss, inflammation reduction, and cognitive benefit.
- **How:** Obtain compounded tirzepatide or semaglutide. Start at the lowest possible dose; titrate slowly targeting < 2 lbs/week weight loss. Pair with adequate protein and resistance training. Co-compound with sermorelin to offset muscle loss.
- **When:** Ongoing; re-evaluate every 4–8 weeks.
- **Ref:** [00:15:49](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=949s)(transcript.md#001549-glp-1-microdoses-muscle-loss-inflammation)

### BPC-157 Subcutaneous Injection
- **What:** Body Protection Compound 157 — anti-inflammatory, tissue repair, GH receptor upregulator.
- **How:** Compounded BPC-157, 500–5,000 mcg/day via subcutaneous injection (30–31 gauge insulin needle, abdomen or glute). Protocol: 5 days on / 2 days off. For localized injury: inject near site. Mix with PRP/PRF for direct tendon injection.
- **When:** Acute injury (short intensive course) or chronic inflammation (longer cycle). Off-label; requires physician.
- **Ref:** [00:23:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=1407s)(transcript.md#002327-bpc-157-injection--oral-forms-injury-repair)

### BPC-157 Oral (GI-specific use)
- **What:** Oral capsule form; survives stomach acid as BPC-157 is a native gut peptide. Best for GI conditions (IBD, Crohn's, ulcerative colitis, IBS, leaky gut).
- **How:** Capsule form on empty stomach. Injectable preferred for systemic effects.
- **When:** GI-focused use cases.
- **Ref:** [00:23:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=1407s)(transcript.md#002327-bpc-157-injection--oral-forms-injury-repair)

### Pentadeca Arginate (PDA)
- **What:** BPC-157 analog; 15-amino-acid gastric-derived peptide available post-Category-2 ban (one amino acid substitution: acetate → arginate).
- **How:** 250–500 mcg/day subcutaneous injection Monday–Friday (weekends off).
- **When:** Use when BPC-157 unavailable through compounding pharmacy; same anti-inflammatory and tissue-repair indications.
- **Ref:** [00:43:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=2580s)(transcript.md#004300-bpc-157-pentadeca-arginate-pda-side-effects--doses)

### Ipamorelin (Sleep + GH Optimization)
- **What:** GHRP — growth hormone releasing peptide; pure GH pulse without cortisol/prolactin elevation.
- **How:** 100–300 mcg subcutaneous injection at bedtime. No carbohydrates 45 min prior. Stack with a GHRH (tesamorelin, sermorelin, or CJC-1295 w/ DAC) for synergistic effect.
- **When:** Nightly at bedtime. Cycle: 5 days on / 2 days off.
- **Ref:** [00:46:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=2795s)(transcript.md#004635-ipamorelin-ghrp-6-sleep-appetite-tool-sleep--growth-hormone)

### GHRP-6 (Appetite + GH)
- **What:** Growth hormone releasing peptide with strong appetite-stimulating ghrelin activity; also elevates cortisol and prolactin.
- **How:** 100 mcg subcutaneous injection. Increases appetite substantially.
- **When:** Best for individuals needing to gain mass or with appetite suppression. Avoid if appetite control is a goal or sleep disruption is a concern.
- **Ref:** [00:46:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=2795s)(transcript.md#004635-ipamorelin-ghrp-6-sleep-appetite-tool-sleep--growth-hormone)

### Hexarelin (Morning Energy + GH)
- **What:** GHRP with clean energy-endurance effect; does not spike appetite; cardioprotective via CD36 receptor activation.
- **How:** 100 mcg subcutaneous injection in the morning.
- **When:** Morning use for energy and endurance; suitable when appetite stimulation is undesirable.
- **Ref:** [01:19:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=4772s)(transcript.md#011932-hexarelin-growth-hormone-secretagogues-dosing)

### Tesamorelin / Sermorelin / CJC-1295 (GHRH Stack)
- **What:** Growth hormone releasing hormones — stimulate pituitary GH secretion via GHRH pathway.
- **How:** Tesamorelin: 2 mg (2,000 mcg)/night subcutaneous. Sermorelin: 200–3,000 mcg/night depending on goals. CJC-1295 with DAC extends GH pulse duration. Stack with ipamorelin for pulse + amplitude effect. 5 days on / 2 days off.
- **When:** Anti-aging, body composition, visceral fat reduction, recovery. Tesamorelin appears more effective in females. CJC-1295 recently re-added to compounding-approved list.
- **Ref:** [00:54:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=3257s)(transcript.md#005417-tesamorelin-sermorelin-cjc-1295-stacking-peptides)

### MK-677 (Ibutamoren) — Oral GH Secretagogue
- **What:** Oral ghrelin mimetic; functionally equivalent to GHRP-6 but taken orally. Not a SARM.
- **How:** Oral; increases appetite substantially. Avoid taking at bedtime if sleep disruption is a concern.
- **When:** Alternative to injectable GH peptides; most useful for older adults who cannot gain muscle mass despite optimal lifestyle. Currently on the FDA do-not-compound list.
- **Ref:** [01:17:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=4633s)(transcript.md#011713-mk-677-appetite)

### CoQ10 (Mitochondrial Support)
- **What:** Ubiquinol/ubiquinone — electron carrier at cytochrome 3 (CoQ10 + vitamin K2) and active across all five mitochondrial cytochrome complexes.
- **How:** Take with a fat-containing meal (fat-soluble). Safe up to 2,400 mg/day per research.
- **When:** Daily foundational supplement, especially for anyone on statins, with fatigue, migraine, cardiovascular disease, or neurodegenerative conditions.
- **Ref:** [01:01:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=3714s)(transcript.md#010154-coenzyme-q10-coq10-mitochondrial-health)

### Methylated B Vitamins + Homocysteine Protocol
- **What:** Pre-methylated B12 (methylcobalamin), methylfolate, TMG (trimethylglycine), and methionine to lower homocysteine and support methylation/liver detox.
- **How:** Methylated B vitamin complex in the morning. Add TMG as methyl donor. Check homocysteine at baseline; target < 7–9 umol/L (lab-dependent). Use again at 3pm instead of coffee.
- **When:** Anyone with elevated homocysteine, suspected MTHFR variants, cardiovascular risk, or cognitive concerns. Run labs first.
- **Ref:** [01:21:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=4870s)(transcript.md#012110-methylated-b-vitamins-homocysteine)

### Pinealon (REM Sleep)
- **What:** Tripeptide peptide bioregulator targeting pinealocytes; dramatically enhances REM sleep. Improves sleep even on nights not taken.
- **How:** Injectable, combined with injectable glycine (50–100 mg) at bedtime for synergistic effect.
- **When:** Sleep quality intervention; best for poor REM or age-related sleep disruption. Pinealon remains on the compounding-allowed list (unlike Epitalon which is now banned from compounding).
- **Ref:** [01:24:47](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=5087s)(transcript.md#012447-peptides-for-sleep-pinealon-epitalon)

### Glycine (Liver Detox + Sleep Synergy)
- **What:** Amino acid; cofactor for phase II liver detoxification; promotes GABA-like calming and augments pinealon's sleep effect.
- **How:** Injectable: 50–100 mg per injection alongside pinealon at bedtime.
- **When:** Nightly alongside pinealon for REM enhancement; also used for liver detox support.
- **Ref:** [01:31:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=5463s)(transcript.md#013103-glycine-liver-detoxification-dosage)

### NAD IV Infusion
- **What:** Intravenous nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide — highest bioavailability delivery for NAD repletion.
- **How:** 250–1,000 mg IV over 2–4 hours. Initial course: 5–10 infusions over a few weeks; taper to monthly or quarterly for maintenance. Side effects (flushing, chest tightness) are uncomfortable but not dangerous.
- **When:** Acute recovery (post-viral/COVID, TBI), addiction recovery, or when oral precursors are insufficient. Requires clinic.
- **Ref:** [01:50:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=6620s)(transcript.md#015020-covid-nad-infusion-nmn--nr-supplements)

### Oral NAD Precursors (NMN / NR)
- **What:** NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) and NR (nicotinamide riboside) as oral NAD boosters.
- **How:** NMN: 500–1,000 mg/day. NMN is one biosynthetic step from NAD; NR is two steps — NMN is more direct and efficient. Effect is real but smaller than IV.
- **When:** Daily supplementation for energy, DNA repair, and longevity support.
- **Ref:** [01:57:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=7033s)(transcript.md#015713-nutritional-deficiencies-nad-dose--regimen-nmn--nr)

### Methylene Blue (Cognitive + Mitochondrial)
- **What:** Phenothiazine compound; donates electrons directly at cytochrome C oxidase (cytochrome 4), bypassing blockages at complexes I–III. Supports cognitive function.
- **How:** Pharmaceutical grade only (not industrial). Starting dose: 1–2 mcg/kg body weight (~0.5–1 mg for most people). Turns urine and mouth blue transiently — normal and safe. Do NOT combine with SSRIs, SNRIs, or MAOIs (serotonin syndrome risk).
- **When:** Cognitive enhancement, post-COVID brain fog, mitochondrial dysfunction. Clinic-administered preferred.
- **Ref:** [01:44:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=6257s)(transcript.md#014417-peptides-for-cognitive-function-methylene-blue-doses)

### Thymosin Alpha-1 (Immune Modulation)
- **What:** 28-amino-acid thymic peptide; upregulates T-cell function and immune surveillance.
- **How:** Subcutaneous injection. Recently re-added to compounding-approved list alongside CJC-1295 and ipamorelin.
- **When:** Immune deficiency, post-viral immune dysregulation, autoimmune modulation. Requires physician.
- **Ref:** [01:41:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=6078s)(transcript.md#014118-thymosin-alpha-1-cerebrolysin--brain-health)

### Cerebrolysin (Neuroprotection)
- **What:** Peptide mixture derived from porcine brain proteins; contains neurotrophic factors (BDNF-like, NGF) that support neuronal growth, repair, and connectivity.
- **How:** IV infusion or intramuscular injection in clinic. Widely used in Europe and Russia; limited US availability.
- **When:** Traumatic brain injury, post-COVID brain fog, age-related cognitive decline, post-stroke recovery.
- **Ref:** [01:41:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=6078s)(transcript.md#014118-thymosin-alpha-1-cerebrolysin--brain-health)

### PT-141 / Bremelanotide (Libido)
- **What:** Melanocortin receptor agonist; acts centrally in the brain (not genitally) to increase libido in both men and women.
- **How:** 0.5–1 mg subcutaneous injection 30–45 min before sexual activity. Manage nausea (primary side effect) with hydration and anti-nausea medications if needed.
- **When:** Low libido in men and women; works independently of testosterone levels. Brand-name Vyleesi (FDA-approved for premenopausal women); compounded form no longer available.
- **Ref:** [02:07:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=7673s)(transcript.md#020753-pt-141-vyleesi-libido-nausea)

### Testosterone Therapy
- **What:** Testosterone cypionate or enanthate (injectable); cream/gel also available. Nandrolone (Deca) is the only other anabolic steroid that can be officially prescribed alongside testosterone.
- **How:** Dose individualized by baseline labs (total T, free T, SHBG, estradiol, DHT, CBC, metabolic panel). Women use much lower doses, typically cream.
- **When:** Confirmed hypogonadism with symptoms. Caution: testosterone suppresses sperm production. Young men (20s–30s) with adequate sleep, nutrition, and training generally do not need exogenous testosterone.
- **Ref:** [00:28:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wRsX_ZkzxvQ&t=1723s)(transcript.md#002843-sourcing-anabolic-steroids-testosterone)
