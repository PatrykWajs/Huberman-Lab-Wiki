# Episode 328: LIVE EVENT Q&A — Dr. Andrew Huberman Question & Answer in Los Angeles, CA

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Recorded at The Wiltern theater in Los Angeles as part of The Brain Body Contract lecture series, this episode is the full Q&A session from that live event. Huberman answers audience questions spanning ADHD, cold exposure, EMDR, gut health, psilocybin, motivation, discipline, achieving dreams, sauna physiology, and lessons from skateboarding and Richard Feynman. No lecture content is included — this is purely the interactive Q&A portion of the evening.

## Key Takeaways

- **ADHD involves a higher dopamine threshold, not a focus deficit:** People with ADHD can focus intensely on things they enjoy, proving the capacity is present. Non-medication approaches include visual focus exercises (1–3 minutes on a single point), a 13-minute daily breathing meditation shown by Wendy Suzuki's NYU lab to significantly improve focus, and space-time bridging — shifting attention from internal (eyes closed, behind the forehead) to progressively external focal points. This alters time perception and reduces stress-induced attentional locking. [00:02:23](transcript.md#what-occurs-in-the-mindbody-when-you-have-adhd-are-there-ways-to-address-it-without-medication)

- **Teenager self-optimization starts with awareness, not protocols:** Prioritize sleep above everything. Develop a keen sense of what raises your adrenaline, dopamine, and serotonin and what suppresses them. At 15, neuroplasticity is at a peak — learn your own nervous system's patterns first before reaching for any specific tools. [00:11:55](transcript.md#as-a-teenager-what-are-5-things-you-would-recommend-to-physically-feel-my-best)

- **Cold exposure dopamine is dose-dependent on mental discomfort:** The goal is to reach the "I really want to get out but I know I can stay" threshold — not panic. The degree of mental discomfort is directly predictive of the subsequent dopamine surge. Cold exposure produces a 100–200% rise in dopamine lasting many hours (per European Journal of Physiology) — a slow, sustained arc that functions as a genuine antidepressant. Dr. Anna Lembke documented patients using cold water therapy to exit depressive phases in her book Dopamine Nation. [00:14:42](transcript.md#should-we-wait-to-feel-the-rise-of-adrenaline-and-the-fall-of-it-before-bailing-from-cold-water)

- **Building confidence under stress requires keeping the prefrontal cortex as coach:** Stress inverts the brain's normal hierarchy — the insula (internal body monitoring) overrides the prefrontal cortex (creative, rule-generating thinking). The dopamine system is context-sensitive: reframing a failure as a partial win, if genuinely supportable, produces a real neurochemical reward — as demonstrated by Viktor Frankl and Nelson Mandela under extreme constraint. [00:14:42](transcript.md#should-we-wait-to-feel-the-rise-of-adrenaline-and-the-fall-of-it-before-bailing-from-cold-water)

- **EMDR works by pairing amygdala suppression with trauma recall:** Lateral eye movements potently suppress activity in the amygdala. Walking through space naturally produces these lateral micro-movements unless you are tracking a fixed target. EMDR pairs this amygdala-quieted state with trauma recollection — behavioral desensitization in reverse Pavlovian fashion. It works best for single-event traumas. The future of trauma treatment will likely be combination therapies: neuroplasticity-boosting pharmacology combined with behavioral interventions like EMDR. [00:24:03](transcript.md#what-is-the-competing-mechanism-behind-bilateral-eye-movement-emdr--walking-that-helps-resolve-psychological-trauma)

- **Modulation vs. mediation is the coming key distinction in health research:** Sleep, gut microbiome, exercise, sunlight, and social connection all modulate health — they raise or lower the baseline but do not directly mediate specific outcomes. The gut microbiota produce precursors that become brain serotonin and dopamine; low-sugar fermented foods support this. Palmer cooling (cold contact on palms, soles of feet, or upper face) bypasses capillary beds to cool the body core rapidly, potentially doubling exercise endurance — Dr. Craig Heller's work at Stanford. [00:28:07](transcript.md#what-new-research-or-interventions-are-you-most-excited-about-in-the-health--wellness-realm)

- **Skateboarding built the failure tolerance and taste that shapes scientific judgment:** The skateboarding community has an extremely high bar — not just what you do, but how you do it. Navigating thousands of scientific papers requires the same filtering instinct. Surrounding yourself with people whose energy aligns with yours is itself a neurochemical tool — the same systems that fired in skateboarding fire in running a lab and recording a podcast. [00:37:30](transcript.md#what-lessons-from-skateboarding-have-you-learned-that-can-be-applied-to-neuroscience)

- **Richard Feynman's approach: find principles, not taxonomy:** Feynman wanted people to get turned on to how amazing physics was, not memorize specifics. His father taught him birdwatching by ignoring names and taxonomy — instead finding the commonalities and regularities in behavior. That principle-first approach guided the evening's lecture format. [00:39:03](transcript.md#favorite-feynman-story)

- **The physiological sigh is a universal stress regulation mechanism across mammalian species:** The double inhale, long exhale transcends species. Dogs, horses, and humans all show individual variation along a parasympathetic–sympathetic spectrum that is present from birth. Sue McConnell (Stanford dog genetics) notes some breeds are strongly parasympathetic-dominant while others are sympathetically driven. The autonomic nervous system has a "hinge" — a calibration point — and this is observable from birth. [00:42:10](transcript.md#do-you-suppose-this-physiological-stress-regulator-transcends-species)

- **Motivation runs on hybrid fuel — grinding plus delight:** The grinding fuel (grit, anger, competitive determination) is real but depletable without significant rest. The more sustaining system is the delight system — dopamine and serotonin activated by genuine interest. Find the gems within what you are working on. Discipline is also partly about setting the right environmental context (sleep, stress baseline, nutrition) so the brain is primed for effort. [00:47:20](transcript.md#is-there-any-science-behind-staying-motivated-or-developing-discipline)

- **Achieving dreams requires finding your seed message:** Find the thing that lit you up before puberty, before external pressures shaped your preferences — Robert Greene's framing. The object of the obsession is not the point; the quality of the attraction is the signal. Dreams emerge through active engagement with the world, diverse people, and willingness to iterate quickly but not so fast you exit the game entirely. [00:50:48](transcript.md#what-would-be-your-biggest-piece-of-advice-for-achieving-ones-dreams)

- **Psilocybin macro-dose sessions show compelling clinical results; micro-dosing data are weak:** Approximately two-thirds of participants achieve lasting relief from one guided macro-dose session for depression, PTSD, eating disorders, and end-of-life anxiety. Per Nolan Williams (triple-board-certified at Stanford), psilocybin sits near the bottom of lifetime perceived risk across all substances — below caffeine. Key contraindications are psychosis and addiction history. The ability to "let go" at the peak appears to predict a successful outcome. [00:57:09](transcript.md#whats-your-opinion-on-psilocybin)

- **Sauna suppresses appetite via dynorphin and elevates growth hormone:** Heat stress releases dynorphin — the same molecule released during alcohol withdrawal, which produces agitation and dysphoria. Dynorphin is an appetite suppressant. After the session, dynorphin drives upregulation of mu opioid receptors, making endogenous opioids more potent in the recovery window. Once-weekly sessions of four 20-minute blocks separated by five-minute breaks produce the largest growth hormone spikes. [01:01:07](transcript.md#why-does-my-desire-to-eat-disappear-after-i-use-the-sauna)
