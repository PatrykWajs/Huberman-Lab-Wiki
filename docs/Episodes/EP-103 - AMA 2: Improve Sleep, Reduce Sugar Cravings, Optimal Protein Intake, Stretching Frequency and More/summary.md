# Episode 103: AMA #2: Improve Sleep, Reduce Sugar Cravings, Optimal Protein Intake, Stretching Frequency & More

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

This is a premium preview (~28 minutes) of the second Ask Me Anything episode of the Huberman Lab Premium channel, released December 8, 2022. The publicly available portion addresses one detailed listener question from Jack Pritchard: how to specifically increase slow-wave (deep) sleep. Huberman covers sleep architecture basics — deep sleep dominates the first half of the night while REM dominates the second half — and explains why commercial sleep trackers (WHOOP, Oura, Eight Sleep) only estimate sleep stages rather than measure them precisely. He then systematically addresses the major inhibitors of deep sleep (alcohol, caffeine, cannabis/THC/CBD, and late eating) before presenting two evidence-based interventions: one hour of moderate-intensity cardio at approximately 60% VO2 max completed at least six hours before bed, and a supplement stack of magnesium threonate or bisglycinate, theanine, and apigenin — plus optionally myo-inositol at 900 mg — taken 30–60 minutes before sleep. The episode closes with an overview of the Huberman Lab Premium channel and its dollar-matched research-funding partnership with the Tiny Foundation; the full AMA covering protein intake, sugar cravings, stretching frequency, and more is available exclusively to premium subscribers.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Deep sleep (slow-wave sleep) dominates the first half of the night** — this is when growth hormone is released and the brain clears metabolic debris; REM dominates the second half and is critical for emotional processing and trauma release. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
2. **Commercial sleep trackers (WHOOP, Oura, Eight Sleep) estimate sleep stages** using heart rate, HRV, and body movement — they are reasonable approximations, not clinical measurements; lab EEG/EMG is the gold standard. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
3. **The goal is to balance total slow-wave sleep and REM sleep** across the night; the numbers need not be exactly equal, but a significant imbalance suggests disrupted sleep architecture. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
4. **Three major don'ts for deep sleep:** avoid alcohol, caffeine, and cannabis/THC/CBD within 8–12 hours of bedtime; even a glass of wine at lunch can suppress slow-wave sleep for some individuals. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
5. **Avoid eating within 2 hours of sleep** to maximise growth hormone output during slow-wave sleep — but never skip sleep itself to enforce the fast; a small snack beats lying awake hungry. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
6. **One hour of moderate-intensity cardio (≈60% VO2 max)** completed at least 6 hours before bedtime significantly improves slow-wave sleep quality and stability, per a randomised crossover EEG trial. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
7. **Sleep supplement stack Huberman personally uses:** magnesium threonate or bisglycinate + theanine + apigenin, taken 30–60 minutes before bed; theanine should be avoided by those prone to vivid or emotional dreams, night terrors, or sleepwalking. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
8. **Myo-inositol (900 mg, 30–60 min before bed)** improves sleep quality and ease of returning to sleep after mid-night waking, and is especially effective for people following low-carbohydrate diets or eating their last meal 4+ hours before bed. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
9. **Most adults need at least 6 hours per night** to have any chance of achieving a healthy slow-wave/REM balance; 7–8 hours is the practical target for 95% of people. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
10. **A 20–30 minute afternoon nap or NSDR session** supplements nighttime sleep and does not indicate insufficient overnight sleep; Huberman personally uses this alongside 6.5–7 hours of nighttime sleep. [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)

## Tools & Protocols

### Eliminate Alcohol for Better Deep Sleep

- **What:** Remove or sharply restrict alcohol intake before bed.
- **How:** Avoid alcohol within 8 hours of bedtime at minimum; 12 hours is preferable; eliminating alcohol entirely produces the best results for sleep architecture.
- **When:** Every night sleep quality is a priority.
- **Ref:** [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)

### Cut Off Caffeine Early

- **What:** Stop caffeine intake well before bedtime.
- **How:** No caffeine within 8 hours of target sleep time; 12 hours is optimal for most people. Non-caffeine users can skip this entirely.
- **When:** Daily habit — consistency matters more than any single night.
- **Ref:** [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)

### Eliminate Cannabis / THC / CBD Before Sleep

- **What:** Avoid cannabis, THC, and CBD in the hours before bed.
- **How:** Apply the same 8–12 hour buffer used for caffeine and alcohol. These compounds can aid sleep onset but measurably reduce slow-wave sleep quality.
- **When:** Nightly if improving deep sleep is the goal.
- **Ref:** [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)

### Pre-Sleep Eating Window

- **What:** Stop eating 2–4 hours before bedtime to maximise growth hormone release during slow-wave sleep.
- **How:** Finish the last meal at least 2 hours before target sleep time. If hunger would prevent sleep onset, a small snack is preferable to lying awake; sleep takes priority over the fasting window.
- **When:** Every night.
- **Ref:** [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)

### Moderate Cardio Exercise for Deep Sleep Enhancement

- **What:** One hour of moderate-intensity cardiovascular exercise (≈60% VO2 max, roughly zone 3–4) to increase slow-wave sleep stability and quality.
- **How:** Complete the session at least 6 hours before bedtime. Exercising intensely within 6 hours of bed can disrupt slow-wave and REM sleep for many people. Study subjects also abstained from caffeine and alcohol.
- **When:** Most days; a single session produces measurable EEG-measured effects per the randomised crossover trial.
- **Ref:** [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep) — study: "Exercise Improves the Quality of Slow-Wave Sleep by Increasing Slow-Wave Stability"

### Sleep Supplement Stack (Magnesium + Theanine + Apigenin)

- **What:** Three-compound stack to shorten sleep latency and deepen sleep quality.
- **How:** Take magnesium threonate (or magnesium bisglycinate) + theanine + apigenin 30–60 minutes before bed. Dosages are in the Huberman Lab Sleep Toolkit. Avoid theanine if prone to intense or emotional dreams, night terrors, or sleepwalking.
- **When:** Nightly, 30–60 minutes before target sleep time.
- **Ref:** [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)

### Myo-Inositol for Sleep Quality and Mid-Night Waking

- **What:** 900 mg myo-inositol to improve sleep depth, ease returning to sleep after waking, and reduce nighttime anxiety-driven arousal.
- **How:** Take 900 mg 30–60 minutes before bed, alone or alongside the magnesium/theanine/apigenin stack. Particularly effective on low-carbohydrate diets or when the last meal was 4+ hours before bed.
- **When:** Nightly or situationally as needed.
- **Ref:** [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep) — study: "The Impact of Myo-Inositol Supplementation on Sleep Quality in Pregnant Women: A Randomized Double-Blind Placebo Controlled Study"

### NSDR / Afternoon Nap

- **What:** 20–30 minute Non-Sleep Deep Rest or nap to supplement nighttime sleep.
- **How:** Use a guided NSDR script or simply rest/nap in the early-to-mid afternoon.
- **When:** Afternoon, as needed. Does not indicate insufficient overnight sleep.
- **Ref:** [00:00:54](transcript.md#000054-are-there-any-science-backed-protocols-to-increase-deep-sleep)
