# Episode 220: Female-Specific Exercise & Nutrition for Health, Performance & Longevity | Dr. Stacy Sims

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

## Episode Overview

Dr. Stacy Sims, exercise physiologist and nutrition scientist, joins Andrew Huberman to dismantle one-size-fits-all fitness advice and explain why women require fundamentally different training and nutrition strategies than men — strategies that also shift across the lifespan. The episode covers how female hormonal biology — including the menstrual cycle, perimenopause, and menopause — shapes everything from pre-workout nutrition, resistance training prescription, and cardiovascular strategy to supplement choices, sleep quality, and contraception. Sims presents an age-stratified framework covering women in their 20s–40s through post-menopause, with specific protocols for each life stage. Additional topics include deliberate cold and heat exposure, the impact of oral contraceptives on athletic performance, iron deficiency management, creatine use, adaptogens, and training during pregnancy. The overarching message: women are not small men, and the research base now supports precise, actionable protocols distinct from the male-derived defaults that have long dominated the field.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Women should not train fasted.** Women have more oxidative muscle fibers than men, making them already metabolically flexible — the primary benefit fasting is supposed to confer. Fasted training elevates cortisol, downregulates kisspeptin neurons (disrupting thyroid and LH pulsatility within four days), and prevents women from hitting intensities needed for optimal hormonal adaptation. A small pre-workout snack (15 g protein ± 30 g carbs) protects the hypothalamic-pituitary axis. [00:07:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=423s)(transcript.md#000703-intermittent-fasting-exercise--women)

2. **Eat aligned to circadian rhythm, not against it.** Eating from noon to 6 p.m. (skipping breakfast) produces more obesogenic outcomes than eating from 8 a.m. to 4–5 p.m. For women, the post-waking cortisol peak makes skipping breakfast especially harmful — it locks them into a prolonged sympathetic state. Exception: women with obesity, inactivity, or PCOS may use fasting as a strategic short-term intervention. [00:12:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=770s)(transcript.md#001250-cortisol--circadian-rhythm-caffeine--training)

3. **Caloric tipping point for endocrine dysfunction is double for women.** Men show endocrine dysfunction at 15 cal/kg fat-free mass; women at 30 cal/kg. Women's hormonal interplay — estrogen, LH, progesterone, thyroid — requires significantly more baseline calories than men, which is why low-calorie diets and fasting harm women far sooner. [00:31:48](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=1908s)(transcript.md#003148-hormones-calories--women)

4. **Training approach must shift at perimenopause.** Estrogen is "women's testosterone" — the primary driver of strength and power. As it declines (late 40s–early 50s), training must shift toward heavy loads with 2–3 reps in reserve to invoke the CNS response estrogen previously supported. Polarized training (very easy + very hard, minimal moderate) is the framework; soft tissue injuries like frozen shoulder and plantar fasciitis signal this transition is underway. [00:44:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=2656s)(transcript.md#004416-women-perimenopause-training--longevity)

5. **True high-intensity means genuine maximal effort with full recovery.** Real HIIT is 400–800m track repeats or 30-second all-out assault bike efforts with recovery allowing full readiness for the next interval. Moderate-effort circuit classes that keep you continuously moving are not high-intensity training and do not produce the same adaptations. [02:12:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=7938s)(transcript.md#021218-tool-what-is-high-intensity-training-cardiovascular-sets--recovery)

6. **Menstrual cycle phase affects training capacity.** Low hormone phase (day 1 of bleeding through ovulation) = best time for PRs, heavy loads, and maximal efforts. Luteal phase (post-ovulation to period) = higher cortisol, pro-inflammatory, harder to access carbohydrates — increase protein and carb intake the week before menstruation and reduce volume slightly. Individual variability is high; track for 3+ months before relying on population averages. [00:59:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=3550s)(transcript.md#005910-menstrual-cycle--training-tool-tracking--individual-variability)

7. **Oral contraceptives have body-wide effects beyond reproduction.** OCs blunt natural hormone fluctuations, reduce VO2max ceiling, and alter amygdala structure (increased fear, decreased risk tolerance) — reversible in adults but unknown in adolescents whose brains are still developing. A progestin-laced hormonal IUD (e.g., Mirena) is preferred by high-performance athletes: minimal systemic effect on adaptation, mood, or performance. [01:12:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=4342s)(transcript.md#011222-oral-contraception-hormones-athletic-performance-iud)

8. **Post-training sauna (Sims Protocol) increases blood volume and athletic performance.** Training induces passive dehydration; entering the sauna immediately after extends the cardiovascular stimulus, signaling upregulation of red blood cell production — analogous to altitude training. Also reduces hot flash frequency in perimenopausal women by resetting hypothalamic thermoregulation and improving gut-derived serotonin production. [01:42:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=6139s)(transcript.md#014219-tools-sims-protocol-post-training-sauna--performance-track-stack)

9. **Creatine monohydrate is the #1 supplement for women of all ages.** 3–5 g/day of Creapure. Benefits extend beyond muscle to brain function, mood, and gut health. The water-weight gain concern reflects muscular intracellular hydration, not fat. The DHT/hair loss concern comes from a poorly designed study and is not well supported by current evidence. [01:52:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=6774s)(transcript.md#015254-supplements-creatine-water-weight-hair-loss-vitamin-d3)

10. **Iron deficiency is pervasive and underdiagnosed in active women.** Test ferritin, serum iron, and TIBC approximately one week after period ends. Target ferritin above 50 ng/mL for active women — labs flag "normal" at 12+ but this is too low for performance. Vitamin D3 deficiency compounds iron absorption issues. [01:26:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=5191s)(transcript.md#012631-iron-fatigue-blood-testing--menstrual-cycle)

11. **For longevity, women need lactate production from high-intensity work.** Women's higher proportion of oxidative fibers means they naturally produce less lactate. Lactate is a signaling molecule for brain health and a factor in attenuating Alzheimer's plaque development. Starting sprint interval training early exposes the brain to lactate and builds glycolytic capacity — reducing cognitive decline risk decades later. [02:09:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=7778s)(transcript.md#020938-tool-women-in-20s-40s--training-lactate)

12. **For women 50+, three non-pharmaceutical longevity levers exist.** Jump training (plyometrics) for bone density and neural drive, heavy compound resistance training for strength preservation, and sprint interval training for lactate-based neuroprotection. Protein intake of 1–1.1 g/lb body weight per day is non-negotiable at this stage. [02:06:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=7579s)(transcript.md#020619-tool-women-in-50s--older-training--nutrition-for-longevity)

13. **Sleep architecture changes with the menstrual cycle.** In the mid-luteal to premenstrual phase (~10 days before period), women experience significantly less slow-wave sleep, increased sleep latency, and more light sleep — driving mood disturbances. Optimize with L-theanine, apigenin (chamomile), cool room temperature, and limiting screens. In perimenopause, hot flashes disrupt sleep architecture; NSDR/Yoga Nidra, rhodiola, and theanine can help. [01:49:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=6577s)(transcript.md#014937-women-hormones--sleep-perimenopause--sleep-hygiene)

14. **Caffeine in perimenopause amplifies cortisol and worsens hot flashes.** Baseline cortisol is already elevated in perimenopause; adding caffeine exacerbates sympathetic drive, disrupts sleep, and amplifies hot flashes. Reducing dose or cutting off earlier in the day is often warranted. [01:29:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=5373s)(transcript.md#012933-caffeine--perimenopause-nicotine-schisandra)

15. **The 80/20 nutrition rule underpins everything.** 80% whole foods with adequate protein (1–1.1 g/lb body weight/day), complex carbs timed around training, predominantly plant-based fats. 20% enjoyment foods. Women who eat more quality food are consistently leaner, stronger, and healthier than those in chronic caloric restriction. [02:19:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=8370s)(transcript.md#021930-nutrition-8020-rule)

## Tools & Protocols

### Pre-Workout Nutrition for Women
- **What:** Small protein-forward snack before morning training to protect kisspeptin neurons and enable training intensity.
- **How:** Strength training only: 15 g protein (e.g., protein powder in almond milk with espresso). Strength + cardio up to 1 hour: add 30 g carbohydrates. Does not need to be solid food. Follow with a full post-workout meal (35–40 g protein + carbohydrates) within 45 minutes of finishing.
- **When:** Within ~30 minutes before training.
- **Ref:** [00:21:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=1266s)(transcript.md#002106-pre-training-meal--brain-kisspeptin)

### Post-Training Recovery Meal
- **What:** High-leucine protein meal with carbohydrates to replenish glycogen, support muscle repair, and end the catabolic training window.
- **How:** 35–40 g protein from whey (or pea + rice for plant-based) plus adequate carbohydrates. Not delaying this meal is key — delaying post-training eating causes muscle protein breakdown and prolongs the stress response.
- **When:** Within 45 minutes of finishing training.
- **Ref:** [00:26:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=1605s)(transcript.md#002645-post-training-meal--recovery-window)

### Polarized Training Framework
- **What:** Training that alternates very easy recovery work with true maximal-effort high-intensity intervals, avoiding the chronic moderate-intensity "grey zone."
- **How:** Low-intensity days: walking, easy cycling, yoga at conversational heart rate. High-intensity days: sprint intervals (30-sec all-out assault bike with full recovery, or 400–800m track repeats at 80%+ effort) and heavy compound resistance training with adequate rest. EMOM formats (explosive compound work in structured intervals) count as true high-intensity.
- **When:** Critical for perimenopausal women. Minimum: 1 sprint interval session + 3 resistance sessions per week.
- **Ref:** [00:47:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=2834s)(transcript.md#004714-women--training-for-longevity-cardio-zone-2)

### Age-Stratified Training for Women
- **What:** Training emphasis changes with hormonal life stage.
- **How:**
  - Ages 20–40: Compound resistance training to failure or near failure. Periodize mesocycles. Include zone 2 and true HIIT. Build neural and muscular base before perimenopause arrives.
  - Ages 40+ (perimenopause onset): Heavy loads with 2–3 reps in reserve. Prioritize compound movements. Minimize chronic moderate-intensity cardio. Add jump training for bone density and neural drive.
  - Ages 50+: Jump training + heavy resistance + sprint intervals are the three non-pharmaceutical longevity levers. Protein 1–1.1 g/lb/day is non-negotiable. Protein powder is practical when appetite decreases with age.
- **When:** Continuously adapted across life stages. Start heavy compound training before perimenopause so the body is conditioned when hormonal changes arrive.
- **Ref:** [00:39:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=2350s)(transcript.md#003910-tool-women--training-goals-by-age-range)

### Menstrual Cycle-Based Training Periodization
- **What:** Adjust training intensity and nutrition based on hormonal phase.
- **How:**
  - Follicular phase (day 1 to ovulation): Schedule heaviest lifts, PRs, and maximal sprint work. High physical and mental stress tolerance.
  - Luteal phase (ovulation to period): Increase protein and carbohydrates, especially the week before menstruation. Slightly reduce volume. Prioritize recovery.
  - 10-Minute Rule: On a bad day, start training and give it 10 minutes. If still terrible, switch to active recovery. Usually feel better after the warm-up.
- **When:** Track personal cycle patterns for 3+ months before relying on population averages. Not every woman ovulates every cycle.
- **Ref:** [00:59:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=3550s)(transcript.md#005910-menstrual-cycle--training-tool-tracking--individual-variability)

### Sims Protocol (Post-Training Sauna)
- **What:** Sauna session immediately after cardiovascular or resistance training to drive blood volume expansion, aerobic adaptation, and hot flash reduction.
- **How:** Enter sauna after training while passively dehydrated. The extended cardiovascular stress signals the body to upregulate blood volume via increased red blood cell production — akin to altitude training. Wear a head covering to reduce brain heat load. Move to a lower bench if breathing becomes uncomfortable.
- **When:** 2–4x per week post-training. Do not replace with cold if seeking hypertrophy — cold post-training blunts the inflammatory response needed for muscle adaptation.
- **Ref:** [01:42:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=6139s)(transcript.md#014219-tools-sims-protocol-post-training-sauna--performance-track-stack)

### Track Stack (Pre-Sprint Performance Protocol)
- **What:** Combination supplement stack for designated maximal-effort sprint sessions.
- **How:** 200 mg caffeine + low-dose aspirin (blood-thinning) + beta-alanine (vasodilatory and carnosine-related muscle contraction benefits), taken before sprint training only.
- **When:** Sprint interval training days only. Ensure adequate recovery — do not stack two high-intensity days consecutively when using this protocol.
- **Ref:** [01:42:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=6139s)(transcript.md#014219-tools-sims-protocol-post-training-sauna--performance-track-stack)

### Creatine for Women
- **What:** Daily creatine monohydrate supplementation for muscle, brain, mood, and gut health.
- **How:** 3–5 g/day of Creapure-labeled creatine monohydrate (water-based wash, less GI distress than acid-wash brands). Take at any time of day with or without food. No loading phase required.
- **When:** Daily and continuously. Benefits accumulate over weeks to months.
- **Ref:** [01:52:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=6774s)(transcript.md#015254-supplements-creatine-water-weight-hair-loss-vitamin-d3)

### Deliberate Cold Exposure for Women
- **What:** Temperature-appropriate cold immersion targeting dopamine/norepinephrine response without severe vasoconstriction.
- **How:** Water temperature around 16°C (55–56°F). Avoid below ~10°C (50°F) which causes counterproductive vasoconstriction in women. For endometriosis management: cold exposure around ovulation for 10 days across three menstrual cycles (pilot data) to attenuate inflammatory symptoms.
- **When:** Not post-resistance training if seeking hypertrophy or strength gains. Cold before training or on separate days is acceptable.
- **Ref:** [01:34:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=5664s)(transcript.md#013424-deliberate-cold-exposure--women-endometriosis-tool-sauna--hot-flashes)

### Sleep Optimization by Cycle Phase
- **What:** Phase-targeted sleep hygiene to counter hormonal disruptions to slow-wave sleep.
- **How:** Mid-luteal to premenstrual phase (~10 days before period): add L-theanine, apigenin (chamomile extract), cool room temperature, limit screens. Perimenopause: rhodiola stacked with theanine, NSDR/Yoga Nidra, manage room temperature, work with sleep specialist for persistent insomnia.
- **When:** Applied during the specific cycle phases when sleep architecture is most disrupted.
- **Ref:** [01:49:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=6577s)(transcript.md#014937-women-hormones--sleep-perimenopause--sleep-hygiene)

### Adaptogen Use for Women
- **What:** Strategic use of adaptogens to modulate cortisol and support training during high-load periods.
- **How:** Schisandra — cortisol modulation and stress tolerance, particularly for perimenopausal women with high training loads. Rhodiola — sleep support in perimenopause. Ashwagandha — sleep-supportive (sedating), take at night. Cycle adaptogens during high-stress/high-load periods rather than indefinitely.
- **When:** During high training load or high-stress life periods. Taper during lower-demand phases.
- **Ref:** [01:57:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5LYGzKUPlE&t=7041s)(transcript.md#015721-protein-powder-adaptogens--timing)
