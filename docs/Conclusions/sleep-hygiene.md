# Sleep Hygiene

Aggregated findings across all Huberman Lab episodes on this topic.


- Grief is a process with a beginning, middle, and end that can be mapped neurologically and psychologically, distinct from depression despite overlapping symptoms like loss of appetite, sleep challenges, and unexpected crying. — *from [EP-408 - The Science & Process of Healing from Grief | Huberman Lab Essentials](../Episodes/EP-408 - The Science and Process of Healing from Grief/summary.md)*
- The brain maps relationships through three interconnected dimensions: space (where people are), time (when we interact with them), and closeness (attachment intensity), which are governed by the inferior parietal lobule. — *from [EP-408 - The Science & Process of Healing from Grief | Huberman Lab Essentials](../Episodes/EP-408 - The Science and Process of Healing from Grief/summary.md)*
- Grief fundamentally involves uncoupling and remapping the relationships between physical proximity, temporal expectations, and emotional attachment when someone is lost, as the brain continues making predictions about seeing them again. — *from [EP-408 - The Science & Process of Healing from Grief | Huberman Lab Essentials](../Episodes/EP-408 - The Science and Process of Healing from Grief/summary.md)*

- **Non-REM and REM alternate in ~90-minute cycles, but the ratio shifts across the night.** Early cycles are dominated by deep Non-REM (stages 3–4); later cycles are dominated by REM. Cutting sleep short disproportionately eliminates REM — losing 2 of 8 hours can eliminate 60–80% of REM sleep. Individual cycle length varies (75–120 min); waking at artificial "90-minute marks" is not beneficial and should be ignored. () — *from [EP-196 - Dr. Matt Walker: The Biology of Sleep and Your Unique Sleep Needs - Huberman Lab Guest Series](../Episodes/EP-196 - Dr. Matt Walker: The Biology of Sleep and Your Unique Sleep Needs - Huberman Lab Guest Series/summary.md)*
- **Deep Non-REM sleep (stages 3–4) produces massive synchronized slow brain waves unique to sleep.** Hundreds of thousands of cortical neurons fire and go silent in unison at ~1–2 Hz, producing huge-amplitude slow waves with sleep spindles (12–15 Hz) riding on top. This synchronized state does not occur in any waking brain state. It drives cardiovascular calming, immune restoration, metabolic regulation, memory consolidation, and glymphatic brain cleansing. () — *from [EP-196 - Dr. Matt Walker: The Biology of Sleep and Your Unique Sleep Needs - Huberman Lab Guest Series](../Episodes/EP-196 - Dr. Matt Walker: The Biology of Sleep and Your Unique Sleep Needs - Huberman Lab Guest Series/summary.md)*
- **Selective deep-sleep deprivation impairs blood glucose regulation on both sides of the equation.** The pancreas under-releases insulin, and cells become insulin-resistant. Five nights of short sleep can move a healthy individual to pre-diabetic glucose levels. () — *from [EP-196 - Dr. Matt Walker: The Biology of Sleep and Your Unique Sleep Needs - Huberman Lab Guest Series](../Episodes/EP-196 - Dr. Matt Walker: The Biology of Sleep and Your Unique Sleep Needs - Huberman Lab Guest Series/summary.md)*

- Higher fiber intake is associated with more deep sleep, while higher saturated fat intake reduces deep sleep and refined carbohydrates/simple sugars increase sleep arousals. — *from [EP-411 - Eating for Better Sleep & Foods that Improve Metabolic Health | Dr. Marie-Pierre St-Onge](../Episodes/EP-411 - Eating for Better Sleep and Foods that Improve Metabolic Health - Dr. Marie-Pierre St-Onge/summary.md)*
- Sleep deprivation increases hunger differently by sex: in men it elevates ghrelin (appetite-stimulating hormone), while in women it reduces GLP-1 (satiety hormone), causing a 300-calorie daily overeating increase. — *from [EP-411 - Eating for Better Sleep & Foods that Improve Metabolic Health | Dr. Marie-Pierre St-Onge](../Episodes/EP-411 - Eating for Better Sleep and Foods that Improve Metabolic Health - Dr. Marie-Pierre St-Onge/summary.md)*
- Chronic mild sleep restriction of 6 hours per night over 6 weeks (versus 7.5 hours) increases insulin resistance and blood pressure, with worse effects in post-menopausal women. — *from [EP-411 - Eating for Better Sleep & Foods that Improve Metabolic Health | Dr. Marie-Pierre St-Onge](../Episodes/EP-411 - Eating for Better Sleep and Foods that Improve Metabolic Health - Dr. Marie-Pierre St-Onge/summary.md)*

- Viewing bright light from sunlight within 30-60 minutes of waking triggers cortisol release, sets your circadian clock, and creates an optimal 16-hour sleep pressure window for falling asleep that night. — *from [EP-412 - Essentials: Sleep Toolkit for Optimizing Sleep & Sleep-Wake Timing](../Episodes/EP-412 - Essentials Sleep Toolkit for Optimizing Sleep and Sleep-Wake Timing/summary.md)*
- On clear days, get 5 minutes of sunlight exposure to your eyes; on cloudy days, 10 minutes; on densely overcast days, 20-30 minutes—never view through car windshields or windows as this doesn't trigger the necessary mechanisms. — *from [EP-412 - Essentials: Sleep Toolkit for Optimizing Sleep & Sleep-Wake Timing](../Episodes/EP-412 - Essentials Sleep Toolkit for Optimizing Sleep and Sleep-Wake Timing/summary.md)*
- Delay caffeine intake by 90-120 minutes after waking to achieve a longer, more sustained energy arc throughout the day and reduce the need for additional caffeine in the afternoon. — *from [EP-412 - Essentials: Sleep Toolkit for Optimizing Sleep & Sleep-Wake Timing](../Episodes/EP-412 - Essentials Sleep Toolkit for Optimizing Sleep and Sleep-Wake Timing/summary.md)*

- Discipline is a valuable scaffolding tool to initiate tasks, but relying too heavily on it creates dependency; the goal is to use discipline as training wheels and gradually develop will by introducing playfulness and choice into the process. — *from [EP-417 - Movement Practice to Strengthen Your Mind-Body Connection | Ido Portal](../Episodes/EP-417 - Movement Practice to Strengthen Your Mind-Body Connection - Ido Portal/summary.md)*
- Will is not developed but rather exposed through practice; it represents the totality and harmonious integration of all that you are, making you reliable when discipline alone would fail. — *from [EP-417 - Movement Practice to Strengthen Your Mind-Body Connection | Ido Portal](../Episodes/EP-417 - Movement Practice to Strengthen Your Mind-Body Connection - Ido Portal/summary.md)*
- Meditation retreats and extended sitting practices can create dependence on the formal practice environment; the real goal is integrating meditative states into daily life through micro-dosages and brief moments of intentional awareness. — *from [EP-417 - Movement Practice to Strengthen Your Mind-Body Connection | Ido Portal](../Episodes/EP-417 - Movement Practice to Strengthen Your Mind-Body Connection - Ido Portal/summary.md)*
