# Cold Plunges and Deliberate Cooling

Aggregated findings across all Huberman Lab episodes on this topic.


- **Winter cold and flu prevalence is real, not a myth.** Research clearly shows greater prevalence in winter months, driven by multiple converging factors — shorter days, colder temperatures, more time indoors, and drier air — not a single cause. — *from [EP-174 - AMA 13: Winter Months and Sickness, Wim Hof Breathing and Stressors](../Episodes/EP-174 - AMA 13: Winter Months and Sickness, Wim Hof Breathing and Stressors/summary.md)*
- **Indoor proximity is the primary transmission driver.** Cold weather pushes people indoors, reducing interpersonal distance. Studies measured transmission probability at one, three, and six feet and confirmed a direct dose-response: closer proximity = higher infection risk. — *from [EP-174 - AMA 13: Winter Months and Sickness, Wim Hof Breathing and Stressors](../Episodes/EP-174 - AMA 13: Winter Months and Sickness, Wim Hof Breathing and Stressors/summary.md)*
- **Any physical contact further raises risk.** Kissing, hugging, handshakes after nose-wiping — cold and flu virus survives on skin and transmits via touch, not just respiratory droplets. — *from [EP-174 - AMA 13: Winter Months and Sickness, Wim Hof Breathing and Stressors](../Episodes/EP-174 - AMA 13: Winter Months and Sickness, Wim Hof Breathing and Stressors/summary.md)*

- **Cold showers produce an adrenaline shock but don't meaningfully improve performance on their own.** The "benefit" people feel is largely an adrenal response rather than a physiological performance enhancement. () — *from [EP-315 - Essentials - Increase Strength and Endurance with Cooling Protocols - Dr. Craig Heller](../Episodes/EP-315 - Essentials - Increase Strength and Endurance with Cooling Protocols - Dr. Craig Heller/summary.md)*
- **Pre-aerobic cold exposure extends endurance by increasing your body's heat buffer.** A cool shower before a long run lowers starting core temperature, giving the body more capacity to absorb exercise-generated heat before reaching the impairing threshold. Pacers will go farther; forcers will go faster. () — *from [EP-315 - Essentials - Increase Strength and Endurance with Cooling Protocols - Dr. Craig Heller](../Episodes/EP-315 - Essentials - Increase Strength and Endurance with Cooling Protocols - Dr. Craig Heller/summary.md)*
- **Anaerobic muscle failure is driven largely by local overheating.** Muscle metabolism rises 50–60× during anaerobic effort, but blood flow cannot keep pace. A temperature-sensitive enzyme critical for ATP production shuts off above ~39–39.5°C — this is the physiological basis of hitting failure. () — *from [EP-315 - Essentials - Increase Strength and Endurance with Cooling Protocols - Dr. Craig Heller](../Episodes/EP-315 - Essentials - Increase Strength and Endurance with Cooling Protocols - Dr. Craig Heller/summary.md)*
