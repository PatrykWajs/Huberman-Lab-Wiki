# Emotional Intelligence and Relationships

<!-- topic-intro-added -->
> **Overview**
>
> Emotional intelligence encompasses the ability to identify, comprehend, and manage one’s own emotions, while also recognizing and influencing the emotions of others to enhance interpersonal connections. This skill set includes emotional regulation, empathy, and proficient communication, all of which are vital for establishing and nurturing relationships and making informed decisions. The effective application of these skills in daily life significantly contributes to overall emotional health, which is as essential as physical and mental well-being for a fulfilling life.
> 
> Prioritizing fundamental aspects of health such as adequate sleep, balanced nutrition, and regular physical activity can create a neurochemical environment conducive to emotional regulation and resilience. This idea of managing one’s "brain-body budget" involves ensuring that the brain is equipped with sufficient resources to positively navigate life’s challenges, thus bolstering emotional resilience. Deep and rewarding relationships, along with meaningful social connections, are critical components of emotional health and overall wellness, leading to enhancements in mental health, a decreased likelihood of cardiovascular issues, and even increased longevity.
> 
> Skills such as self-reflection, compassion for oneself and others, contextual understanding of emotions, and effective conflict resolution are essential for navigating social interactions and building stronger connections with others. Understanding and tracking these emotional intelligence principles are important for practical application in everyday scenarios, enabling individuals to foster healthier relationships and improve their quality of life.
