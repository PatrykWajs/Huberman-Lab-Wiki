# The Science of ADHD

Aggregated findings across all Huberman Lab episodes on this topic.


- **Developmental vs. adult plasticity are fundamentally different** — From birth to ~age 25 the nervous system rewires mainly by pruning the overconnected circuits formed at birth; children acquire language, motor skills, and emotional associations through passive exposure without needing deliberate effort. After age 25 the extracellular space fills with matrix and glia, and passive exposure produces no lasting change — deliberate alertness plus selective attention are required. — *from [EP-6 - How to Focus to Change Your Brain](../Episodes/EP-006 - How to Focus to Change Your Brain/summary.md)*
- **Experience alone does not change the adult brain** — Huberman calls "every experience changes your brain" one of the biggest lies circulating in popular culture. Hubel and Wiesel's control experiment (closing both eyes produced no cortical change; closing only one did) and the Merzenich auditory-cue control both confirm that mere repetition or exposure without selective attention produces zero cortical plasticity in adults. — *from [EP-6 - How to Focus to Change Your Brain](../Episodes/EP-006 - How to Focus to Change Your Brain/summary.md)*
- **Three neurochemicals must be simultaneously active to gate adult plasticity** — Epinephrine from the locus coeruleus (alertness); acetylcholine from the brainstem parabigeminal/parabrachial nuclei (attentional spotlight on selected sensory input); and acetylcholine from the nucleus basalis of Meynert (forebrain source). Merzenich, Kilgard, and others showed that electrically stimulating all three while presenting a stimulus produces immediate, massive one-trial plasticity. Without all three present together, no durable rewiring occurs. — *from [EP-6 - How to Focus to Change Your Brain](../Episodes/EP-006 - How to Focus to Change Your Brain/summary.md)*

- **Psychiatry relies entirely on language** — unlike neurology (which has brain scans, EEGs, and blood tests), psychiatry has no objective diagnostic test for any individual patient. Every diagnosis rests on words and behavioral rating scales, making it simultaneously the most mysterious and most powerful branch of medicine. — *from [EP-26 - Understanding and Healing the Mind - Dr. Karl Deisseroth](../Episodes/EP-026 - Understanding and Healing the Mind - Dr. Karl Deisseroth/summary.md)*
- **Early morning awakening is an early warning sign of depression** — patients beginning to slide into depression often start waking at 5 AM, then 4 AM, then 3 AM, unable to return to sleep. This vegetative sign can precede the patient's own subjective awareness of a depressive episode and can trigger early intervention. — *from [EP-26 - Understanding and Healing the Mind - Dr. Karl Deisseroth](../Episodes/EP-026 - Understanding and Healing the Mind - Dr. Karl Deisseroth/summary.md)*
- **The most effective psychiatric drugs often carry the most side effects** — clozapine, the most powerful antipsychotic, hits virtually every receptor type (serotonin, muscarinic, dopamine, and more), causing agranulocytosis risk, drooling, and dizziness. Yet for patients where nothing else works, it clears hallucinations and paranoia decisively. This clozapine paradox defines the current pharmacology landscape. — *from [EP-26 - Understanding and Healing the Mind - Dr. Karl Deisseroth](../Episodes/EP-026 - Understanding and Healing the Mind - Dr. Karl Deisseroth/summary.md)*

- **ADHD is strongly genetic but unrelated to IQ.** Concordance in identical twins reaches ~75%; fraternal twins 50–60%; parent-child ~10–25%. Having a close relative with ADHD greatly raises probability, but intelligence of any kind — standard IQ, emotional, spatial — has no relationship to ADHD. — *from [EP-37 - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus](../Episodes/EP-037 - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus/summary.md)*
- **ADHD is a dopamine-deficit disorder at the circuit level.** Low dopamine in prefrontal circuits causes the default mode network and task-directed networks to fire together (correlated) rather than in opposition (anti-correlated), producing mind-wandering instead of sustained focus. Brain imaging confirms that when ADHD improves — through treatment or aging — anti-correlation is restored. — *from [EP-37 - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus](../Episodes/EP-037 - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus/summary.md)*
- **Hyper-focus is the flip side of ADHD inattention.** People with ADHD can sustain intense focus on intrinsically rewarding activities because those activities spike dopamine enough to momentarily balance the circuit. This proves the capacity to focus is present — the deficit is in engaging it for non-preferred tasks. — *from [EP-37 - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus](../Episodes/EP-037 - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus/summary.md)*

- **The human circadian clock runs ~24.2 hours — light entrains it to exactly 24.** Without daily light exposure you drift ~12 minutes per day; after 25 days you are 5–6 hours out of phase — jet-lagged at home. Morning sunlight resets the clock to exactly 24 hours each day. — *from [EP-43 - Timing Light, Food, and Exercise for Better Sleep, Energy and Mood - Dr. Samer Hattar](../Episodes/EP-043 - Timing Light, Food, and Exercise for Better Sleep, Energy and Mood - Dr. Samer Hattar/summary.md)*
- **ipRGCs (melanopsin cells) are the dedicated clock-setting photoreceptors in the eye.** Co-discovered by Hattar, David Berson, and Ignacio Provencio, these ~1–2% of retinal ganglion cells are intrinsically photosensitive, project to the SCN (master clock) and other non-visual brain regions, and operate entirely independently of rod/cone image vision. People who are image-blind but have intact eyes often maintain normal circadian entrainment. — *from [EP-43 - Timing Light, Food, and Exercise for Better Sleep, Energy and Mood - Dr. Samer Hattar](../Episodes/EP-043 - Timing Light, Food, and Exercise for Better Sleep, Energy and Mood - Dr. Samer Hattar/summary.md)*
- **Get 10–15 minutes of outdoor light within the first hour of waking.** Even on overcast days, outdoor light far exceeds indoor artificial light intensity. Shade on a sunny day is sufficient; direct sun viewing is not required. On very dark days, extend to 30–45 minutes or use a light therapy box. — *from [EP-43 - Timing Light, Food, and Exercise for Better Sleep, Energy and Mood - Dr. Samer Hattar](../Episodes/EP-043 - Timing Light, Food, and Exercise for Better Sleep, Energy and Mood - Dr. Samer Hattar/summary.md)*

- **Play is homeostatically regulated** — Like hunger or sleep, play is driven by ancient circuitry. Animals restricted from playing will play more when given the opportunity, placing play alongside other fundamental biological drives. () — *from [EP-58 - Using Play to Rewire and Improve Your Brain](../Episodes/EP-058 - Using Play to Rewire and Improve Your Brain/summary.md)*
- **The neurochemistry of play is specific: high opioids, low epinephrine** — Genuine play requires elevated endogenous opioids released from the periaqueductal gray (PAG) and low levels of adrenaline/epinephrine. Any elevation of epinephrine — from stress, competitive anxiety, or trauma — blocks the play circuits entirely. () — *from [EP-58 - Using Play to Rewire and Improve Your Brain](../Episodes/EP-058 - Using Play to Rewire and Improve Your Brain/summary.md)*
- **Play is the primary portal to neuroplasticity** — Across all animal species, those that play longest throughout life have the greatest capacity for brain plasticity. Play is not a hack; it is the evolutionarily conserved mechanism by which the nervous system rewires itself at any age. () — *from [EP-58 - Using Play to Rewire and Improve Your Brain](../Episodes/EP-058 - Using Play to Rewire and Improve Your Brain/summary.md)*

- **Hypnosis is a state of focused attention, not loss of control.** It resembles looking through a telephoto lens — heightened detail and absorption, reduced peripheral context. Stage hypnosis exploits this state for entertainment and can harm participants; clinical self-hypnosis *increases* self-control rather than surrendering it. — *from [EP-60 - Using Hypnosis to Enhance Health and Performance - Dr. David Spiegel](../Episodes/EP-060 - Using Hypnosis to Enhance Health and Performance - Dr. David Spiegel/summary.md)*
- **Three neural signatures define the hypnotic state.** fMRI studies show: (1) reduced activity in the dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (DACC/salience network), making distraction less likely; (2) increased functional connectivity between the DACC and the left dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (executive control network); and (3) altered inverse connectivity between the DLPFC and the posterior cingulate cortex, producing dissociation and cognitive flexibility. — *from [EP-60 - Using Hypnosis to Enhance Health and Performance - Dr. David Spiegel](../Episodes/EP-060 - Using Hypnosis to Enhance Health and Performance - Dr. David Spiegel/summary.md)*
- **Hypnosis also alters DLPFC-insula connectivity, enabling body control.** The DLPFC communicates through the insula — part of the salience and mind-body network — to regulate internal physiology. In one study, hypnotized subjects imagining eating their favorite foods increased gastric acid secretion by 87%; imagining abstaining produced a 40% decrease. Even after Pentagastrin injection (which triggers acid release), the hypnosis condition produced a 19% reduction. — *from [EP-60 - Using Hypnosis to Enhance Health and Performance - Dr. David Spiegel](../Episodes/EP-060 - Using Hypnosis to Enhance Health and Performance - Dr. David Spiegel/summary.md)*

- **Two parallel pathways drive sugar cravings** — one based on the sweet taste itself (conscious), another based on the nutritive/blood-glucose-raising properties of food operating entirely below conscious awareness (subconscious post-ingestive). Both are hardwired and exist across all mammals. — *from [EP-64 - Controlling Sugar Cravings and Metabolism with Science-Based Tools - Huberman Lab Podcast 64](../Episodes/EP-064 - Controlling Sugar Cravings and Metabolism with Science-Based Tools - Huberman Lab Podcast 64/summary.md)*
- **Neuropod cells are the subconscious accelerator** — neurons in the gut discovered by Dr. Diego Bohorquez detect sugar, amino acids, and essential fatty acids, then signal the brain via the vagus nerve → nodose ganglion → nucleus of the solitary tract, releasing dopamine even when sweetness cannot be tasted. — *from [EP-64 - Controlling Sugar Cravings and Metabolism with Science-Based Tools - Huberman Lab Podcast 64](../Episodes/EP-064 - Controlling Sugar Cravings and Metabolism with Science-Based Tools - Huberman Lab Podcast 64/summary.md)*
- **Three accelerators, not two** — beyond taste-driven dopamine and gut-driven dopamine, a third pathway involves the metabolic use of glucose by neurons themselves; blocking glucose uptake with 2-Deoxy-D-glucose (2-DG) eliminates sweet preference entirely. — *from [EP-64 - Controlling Sugar Cravings and Metabolism with Science-Based Tools - Huberman Lab Podcast 64](../Episodes/EP-064 - Controlling Sugar Cravings and Metabolism with Science-Based Tools - Huberman Lab Podcast 64/summary.md)*

- **Aggression is a process, not an event.** It has a beginning, middle, and end, organized by neural circuits firing in sequence — not a single on/off switch. Understanding this process is essential for interrupting it. — *from [EP-71 - Understanding and Controlling Anger and Aggression](../Episodes/EP-071 - Understanding and Controlling Anger and Aggression/summary.md)*
- **Konrad Lorenz's hydraulic pressure model captures how the system works.** Multiple internal and external variables build pressure toward aggressive behavior. Lorenz studied fixed action patterns and showed entire behavioral sequences can be evoked by a single stimulus — the foundation of modern aggression neuroscience. — *from [EP-71 - Understanding and Controlling Anger and Aggression](../Episodes/EP-071 - Understanding and Controlling Anger and Aggression/summary.md)*
- **The ventromedial hypothalamus (VMH) is the central hub of aggression.** Only ~3,000 neurons (across both hemispheres), all bearing estrogen receptors, are both necessary and sufficient to generate the full catalog of aggressive behaviors. Walter Hess first identified this region via electrode stimulation in cats. — *from [EP-71 - Understanding and Controlling Anger and Aggression](../Episodes/EP-071 - Understanding and Controlling Anger and Aggression/summary.md)*

- **Trauma is defined by functional brain change, not just negative experience.** Trauma is something that overwhelms coping skills and leaves the brain functioning differently — identifiable through shifts in mood, behavior, sleep, anxiety, and hypervigilance. Not every hard experience qualifies. — *from [EP-75 - Therapy, Treating Trauma and Other Life Challenges - Dr. Paul Conti](../Episodes/EP-075 - Therapy, Treating Trauma and Other Life Challenges - Dr. Paul Conti/summary.md)*
- **Guilt and shame are evolved mechanisms that become maladaptive.** The limbic system embeds negative experiences deeply for survival purposes. Shame is aroused affect — it arises without choice. In modern life these mechanisms prevent people from processing trauma by driving avoidance instead of confrontation. Vicarious experiences — such as prolonged news exposure — can also cause trauma-like changes in brain function. — *from [EP-75 - Therapy, Treating Trauma and Other Life Challenges - Dr. Paul Conti](../Episodes/EP-075 - Therapy, Treating Trauma and Other Life Challenges - Dr. Paul Conti/summary.md)*
- **The repetition compulsion is real and rooted in limbic logic.** People repeatedly recreate the emotional conditions of their trauma because the limbic system — which doesn't understand time — believes that solving a similar situation now will also resolve the original wound. A person doesn't have seven different abusive relationships; they have the same relationship seven times. — *from [EP-75 - Therapy, Treating Trauma and Other Life Challenges - Dr. Paul Conti](../Episodes/EP-075 - Therapy, Treating Trauma and Other Life Challenges - Dr. Paul Conti/summary.md)*

- **The Arrow Model of Focus** — Epinephrine sets overall alertness (shaft of the arrow), acetylcholine narrows attention like a spotlight onto a specific target (arrowhead), and dopamine sustains motivation to keep the arrow moving (engine). All three must be engaged for deep work. — *from [EP-88 - Focus Toolkit: Tools to Improve Your Focus and Concentration](../Episodes/EP-088 - Focus Toolkit: Tools to Improve Your Focus and Concentration/summary.md)*
- **Sleep is the master modulator — no tool overrides chronic deprivation** — Sleep modulates virtually every process in brain and body. No supplement, drug, or protocol can compensate for chronic sleep deprivation. Aim for quality sleep 80% of nights. — *from [EP-88 - Focus Toolkit: Tools to Improve Your Focus and Concentration](../Episodes/EP-088 - Focus Toolkit: Tools to Improve Your Focus and Concentration/summary.md)*
- **Ultradian cycles are the natural unit of focused work** — The brain operates in ~90-minute ultradian cycles. Expect a 5–15 minute warm-up where focus feels hard; push through it. Plan 1–3 such deep-work sessions per day maximum. More trained = fewer sessions sustainable per day. — *from [EP-88 - Focus Toolkit: Tools to Improve Your Focus and Concentration](../Episodes/EP-088 - Focus Toolkit: Tools to Improve Your Focus and Concentration/summary.md)*

- **Caffeine operates through four distinct mechanisms** — (1) it acts as a subconscious reinforcer, making you prefer foods, drinks, containers, and company associated with its consumption; (2) it releases dopamine and acetylcholine in the forebrain, improving clarity, focus, and rule-switching; (3) it increases the number of dopamine receptors in the brain's reward pathways, making positive experiences feel even better; and (4) it blocks adenosine receptors to reduce sleepiness. — *from [EP-102 - Using Caffeine to Optimize Mental and Physical Performance](../Episodes/EP-102 - Using Caffeine to Optimize Mental and Physical Performance/summary.md)*
- **Caffeine works primarily by blocking adenosine receptors** — adenosine builds up during waking hours and drives sleepiness; caffeine occupies those receptors without clearing adenosine, which then surges when caffeine wears off, causing an afternoon crash. Caffeine borrows energy from the future rather than creating it. — *from [EP-102 - Using Caffeine to Optimize Mental and Physical Performance](../Episodes/EP-102 - Using Caffeine to Optimize Mental and Physical Performance/summary.md)*
- **Delay morning caffeine 90–120 minutes after waking** — this allows cortisol (boosted further by morning sunlight and exercise) to naturally clear residual adenosine. Drinking caffeine immediately upon waking prevents this clearance and guarantees an afternoon crash; delaying it eliminates the crash for most people. Exception: if doing intense exercise within 90 minutes of waking, caffeine before that workout is appropriate. — *from [EP-102 - Using Caffeine to Optimize Mental and Physical Performance](../Episodes/EP-102 - Using Caffeine to Optimize Mental and Physical Performance/summary.md)*

- **Humans are better at smell than commonly believed** — Humans can follow buried scent trails on hands and knees with high fidelity, comparable to dogs in some conditions. The claim that bloodhounds have "a billion" olfactory receptors was traced back to a fabricated number originating from an offhand lecture remark by geneticist Doron Lancet, with no scientific basis. Humans have ~7 million receptors of ~350 subtypes. Two nostrils confer a measurable advantage over one centralized nostril for odor tracking — analogous to stereo hearing for sound localization. ((https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EOstSv7SrRU&t=406s)(transcript.md#000646-olfaction-circuits-smell)) — *from [EP-134 - How Smells Influence Our Hormones, Health and Behavior - Dr. Noam Sobel](../Episodes/EP-134 - How Smells Influence Our Hormones, Health and Behavior - Dr. Noam Sobel/summary.md)*
- **The nasal cycle reflects autonomic nervous system state** — Each nostril alternates between high- and low-airflow roughly every 2.5 hours. The high-flow nostril corresponds to a contralateral shift in autonomic tone. Right nostril dominance correlates with higher alertness/sympathetic drive; left nostril with calmer/parasympathetic state. Voluntary forced nasal breathing can shift this balance. The Sobel lab built a "nasal halter" wearable that logs 24-hour nostril airflow and can distinguish ADHD adults from non-ADHD adults, and detect whether they are on Ritalin — entirely from the nasal airflow record. ((https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EOstSv7SrRU&t=2365s)(transcript.md#003925-the-alternating-nasal-cycle--autonomic-nervous-system)) — *from [EP-134 - How Smells Influence Our Hormones, Health and Behavior - Dr. Noam Sobel](../Episodes/EP-134 - How Smells Influence Our Hormones, Health and Behavior - Dr. Noam Sobel/summary.md)*
- **Breathing phase gates cognition** — Inhalation vs. exhalation phase-locks to neural oscillations across widespread brain regions. Memory encoding, fear processing, and attention are enhanced during the inhalation phase. The effect is stronger with nasal than oral breathing, though a weaker version exists with mouth breathing. A study by Off Perl from the Sobel lab showed significantly better visual-spatial performance on nasal inhalation versus exhalation, using a task with no olfactory component. ((https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EOstSv7SrRU&t=2898s)(transcript.md#004818-cognitive-processing--breathing)) — *from [EP-134 - How Smells Influence Our Hormones, Health and Behavior - Dr. Noam Sobel](../Episodes/EP-134 - How Smells Influence Our Hormones, Health and Behavior - Dr. Noam Sobel/summary.md)*

- **ADHD is a coordination failure, not a simple deficit.** The prefrontal cortex in ADHD fails to properly coordinate other brain networks — leaving the default mode network, salience network, and dorsal attention network hyper-connected and co-active when they should be suppressed. The result is extreme difficulty directing attention to non-preferred tasks. — *from [EP-138 - Adderall, Stimulants and Modafinil for ADHD: Short- and Long-Term Effects](../Episodes/EP-138 - Adderall, Stimulants and Modafinil for ADHD: Short- and Long-Term Effects/summary.md)*
- **Stimulants are sympathomimetics — they mimic the fight-or-flight response.** Almost all ADHD drugs activate the sympathetic arm of the autonomic nervous system by boosting dopamine and norepinephrine, modulating multiple brain circuits to improve signal-to-noise ratio and restore prefrontal coordination. — *from [EP-138 - Adderall, Stimulants and Modafinil for ADHD: Short- and Long-Term Effects](../Episodes/EP-138 - Adderall, Stimulants and Modafinil for ADHD: Short- and Long-Term Effects/summary.md)*
- **Adderall is a 3:1 mix of D-amphetamine and L-amphetamine with three mechanisms of action.** D-amphetamine drives primarily brain (dopamine/norepinephrine) effects; L-amphetamine drives peripheral effects (heart rate, blood pressure). Adderall boosts dopamine and norepinephrine via: blocking DAT/NET reuptake transporters, disrupting VMAT2 vesicle packaging, and disrupting the protein complex between them — making it far more potent than methylphenidate. — *from [EP-138 - Adderall, Stimulants and Modafinil for ADHD: Short- and Long-Term Effects](../Episodes/EP-138 - Adderall, Stimulants and Modafinil for ADHD: Short- and Long-Term Effects/summary.md)*

- **Working memory differs from long-term memory in a fundamental way.** It does not involve neuroplasticity — information is held temporarily in a running neural circuit and then intentionally discarded. Capacity is small (roughly 4 ± 1 items) and tightly coupled to attention., — *from [EP-184 - Tools to Enhance Working Memory and Attention](../Episodes/EP-184 - Tools to Enhance Working Memory and Attention/summary.md)*
- **Long-term memory has two subtypes that free up working memory.** Declarative memory stores facts; procedural memory stores action sequences (riding a bike, tying shoes). Once skills become automatic they no longer tax working memory capacity. — *from [EP-184 - Tools to Enhance Working Memory and Attention](../Episodes/EP-184 - Tools to Enhance Working Memory and Attention/summary.md)*
- **"Neurons that fire together wire together" was coined by Carla Shatz, not Donald Hebb.** Hebb discussed neuroplasticity broadly, but the specific phrase belongs to Shatz (Stanford). Long-term potentiation (LTP) — synaptic strengthening through co-activation — is the mechanism. — *from [EP-184 - Tools to Enhance Working Memory and Attention](../Episodes/EP-184 - Tools to Enhance Working Memory and Attention/summary.md)*

- **Sturdiness is the foundation of healthy relationships.** Being sturdy means staying connected to yourself (your values, needs, limits) and to the other person at the same time. It is the opposite of either giving in or shutting down. — *from [EP-189 - Protocols for Excellent Parenting and Improving Relationships of All Kinds - Dr. Becky Kennedy](../Episodes/EP-189 - Protocols for Excellent Parenting and Improving Relationships of All Kinds - Dr. Becky Kennedy/summary.md)*
- **Boundaries are actions you take, not demands you make.** A boundary is something you will do — not something you ask the other person to do. "I'm going to turn off the TV" is a boundary. "Stop watching TV" is a request. Boundaries require no cooperation from the other person. — *from [EP-189 - Protocols for Excellent Parenting and Improving Relationships of All Kinds - Dr. Becky Kennedy](../Episodes/EP-189 - Protocols for Excellent Parenting and Improving Relationships of All Kinds - Dr. Becky Kennedy/summary.md)*
- **Kids aren't bad — they're underskilled.** Reframing misbehavior as a skills deficit rather than a character flaw changes the parental response from punishment to teaching. Children are born with all the feelings and none of the skills to manage those feelings; behaviors are surface signals of an underlying unmet need. — *from [EP-189 - Protocols for Excellent Parenting and Improving Relationships of All Kinds - Dr. Becky Kennedy](../Episodes/EP-189 - Protocols for Excellent Parenting and Improving Relationships of All Kinds - Dr. Becky Kennedy/summary.md)*

- **Smartphones are only problematic when loaded with engineered-attention apps.** Newport owns a smartphone but uses no social media apps, which makes it genuinely useful rather than addictive — closer to the original 2007 Steve Jobs vision of a useful phone plus maps plus music. The hardware is not the danger; the software layer deliberately engineered to exploit dopamine and novelty circuits is. — *from [EP-192 - How to Enhance Focus and Improve Productivity - Dr. Cal Newport](../Episodes/EP-192 - How to Enhance Focus and Improve Productivity - Dr. Cal Newport/summary.md)*

He brings his laptop in only to write. Books are carefully curated by shelf for different types of inspiration. A fireplace provides visual stimulation for reading and serendipitous idea generation. - **Productive meditation — thinking on foot — is trainable and transfers directly to working memory capacity.** Newport coined the term "productive meditation" in *Deep Work* for the practice of walking while holding a single hard problem in mind. Motor-circuit activation during walking inhibits extraneous neural networks and supports sustained internal focus on a concept. — *from [EP-192 - How to Enhance Focus and Improve Productivity - Dr. Cal Newport](../Episodes/EP-192 - How to Enhance Focus and Improve Productivity - Dr. Cal Newport/summary.md)*

- **Memory is about the present and future, not the past.** The brain uses stored patterns to predict and interpret the present and simulate possible futures. - **ADHD is defined by a cluster of deficits plus a paradox:** challenges with attention, impulse control, emotionality, time perception, and working memory coexist with an ability to hyperfocus intensely on intrinsically rewarding activities — because hyperfocus is driven by internally generated dopamine, not willpower. — *from [EP-313 - Essentials - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus](../Episodes/EP-313 - Essentials - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus/summary.md)*
- **Dopamine is the neurochemical identity of focus:** dopamine narrows the visual and auditory cone of attention; when it is low, the default mode network and task networks fire together (correlated) instead of alternating (anti-correlated), producing the inattention signature of ADHD. — *from [EP-313 - Essentials - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus](../Episodes/EP-313 - Essentials - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus/summary.md)*
- **The 2015 low-dopamine hypothesis explains ADHD self-medication:** insufficient dopamine in attention-regulating circuits causes spurious neural firing; people with ADHD chronically reach for sugar, coffee, nicotine, and stimulants because all of these raise dopamine — effectively self-medicating. — *from [EP-313 - Essentials - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus](../Episodes/EP-313 - Essentials - ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus/summary.md)*
