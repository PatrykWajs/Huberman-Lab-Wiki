# Mental Health

<!-- topic-intro-added -->
> **Overview**
>
> Mental health encompasses an individual's emotional, psychological, and cognitive well-being, intricately linked to various biological systems within the body. The boundary separating mental and physical health is largely fabricated, as both domains are interconnected through numerous shared pathways that involve the nervous, endocrine, and immune systems. The functioning of the brain—driven by networks of neurons, neurotransmitters, and stress hormones—affects not only thoughts and emotions but also physiological indicators such as heart rate, digestion, and immune function. Key structures within the limbic system, including the amygdala, hippocampus, and prefrontal cortex, play critical roles in processing emotions and managing stress responses.
> 
> This intricate relationship suggests that mental states can influence physical health, and vice versa. By understanding this mind-body connection, individuals can implement strategies that positively affect their mental health by also addressing their physical well-being. Integrating practices such as breathwork, intentional exposure to heat or cold, and regular exercise into daily life can lead to significant improvements in mental health and overall wellness. Tracking these insights is essential for applying them effectively in everyday situations to enhance quality of life.
