- **Dopamine drives pursuit, not pleasure** — the same molecule governs motivation and movement; it fires at baseline ~3–4 Hz and spikes to ~30–40 Hz in anticipation. - **The reward pathway: accelerator and brake** — the VTA projects dopamine to the nucleus accumbens (accelerator); the prefrontal cortex acts as a brake on impulsivity. Without the prefrontal brake you would be a pure pleasure-seeking animal. — *from [EP-12 - How to Increase Motivation and Drive](../Episodes/EP-012 - How to Increase Motivation and Drive/summary.md)*
- **The dopamine staircase** — food raises dopamine ~50% above baseline; sex ~100%; nicotine ~150%; cocaine and amphetamine ~1000-fold within ~10 seconds. Video games can spike dopamine between nicotine and cocaine levels. — *from [EP-12 - How to Increase Motivation and Drive](../Episodes/EP-012 - How to Increase Motivation and Drive/summary.md)*

- **Dopamine drives motivation and pursuit, not just pleasure.** It governs wanting, craving, and seeking far more than the pleasure of receiving. Baseline (tonic) dopamine determines your general drive; peaks (phasic) ride above it. — *from [EP-39 - Controlling Your Dopamine For Motivation, Focus and Satisfaction](../Episodes/EP-039 - Controlling Your Dopamine For Motivation, Focus and Satisfaction/summary.md)*
- **Every dopamine peak drops the baseline below where it started.** The post-reward crash — including "postpartum depression" after a big win — is a direct neurobiological consequence of peak-driven depletion of the readily releasable vesicle pool. — *from [EP-39 - Controlling Your Dopamine For Motivation, Focus and Satisfaction](../Episodes/EP-039 - Controlling Your Dopamine For Motivation, Focus and Satisfaction/summary.md)*
- **How satisfying something is depends on peak height relative to baseline, not peak height alone.** Raising both the baseline and the peak simultaneously via stacking does not produce more pleasure — it compresses the dynamic range. — *from [EP-39 - Controlling Your Dopamine For Motivation, Focus and Satisfaction](../Episodes/EP-039 - Controlling Your Dopamine For Motivation, Focus and Satisfaction/summary.md)*

- **Pro-social and defensive circuits are antagonistic.** The brain has a seesaw — pro-social circuits (approach, affiliation) suppress defensive circuits (fear, freezing). - **Gratitude circuits are serotonergic.** Serotonin from the raphe nucleus activates the anterior cingulate cortex and medial prefrontal cortex — the two key brain regions of the gratitude network.
