# NSDR, Meditation and Breathwork

<!-- topic-intro-added -->
> **Overview**
>
> The interplay between non-sleep deep rest (NSDR), meditation, and breathwork is crucial for harnessing specific physiological responses that yield various advantages for mental and physical well-being. Practices such as NSDR and yoga nidra, often referred to as yogic sleep, facilitate a profound state of relaxation while keeping an individual awake. These techniques can serve as effective substitutes for occasional sleep deprivation, help restore dopamine levels, lower cortisol levels, and enhance cognitive functions such as learning and memory.
> 
> Meditation and breathwork also play significant roles in achieving relaxation, with certain methods—like focused meditation and cyclic hyperventilation—designed to stimulate the nervous system, thereby boosting alertness and concentration. Although each practice possesses distinct characteristics, they can be combined strategically to maximize their contributions to overall health. Understanding and tracking the effects of these techniques is essential for integrating them into daily life effectively.
