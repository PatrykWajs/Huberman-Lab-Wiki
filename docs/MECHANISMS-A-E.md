# Mechanisms A–E

## Acetylcholine

Neurotransmitter involved in focus, attention, and muscle activation. Released during learning and novelty. High acetylcholine = high alertness and neuroplasticity window.

## Adrenaline (Epinephrine)

Stress hormone released by the adrenal glands. Mobilizes energy, increases heart rate, suppresses digestion. Short bursts enhance memory encoding and alertness.

## BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor)

"Fertilizer for the brain." Promotes growth of new neurons and synaptic connections. Increased by: exercise, cold exposure, sleep, fasting. Decreased by: chronic stress, alcohol.

## Cortisol

Primary stress hormone. High in the morning (healthy) — provides wakefulness. Chronically elevated = immunosuppression, fat storage, sleep disruption, hippocampal damage.

## Dopamine

Motivation, anticipation, reward. Not primarily "pleasure" — it's the drive to seek. Released during pursuit of goals. Dopamine system links effort → reward expectation.

## Endorphins

Released during intense exercise, laughter, and social bonding. Reduce pain perception and create euphoria. Distinct from endocannabinoids (also released by exercise).

## Estrogen

Primary female sex hormone. Regulates mood, bone density, cardiovascular health, and reproductive cycles. Neuroprotective — affects cognitive function across the lifespan.
