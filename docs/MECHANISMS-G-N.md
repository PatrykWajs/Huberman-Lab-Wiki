# Mechanisms G–N

## GABA (Gamma-Aminobutyric Acid)

Primary inhibitory neurotransmitter. Calms neural activity, promotes relaxation and sleep. Benzodiazepines work by enhancing GABA. Alcohol mimics GABA — causing its suppressive effects.

## Glutamate

Primary excitatory neurotransmitter. Drives learning and memory formation (LTP). Too much = excitotoxicity and neuron death (relevant in traumatic brain injury, stroke).

## HGH (Human Growth Hormone)

Released in pulses during deep sleep and high-intensity exercise. Promotes tissue repair, fat oxidation, and muscle protein synthesis. Blunted by poor sleep and chronic stress.

## IGF-1 (Insulin-Like Growth Factor 1)

Downstream of HGH. Promotes muscle growth and cellular proliferation. Elevated by protein consumption and exercise. Associated with longevity pathways in complex ways.

## Melatonin

Circadian rhythm signal — not a sleep inducer per se. Released when light decreases (especially blue light after sunset). Tells the brain "it's night." Supplementation effective for jet lag at low doses (0.1–0.5mg).

## mTOR (Mechanistic Target of Rapamycin)

Cellular growth and protein synthesis pathway. Activated by: resistance training, protein (especially leucine), growth factors. mTOR inhibition (caloric restriction, rapamycin) linked to longevity extension in animal models.

## Norepinephrine (Noradrenaline)

Closely related to adrenaline. Increases alertness, focus, and vigilance. Involved in the fight-or-flight response. Also released by cold exposure — key mechanism behind cold shower benefits.
